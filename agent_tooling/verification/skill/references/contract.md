# Claim Ledger Contract

The gate validates `schema_version` `1.0`, ledger `mode`, a ledger-level `artifact_revision`, and every claim's matching revision. `start` must run at the root of a Git worktree with a concrete `HEAD`. It captures `git:<HEAD>:sha256:<digest>`, where the digest covers the index plus tracked and nonignored untracked worktree content without following symlinks outside the working directory; `.git` and `.verification` are excluded. The same revision is stored in the ledger and activation marker. Sentinel revisions such as `UNSPECIFIED`, `UNKNOWN`, `TBD`, or an empty value are invalid in every state. The active Stop hook and `validate --cwd .` fail closed if the marker, ledger, or current artifact revision differs, or if the working directory is not a Git worktree. A recursive Stop-hook invocation passes without removing the activation marker so a user interruption is not trapped.

Preserve the fields required by [`../schemas/claim-ledger.schema.json`](../schemas/claim-ledger.schema.json); do not invent fields. A `CANDIDATE` is a queued, unassessed claim and may omit `criteria` and `views` rather than fabricating an assessment. Every other state requires at least one named criterion with a numeric score from 0 to 20 and auditable views. Every aggregate criterion score must equal the mean of the per-view scores for that named criterion.

Use `CANDIDATE` while a claim is queued, `LLM_SUPPORTED` for analysis that lacks closure evidence, `EVIDENCE_VERIFIED` for a closed supporting record, `REFUTED` for a closed contradicting record, and `INCONCLUSIVE` when an open obligation remains. `triage` mode may retain `CANDIDATE` and `LLM_SUPPORTED`; `closure` mode rejects both as nonterminal. In closure mode, missing eligible evidence or unresolved disagreement requires `INCONCLUSIVE`. `INCONCLUSIVE` requires at least one nonempty obligation. Closed claims cannot retain open obligations. Every evidence and counterevidence entry has a stable, claim-unique `id`. Any entry whose artifact revision differs from the ledger revision is stale and cannot close a claim. Stale entries may remain as audit history only for `CANDIDATE`, `LLM_SUPPORTED`, or `INCONCLUSIVE` when `evidence_invalidated` is true. Invalidated evidence cannot support either `EVIDENCE_VERIFIED` or `REFUTED`.

`llm_judgment` may inform triage but cannot by itself assign `EVIDENCE_VERIFIED`. Code and experiment claims require current `mechanical` or `reproduced_output` evidence for verification and refutation. Mathematics claims require current `derivation` or `formal_proof`; numerical evidence does not prove mathematical correctness. Evidence, research, source, and general claims require current `primary_source` or `reproduced_source` evidence to verify. The same `primary_source` or `reproduced_source` kinds are eligible for refutation counterevidence with `supports: false`.

Every `EVIDENCE_VERIFIED`, `REFUTED`, and `INCONCLUSIVE` claim records a structured `verifier-adjudicator` result. The record contains known `view_ids`, a state-consistent `result`, stable `evidence_ids`, and a nonempty `result_location`. Supporting and refuting closures link at least one evidence ID. A high or critical `EVIDENCE_VERIFIED` or `REFUTED` claim also records structured `verifier-skeptic` linkage to known views, evidence, and its result location. The skeptic seeks a decisive counterexample, stale input, missing assumption, or alternative explanation. The adjudicator checks that the ledger state follows from the recorded evidence and resolves neither disagreement nor missing evidence by majority vote.

The ledger contains at least one claim before validation. Every assessed claim has at least one aggregate criterion and every view has exactly those criterion names, with 0 to 20 scores whose mean reconstructs the aggregate. Populate `escalation_triggers` with each applicable item from `small_margin`, `high_dispersion`, `criterion_disagreement`, and `high_severity`; no numeric threshold is implied. Set `escalation_target` to exactly 2, 4, or 8 and record exactly that many unique views. With no trigger, no high or critical severity, and no unresolved disagreement, the target is 2. Any trigger or high or critical severity requires target 4 or 8; high and critical claims include `high_severity`. Unresolved disagreement includes `criterion_disagreement`, and an unresolved disagreement after four views requires target 8. High and critical claims also require skeptic and adjudicator roles.

The `views` object records a nonempty calibration kind, `unresolved_disagreement`, a comparison method and order, candidate IDs with a nonempty description for every candidate, and at least two unique view IDs. Each comparison match records distinct candidate IDs, a known `view_id`, an outcome, criterion-level scores matching the aggregate criterion names, and a `result_location`. Pairwise comparisons use the complete ordered grid across all declared candidate IDs. More than four candidates use `pivot_tournament` with unique candidate IDs, a nonempty proper pivot subset, and the complete pivot-by-nonpivot grid in both orientations. Run the rendered commands from `SKILL.md`: the installer replaces `{{VERIFICATION_GATE_COMMAND}}` with an absolute installed `verification_gate.py` command and `{{VERIFICATION_GATE_SHELL}}` with the destination shell label. `start` creates an initially invalid empty template and activation marker; `validate` checks the completed ledger and its live artifact binding. The final response must name the validated ledger so the Stop hook can permit completion.
