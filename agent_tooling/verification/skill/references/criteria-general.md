# General Adjudication Criteria

Score every claim from 0 to 20 for claim specificity, artifact identity, evidence relevance, evidence freshness, counterevidence handling, independent-view agreement, severity calibration, and closure readiness.

Begin with two independent views. Escalate to four or eight when the leading interpretations have small margins, scores have high dispersion, criteria conflict, or severity is high or critical. Reverse A/B order in pairwise comparisons to control order effects. With more than four candidates, use a balanced pivot tournament so every candidate has comparable opportunities to meet the pivot.

Do not accept a claim because a majority prefers it. If views disagree materially or required evidence is absent, record the disagreement and use `INCONCLUSIVE` with an open obligation. High and critical closures require skeptic and adjudicator participation even when early views agree.
