# Mathematics Criteria

Score each applicable criterion from 0 to 20: statement precision, definitions and domains, assumptions, derivation validity, theorem or lemma dependencies, limiting cases, counterexample search, and agreement between notation and conclusion.

The mathematics verifier reconstructs the derivation or checks a formal proof against the exact claim. The skeptic tries to violate an assumption, identify an invalid inference, produce a counterexample, or distinguish a numerical observation from a theorem. Record the proof artifact and the assumptions it uses.

Only a current `derivation` or `formal_proof` closes a mathematical correctness claim. Simulations, plots, finite tests, and numerical agreement can motivate a claim but never replace a derivation or formal proof. Missing proof or unresolved objection yields `INCONCLUSIVE` with the required proof or condition named as an open obligation.
