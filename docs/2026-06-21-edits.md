# Edits / decisions — 2026-06-21

## kl_max K-saturation of the hyper-prior block KL(s||r): synthesis decision document

Synthesis of four expert memos (skeptic, numerical analyst, theorist, implementation
engineer) on the observation that the hyper-prior block KL(s_i||r) is pinned at exactly
100 for the K=160 runs. All load-bearing file:line claims below were re-verified against
primary sources during this synthesis pass; the verifications are noted inline.

### 1. DIAGNOSIS (confirmed mechanism; corrected severity)

**Confirmed core mechanism.** At K=160 the diagonal KL(s_i||r) is an exact sum over K
coordinates (`vfe3/families/gaussian.py:100-104`, each term a `.sum(dim=-1)`), so it is
K-linear at a fixed per-coordinate rate. The numerical analyst verified the linearity
exactly: at 0.6534 nats/coord, KL = 13.07 / 104.55 / 209.10 for K = 20 / 160 / 320. The
clamp ceiling `kl_max=100.0` is K-INDEPENDENT (`vfe3/families/base.py:26`,
`kl.clamp(min=0.0, max=kl_max)` — re-read and confirmed). Saturation reduces to
`(per-coord rate) >= kl_max/K`; at the checkpoint's measured 0.791 nats/coord this pins the
median belief at K* = 100/0.791 = 126.4, so K=160 saturates (median KL 126.6, matching the
checkpoint) while K=20 (budget 5.0 nats/coord) never does.

The active config takes the closed-form filtering kernel route, not the autograd oracle
(`uses_kernel_route(...)=True` for filtering + gaussian_diagonal + renyi@1.0 + entropy +
flat + no rope, `vfe3/gradients/kernels.py:168-206`). In that kernel,
`self_mask = ((raw_self > 0.0) & (raw_self < kl_max))` (`kernels.py:128-132`, re-read and
confirmed) zeroes the self-coupling gradient wherever the raw self-divergence saturates. The
oracle is no escape: it differentiates THROUGH `safe_kl_clamp`, whose derivative is
identically 0 above `kl_max` (sympy-confirmed by the theorist:
`d/dD Min(D, k_max) = Piecewise((1, D<k_max), (0, True))`). For the ppl=76.53 run 100.0% of
vocab has KL(s||r) >= 100, so the hyper-prior self-direction is in the dead region for the
entire run.

The only live gradient route to `r` is also dead during saturation. With
`learnable_r=True, r_update_mode='gradient'`, the barycenter update is gated out
(`barycenter_r_` requires `r_update_mode=='barycenter'`), and `r` appears nowhere in the
gamma pair coupling. The skeptic's probe confirmed `d(grad_mu)/d(r_mu) = 0.0` when saturated
(vs 8.4 unsaturated). So during the pinned phase `r` is gradient-frozen and the escape seen
in the 2-epoch run is driven by weight_decay (shrinking r_mu/s_mu toward 0) + CE + gamma
coupling, NOT by the hyper-prior term. This part of the established-context diagnosis is
UPHELD.

**Skeptic's correction — PARTIALLY REFUTED the word "inert," and the correction is upheld.**
The kernel masks ONLY the self term. `grad_mu = self_mu + lambda_beta * pair_mu`
(`kernels.py:136`, re-read and confirmed); `self_mu` carries `self_mask` (`kernels.py:134`)
but `pair_mu` does not. In `_refine_s` the gamma model-coupling rides that pair term via
`lambda_beta=cfg.lambda_gamma` (`model.py:587`, re-read and confirmed). The skeptic's probe
with a saturated self-term but unsaturated gamma pair gave `||grad_mu||=16.2` (larger than
the unsaturated baseline 1.3), so `s` keeps training through the gamma path while KL(s||r) is
pinned. Only the consensus-pull-to-`r` sub-channel is dead. The claim "the hyper-prior
channel is inert" is therefore wrong; the correct statement is "the hyper-prior is a silently
disabled regularizer at K=160."

**Severity: a real but contained training pathology — a silent-inert KNOB, not broken
training.** Under `s_e_step=True` the hyper-prior term is gated OUT of the scored M-step loss
(`model.py:890`, `if self.cfg.lambda_h > 0.0 and not self.cfg.s_e_step:` — re-read and
confirmed; the comment at model.py:884-889 documents this is deliberate EM, not an omission).
It acts ONLY as the s E-step descent direction. A dead self-direction is therefore a
disabled regularizer, not a corruption of the trained logits — the configured `lambda_h`
regularizer does nothing for saturated tokens (effectively `lambda_h = 0` for 100% of vocab
at K=160), while the model still trains via CE + gamma. This is genuinely a defect (the
configured consensus regularizer and the learnable `r` are both silently off at the operating
width), and it is independent of remedy: sitting AT a NaN/inf safety-net clamp for 55k/60k
steps is a misuse of that clamp regardless. But it is "inert regularizer / frozen r," not
"inert channel" and not "training-corrupting." Net rating: MEDIUM-severity real pathology
(downgraded from the established context's "inert," consistent with the skeptic's verdict and
the theorist's "high" on the clamp-as-operating-ceiling misuse).

**Strongest evidence triple:** `kernels.py:128-136` (self_mask zeroes self_mu; pair_mu stays
live) + `base.py:26` (K-independent hard clamp, d/dkl = 0 above ceiling) + checkpoint
(100.0% of V=50257 at KL>=100, median 126.6 at 0.791 nats/coord vs the K=20 budget of 5
nats/coord) + figures `model_channel_terms.png` (KL(s||r) pinned at 100 across 5k-60k steps)
and `free_energy_decomposition.png` (hyper-prior contribution = 25.0 = 0.25 * 100).

### 2. SCOPE (K-scaling; what is the bigger lurking risk)

The pathology is monotone in K: the full-K self-divergences (KL(q||p) and KL(s||r)) are the
FIRST saturators, pinning at K* ~ 126 (per-coord rate ~0.79). This is the immediate failure.

The established context mis-sized the belief/gamma energy blocks as "K-linear over full K." It
is wrong about the accumulation dimension. Verified at `free_energy.py:139-182`
(`pairwise_energy`): with `irrep_dims=[d_head]*H` (block_glk, H=4) the energy is computed PER
IRREP BLOCK over `d_head = K/H = 40` coordinates, returning a leading head axis. The
s-channel `_refine_s` passes `grp.irrep_dims` (`model.py:564`), so the gamma model-coupling
energy is ALSO per-block d_head=40, not full K. The "26.7 / 35.2 at K=160" raw values are
per-d_head=40 sums (rates 0.667 / 0.880 nats/coord-block). Block saturation needs
`d_head * rate >= 100`:

- belief pair block saturates at d_head* = 150 -> K* = 600 (at H=4)
- gamma block saturates at d_head* = 114 -> K* = 455 (at H=4)

Both are FAR above the K* ~ 126 where the hyper-prior self-KL already pins. The pair/gamma
energies are NOT the bigger lurking risk at the K where the hyper-prior dies; they become a
risk only at K ~ 450-600, and only if H stays at 4 (raising H with K holds d_head fixed and
they never saturate). The genuinely damaging energy-mask case is UNIFORM row saturation (all
keys far -> uniform beta + all-zero `pair_mask`, `kernels.py:288`), which kills a head's
coupling gradient entirely; this also requires the whole row over d_head to saturate, so it
lags the hyper-prior pathology to K ~ 450-600. It is a real second-order risk, not the
immediate one.

Conclusion: size `kl_max` against the full-K self-divergences (the first saturators). Doing so
also clears the per-block energies with large margin at the same time.

### 3. REMEDY

**Recommended: Remedy A — make `kl_max` scale with K (a numerical safety-net, not an operating
ceiling).** Set it well above the routine operating KL so it binds ONLY on genuine
NaN/inf/Cholesky blowups (the role `safe_kl_clamp` documents: `nan_to_num(nan=kl_max,
posinf=kl_max)`, `base.py:25-27`).

Theory justification (theorist, sympy-backed). `kl_max` enters F only through `safe_kl_clamp`,
which is the IDENTITY on `[0, kl_max)`. As long as no operating divergence reaches the ceiling,
`kl_max` does not appear in F, in `beta* = softmax(B - E/tau)`, in `gamma*`, or in any
gradient — F is `kl_max`-independent throughout its intended domain. Raising a non-binding
ceiling cannot move a stationary point; it only widens the live region. This is the only
candidate that is provably theory-neutral. The numerical analyst confirmed the attention side
is benign: `tau = kappa*sqrt(d_energy)` reads only `kappa` and the block size — `kl_max` never
enters tau (`free_energy.py:41-76`), and un-clamping a saturated key moves its logit `-E/tau`
MORE negative, sharpening (not flattening) the softmax toward truly-low-energy keys.

Recommended value: **`kl_max = c * K` with c ~ 3-4** (restores the ~3-5 nats/coord headroom
the K=20 default enjoyed). Concretely:

- K=160: kl_max ~ 506 (healthy median KL ~127, ~4x margin)
- K=256: kl_max ~ 810
- K=512: kl_max ~ 1620

Minimal diff surface. Apply as a RUN-CONFIG edit, not a dataclass-default change: set
`cfg.kl_max` in `train_vfe3.py`'s config dict. `kl_max` is a single global field
(`vfe3/config.py:40`) already threaded to every consumer, including the sole hyper-prior site
`_refine_s` -> `e_step` -> `belief_gradients` -> kernel mask (`model.py:588`, re-read and
confirmed `kl_max=cfg.kl_max`). No call-site edits needed.

Parity / test impact (implementation engineer, verified against the suite descriptions).
Applied as a run-config value it breaks ZERO tests: `tests/test_config.py:9`
(`assert cfg.kl_max == 100.0`) pins the DATACLASS DEFAULT, which is untouched;
`test_config.py:41-43` only requires `> 0`; the kernel saturation tests
(`test_gradients_kernels.py:77-119`) pass a literal saturating belief and pin kernel==oracle
EQUIVALENCE, not the numeric ceiling; the divergence-saturation suite passes `kl_max`
explicitly per call (100, 37, inf) and never reads cfg. VFE_2.0 byte-parity: A is
byte-identical on any run where the old `kl_max=100` never bound (all small-K runs), and
diverges ONLY where the old ceiling was wrongly binding (large-K) — i.e. exactly the bug being
fixed.

Pure-path confirmation. The pure path is preserved exactly: the hard clamp with
`kl_max -> inf` recovers exact F, and any finite non-binding `kl_max` is byte-identical to it
below the ceiling. The clamp returns to its declared `# numerics` role beside `eps`.

Disclosed consequence (not a blocker). `cfg.kl_max` is global with no per-block seam (grep for
`kl_max_h|kl_max_self|kl_max_pair` returns nothing), so the same raise also un-masks the
belief-channel self/pair masks (`kernels.py:128/288`) and widens the saturated-energy scale —
which is the GOAL: those terms are supposed to be live, and the energy scale they feed
`softmax(-E/tau)` is the canonical undistorted `E/tau`. If a future need arises to un-mask the
hyper-prior WITHOUT touching the belief channel, the surgical alternative is a single new
`kl_max_h: float` field threaded only into the `_refine_s` call (`model.py:588`, replace
`kl_max=cfg.kl_max` with `kl_max=cfg.kl_max_h`), leaving all other sites on `cfg.kl_max`. Not
recommended for now (adds a knob for a problem the global raise already solves correctly).

**Runner-up: none of B or C — both rejected (see below).** If a runner-up must be named it is
the `kl_max_h` per-channel field above (same family as A, more surgical, but adds config
surface), NOT B or C.

### 4. WHAT-NOT-TO-DO

- **`lambda_h_mode='state_dependent_per_coord'` — already refuted (memory obs 20711). Do not
  go there.** It re-linearizes K scaling (the per-coord coupling `c0_h/(b0_h+KL)` does not
  remove the K-sum saturation) and, per the model.py:582 note, under state_dependent
  `cfg.lambda_h` is ignored except as the channel-on gate.

- **Remedy B (1/K per-coordinate-mean divergence normalization) — REJECT as posed.** The
  theorist's sympy analysis shows it silently shifts the effective softmax temperature from the
  canonical `kappa*sqrt(K)` to `kappa*K^{3/2}` (flattening attention by a factor of K),
  because dividing the energy by K while holding `tau = kappa*sqrt(d_energy)` fixed gives the
  stationary point `softmax(-E/(tau*K))`. This contradicts the manuscript's explicit "the
  canonical form is dimensionless in K" with `tau = kappa*sqrt(K)` (PIFB.tex:1262, :1882;
  GL(K)_attention.tex:47). It also rescales every alpha/lambda_h self-coupling weight by 1/K
  (changing M-step balance) and breaks VFE_2.0 byte parity everywhere. It is salvageable only
  by simultaneously co-scaling `tau -> tau/K` (i.e. `kappa_eff = kappa/sqrt(K)`) and every
  lambda by K — at which point it collapses to "Remedy A plus a lambda rescale" with no added
  benefit and a parity break. Strictly dominated by A.

- **Remedy C (soft clamp) — REJECT.** A smooth saturating map `g(D) != D` below threshold
  alters the VALUE of KL(q||p), the pair energy E_ij, and the hyper/gamma energies across the
  entire upper range (not just the tail). Since `beta*`/`gamma*` are functions of those
  energies, it DEFORMS the attention distributions themselves and voids the `-tau log Z_i`
  envelope reduction (PIFB.tex:770-773, GL(K)_attention.tex:859). No toggle value of C recovers
  exact F (a hard clamp with `kl_max -> inf` does), so it breaks the "a theoretically pure path
  always exists" doctrine, and it breaks VFE_2.0 parity. It buys a nonzero gradient in the
  saturated region — but Remedy A delivers that same gradient (the true unclamped gradient) by
  simply not saturating.

## gauge_transport ablation toggle (A1 / EXP-2)

Added `gauge_transport: str = "on"` to `VFE3Config` (`vfe3/config.py`) plus its `train_vfe3.py`
config-dict knob — the gauge ON/OFF/frozen-random ablation from
`docs/hypotheses/2026-06-21-hypotheses.md`. It is a meta-toggle over the GL(K) gauge FRAME, distinct
from `transport_mode` (flat vs Regime-II). `__post_init__` coerces the frame fields up front:
`"on"` (default) is a no-op pure path; `"off"` forces `phi_scale=0.0, pos_phi='none', e_phi_lr=0.0,
m_phi_lr=0.0` so `Omega_ij = exp(0)exp(0) = I` exactly (and raises if `pos_rotation!='none'` or
`transport_mode!='flat'`, which would break Omega=I); `"frozen"` keeps the random frame (`phi_scale`)
but zeros `e_phi_lr`/`m_phi_lr` (raises on `phi_scale<=0`). Each non-`on` mode warns the coercion.
Verified: `phi_embed` is exactly zero under `off` (Omega=I); all three modes run a forward to finite
logits; param counts confirm the documented caveat (`off`=3585 drops the `pos_phi_free` table via
`pos_phi='none'`, while `frozen`=`on`=3841 is the equal-param control). Tests: 7 new in
`tests/test_config.py` (coercion + the rope/regime_ii/zero-scale/unknown guards); full `test_config.py`
56 passed, 0 failed.
