# VFE_3.0 Buildout Roadmap — 2026-06-01 (multi-expert, verified)

## Method

This roadmap synthesizes a two-stage multi-expert buildout investigation of the VFE_3.0 gauge-theoretic transformer. The investigation template specifies ten expert lenses. The first pass populated four — `diffgeo-retraction-numerics`, `variational-fep`, `training-systems`, and `performance` — and a second recovery pass added the `canon-purity` lens, which reads the live kernels (`divergence.py`, `alpha_i.py`, `free_energy.py`, `geometry/{phi_preconditioner,retraction,transport,groups}.py`, `inference/e_step.py`, `gradients/{kernels,oracle}.py`, `attention_prior.py`, `metrics.py`) directly against the three manuscripts and the clean-room spec, and so spans the gauge, families, f-divergence, modularity, decode, and manuscript-fidelity territory the first pass did not reach. Two further lens territories were then investigated by dedicated prose-output agents, the structured-output path having failed for them in both prior passes: `modularity-architecture` (the user's stated expandability priority) and `manuscript-vs-code`. Their findings are integrated below and consolidated in a recovery-pass addendum at the end. The remaining three territories (info-geometry families, gauge transport, transformer attention and decode) were covered by the `canon-purity` lens together with direct synthesizer verification at the source, reported with their own file:line citations; they did not receive a dedicated independent agent, which is the one residual coverage limit of this investigation and is stated here rather than masked. Each finding carries a structured claim plus an independent verifier verdict; only items verified CONFIRMED_SOURCE, CONFIRMED_ABSENCE, or CONFIRMED_MANUSCRIPT are retained, together with confirmed-but-known buildout targets the synthesis brief mandates including. Findings verified REFUTED, flagged `is_novel=false` against an already-listed entry, or duplicating the authoritative perf doc (`docs/perf/2026-05-31-speedup-opportunities.md`) are merged or dropped and listed for traceability at the end. Where a verifier corrected a finding's framing, severity, or magnitude, the corrected version is propagated, because this document is written to `docs/` verbatim.

## Executive summary

The highest-leverage buildout items, ranked by impact over effort:

1. The divergence-layer family seam (`families/base.py` `ExponentialFamily` with natural-parameter, `log_partition`, `entropy`, and Bregman/Renyi-from-A maps), buildable now with Gaussian as the one concrete implementation; gauge action on non-Gaussian families stays Phase-5-deferred. Corroborated by the `canon-purity` lens. The `modularity-architecture` lens sharpens the seam's interface: its true depth is a family-declared covariance-kind tag plus a parameter-object divergence signature, not `A(theta)` alone (addendum findings M1, M2, M4).
2. The hyper-prior `lambda_h KL(s||r)` and model-coupling `gamma KL(s_i||Omega s_j)` channel — the full participatory two-tier free energy and the gate on the entire Regime-II meta-bundle hierarchy — built as a second `BeliefState` that reuses the existing `pairwise_energy`/`reduced_free_energy` machinery. Corroborated by the `canon-purity` lens against `eq:pointwise_free_energy`.
3. A first-class straight-through E-step training toggle matching manuscript Algorithm 1 line 2050, since neither current toggle realizes the prescribed detached-snapshot-plus-live-additive-chain estimator.
4. A `register_retraction`/`get_retraction` registry over the SPD retraction, the one geometry seam the spec names that is still dispatched by a hardcoded tensor-rank branch, blocking log-Euclidean and Bures-Wasserstein.
5. The opt-in self-coupling M-step regularizer `alpha*KL(q_i*||p_i)` from Algorithm 1 line 2083, presently absent from every decode path.
6. The autoregressive generation and sampling path — a major language-model capability the codebase does not implement at all; the decode produces single-step logits but no `generate`/`sample` loop exists.
7. A `register_transport`/`get_transport` registry plus the Regime-II edge-relaxed connection builder, the sole geometry axis the spec promises as registry-backed that has no registry, and the transport that the already-shipped holonomy diagnostic exists to exercise.
8. Checkpoint resume (the save side exists and is write-only) and gradient accumulation, both load-bearing for multi-hour 5090 runs.
9. A chunked-vocab decode kernel registered behind `register_decode`, removing the full `(B,N,V)` logit materialization the perf doc flags as the re-emerging top GPU cost.
10. A causal-packed transport path that skips the `N(N-1)/2` zero-beta upper-triangle pairs the default causal mask discards, an N-factor saving additive to the already-open dense-Omega K-factor fix.

## Tier B — Missing features and modularity/expandability gaps

**Divergence-layer family seam (buildable now, Gaussian as the one concrete impl).** The spec names `families/` as a swap point and specifies `families/base.py` with an `ExponentialFamily` interface (natural-to-moment maps, `log_partition(theta)`, `entropy(theta)`), with `divergence.py` dispatching to it through the Bregman/Renyi-from-A identity while retaining the Gaussian moment kernels as the pinned fast path. The folder does not exist (`Glob vfe3/families/**` returns nothing), and `divergence.py` writes diagonal Gaussian KL and Renyi directly in moment coordinates with no `log_partition`, natural-parameter, or `entropy(theta)` abstraction. Corroborated by three lenses now: `variational-fep` (F3, CONFIRMED_ABSENCE), `diffgeo-retraction-numerics` (F8, tying the covariance-structure tag to this layer), and `canon-purity` (CONFIRMED_ABSENCE), which adds that the `sigma_blend = (1-alpha)*sigma_q + alpha*sigma_t` clamp at `divergence.py:123` is exactly the natural-parameter-domain boundary the generic `A(theta)` form would expose, hardcoded to the Gaussian rather than derived from a log-partition. The `log_partition` symbol at `free_energy.py:227` is the attention softmax normalizer `-tau log Z`, not the exponential-family `A(theta)`, and does not satisfy this seam. This is a value-layer change only; the gauge core stays Gaussian-walled per spec sec 3, so it is buildable without touching `geometry/`. Effort L. Modularity impact: this is the seam the user names first; a new family registers `A(theta)` once and KL, Renyi, and entropy all follow, and it is the natural home for the observation-likelihood family and the model channel's family. *Manuscript/spec ref: spec sec 4.1 lines 46-50, sec 4 line 44, line 34 (generic `R_alpha = 1/(alpha-1)[A(alpha*theta1+(1-alpha)theta2) - alpha*A(theta1) - (1-alpha)*A(theta2)]`).* Known buildout target (`is_novel=false`), retained because the brief mandates the split.

**f-divergence functional seam beyond Renyi (single-entry registry, functional-signature lock-in).** Distinct from the family-covariance seam above. The functional axis IS a real registry — `_FUNCTIONALS`/`register_functional` (`divergence.py:297,300`) — but it carries exactly one member: `_VALID_DIVERGENCE_FUNCTIONALS = ("renyi",)` (`config.py:13`), and every registered functional still bottoms out in the Gaussian-special `_gaussian_*_renyi` kernels. A second f-divergence (forward/reverse KL as a non-limiting member, Hellinger, Jensen-Shannon, general f) cannot be added without either fitting the `renyi(...)` signature (alpha-parameterized) or editing the energy call sites that assume an alpha argument. Verified by synthesizer grep. Effort M. Modularity impact: the f-divergence axis is advertised as modular per CLAUDE.md ("slot in different f-divergences"); today it is a one-member registry whose member signature is the de facto interface. Building it out depends on the family seam, since each new f-divergence's closed form is family-specific until `A(theta)` exists. *Spec ref: sec 3 line 34, sec 4.1.* This is a modularity gap surfaced by synthesizer verification, not a manuscript fidelity violation; flag it as expandability, not impurity.

**Gauge action on non-Gaussian families (Phase-5-deferred per spec).** The companion of the family seam, deliberately held back. Spec sec 4.2 line 56 makes a `(family, group)` pair valid only when the family's divergence is invariant under common pushforward by the group's representation; for non-Gaussian families the admissible group is family-specific and is exactly what the Phase-5 investigation decides (spec line 131, restated line 141: the layer may stay Gaussian-only and the exp-family abstraction confined to the divergence layer, which the design assumes as default). No build action now; this entry enforces the split between the now-buildable divergence-layer seam and the deferred geometry-layer question. *Spec ref: sec 4.2 line 56, Phase 5 line 131, sec 9 line 141.*

**Mixture-family caveat (separate, larger item).** Mixtures are not exponential families: a mixture has no single natural-parameter vector and its KL and Renyi divergences have no closed form (the log of a sum of exponentials does not separate). The `ExponentialFamily` interface does not extend to mixtures; supporting them needs a distinct abstraction with approximate or Monte-Carlo divergences, and it breaks the closed-form pinning the golden tests rely on. Flagged as a separate, larger item, not part of the divergence-layer family seam. No lens produced a manuscript reference for mixtures specifically; treat this as a design caveat surfaced during synthesis, not a verified manuscript-backed target.

**Group-divergence admissibility is declared by a string list, not checked executably.** `GaugeGroup.invariant_for(family)` (`groups.py:48-50`) returns `family in self.invariant_families` — a pure string-membership test — and every `register_group` builder hardcodes the Gaussian admissibility by the default `invariant_families=('gaussian',)` (`groups.py:38`). There is no executable verifier that samples `g in G`, pushes a belief forward by the group's representation, and asserts `D(rho(g)q||rho(g)p) = D(q||p)`. For the only shipped (Gaussian, GL(K)-subgroup) pairs this is mathematically true (`GL(K)_attention.tex` Theorem `glk_invariance`), so nothing breaks today; but a wrongly-declared `invariant_families` string for a future non-Gaussian family or non-congruence representation would silently pass and produce a gauge-non-invariant energy with no test catching it. CONFIRMED_SOURCE (`canon-purity` lens). Interface change: add a property-test helper `check_admissible(group, family, n_samples)` that draws `g = exp(random phi.G)`, applies the family's representation pushforward to a random belief pair, and asserts the registered divergence is invariant to tolerance; optionally have `GaugeGroup` expose the representation map so the check is generic rather than Gaussian-special. Effort M. Modularity impact: turns the family-to-group bridge from a maintained convention into a verified invariant, the precondition for safely adding other gauge groups or (Phase 5) non-Gaussian families behind the same seam. *Spec ref: sec 4.2 line 56 (admissibility criterion as a bridge; the spec does not mandate an executable check, so this is forward-looking hardening rather than a spec omission).*

**Cross-coupled GL(K) basis is implemented but unreachable from any config.** Off-block-diagonal generators — the only route to a genuinely cross-coupled (single-block) GL(K) gauge — are built by `generate_glk_cross_head(K, n_heads, cross_couplings)` (`generators.py:157-193`) and accepted by `_build_block_glk(..., cross_couplings=...)` (`groups.py:95,108-109`). But `config.py` has no `cross_couplings` field (grep returns no match), `_VALID_GAUGE_GROUPS = ("glk","block_glk","tied_block_glk","so_k")` validates only the group name (`config.py:14`), and `build_group` (`model/model.py:36-50`) dispatches purely on the builder's positional arity (arity 1 -> `builder(K)`, arity 2 -> `builder(K, n_heads)`), so it can never forward the `cross_couplings` keyword. The feature exists in the geometry layer and is dead from the entry point. Verified by synthesizer grep. Interface change: add `cfg.cross_couplings: Optional[List[Tuple[int,int]]]` and thread it through `build_group` (either widen the arity dispatcher to pass known config kwargs, or have the `block_glk` builder read it from a passed config), preserving the default `None` (pure block-diagonal) path. Effort S. Modularity impact: makes the cross-head coupling the geometry layer already supports selectable without editing `build_group`; the arity-based dispatcher is itself the call-site coupling the spec's register-and-get pattern is meant to remove. *Manuscript/spec ref: spec sec 4.2 (structure-group axis as a config-selected seam); `GL(K)_attention.tex` cross-block generators.*

**Additional gauge groups (U(K), SU(K), Sp(K)) — registry-ready, members absent.** `_GROUPS` carries exactly four builders — `glk`, `block_glk`, `tied_block_glk`, `so_k` (`groups.py:73,86,124,154`) — with no unitary, special-unitary, or symplectic structure group. The group axis is a genuine registry (`register_group`/`get_group`, `groups.py:53-70`), so each is a write-and-register addition, but none is built and `_VALID_GAUGE_GROUPS` does not list them. Verified by synthesizer grep. Effort M each (a generator basis plus the `register_group` builder; SU/U need the anti-Hermitian basis and a unitary `exp`, Sp the symplectic-form constraint). Modularity impact: low-coupling, and the admissibility verifier above is the precondition for trusting a new group's `(family, group)` validity. *Spec ref: sec 4.2 line 56 (structure-group axis).* Forward-looking expandability; severity low.

**Hyper-prior `lambda_h KL(s||r)` and model-coupling `gamma KL(s_i||Omega s_j)` channel (two-tier free energy; Regime-II hierarchy gate).** Both terms appear only as docstring extension points (`free_energy.py:244-254`); `free_energy()` (`free_energy.py:230-273`) assembles only the belief tier `alpha*D(q||p) + sum beta E + entropy - loglik` and has no `lambda_h`/`gamma` arguments; `BeliefState` (`belief.py:8-13`) carries exactly `(mu, sigma, phi)` with no second model-channel distribution `s_i` and no hyper-prior `r_i`; `config.py` has no `lambda_h`/`gamma` field. Confirmed by two lenses: `variational-fep` (F1, CONFIRMED_SOURCE) and `canon-purity` (CONFIRMED_MANUSCRIPT), the latter pinning the two-tier canonical F to `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:1241-1249` (`eq:pointwise_free_energy`, carrying both the `lambda_h sum_i KL(s_i||r_i)` hyper-prior and the `sum_ij gamma_ij KL(s_i||Omega_ij s_j)` model-coupling plus meta-entropy) and the `h -> s -> p -> q` hierarchy with model field `s_i` at `Participatory:192,514`. The entire Regime-II meta-bundle hierarchy (meta-agents, hyper-prior propagation across scales, holonomy of the meta-bundle) is gated on this model channel existing. Interface change: add `s_i`, `r_i` to the belief container and `PriorBank`; add config fields `lambda_h`, `gamma` with gamma's own attention prior and temperature; add two terms to `free_energy()` reusing `self_divergence` for `KL(s||r)` and the existing `pairwise_energy`/`reduced_free_energy` machinery for the gamma model-coupling block, which is structurally the same softmax-over-KL object as belief coupling; extend `e_step_iteration` with a slow-channel s-update. Effort XL. Modularity impact: building it as a second `BeliefState` that reuses the energy and attention path keeps the divergence, family, and group seams shared across both channels; a bolt-on would fork the seams. The manuscript sets `lambda_h=0` in the simulation regime (`Participatory:1279`), so frame this as a buildout target, not a fidelity bug. Known/expected (`is_novel=false`), retained because the brief places it near the top of the punch list and two lenses support it.

**Regime-II (non-flat / edge-relaxed) transport and the missing transport registry (distinct from the hyper-prior channel above).** The spec designs the connection regime as a registry-backed modular axis on equal footing with the structure group (spec sec 4.2 line 54: "two orthogonal, registry-backed modular axes: the structure group and the connection regime ... config-selected so new variants are added by writing-and-registering, never by editing call sites"), and names the exact Regime-II builder (line 60: "Regime II is the edge-relaxed cocycle `Omega_ij = exp(phi_i) exp(delta_ij . G) exp(-phi_j)` ... The regime is a toggle"). Neither exists. `transport.py` exposes only two FLAT (Regime I) cocycle builders — `compute_transport_operators` (`transport.py:114-153`, `Omega_ij = exp(phi_i)@exp(-phi_j)`) and `compute_transport_operators_direct` (`transport.py:156-196`, still the flat cocycle `Omega_i Omega_j^{-1}`) — and the E-step call sites import `compute_transport_operators` directly (`inference/e_step.py:38-39,50-51`), with no `register_transport`/`get_transport`. The structure-group axis IS a genuine registry (`_GROUPS`, `groups.py:53`), but there is no analogous `_TRANSPORTS` registry, and config offers only `gauge_parameterization in {phi, omega_direct}` (a parameterization axis, `omega_direct` rejected at `config.py:157`), never a connection-regime toggle (grep for `register_transport`/`connection_regime`/`edge_relaxed`/`non_flat`/`delta_ij` returns nothing). The holonomy diagnostic `holonomy_deviation` (`metrics.py:50-60`) is a curvature proxy identically ~0 for the flat cocycle and only nonzero under the absent Regime-II transport — the diagnostic was built, the transport that exercises it was not. CONFIRMED_MANUSCRIPT (`canon-purity` lens). Distinct from the perf-pack dense-Omega item that shares line 152: this is the regime/registry gap, not the `(B,N,N,K,K)` memory concern; and distinct from the hyper-prior channel above, which gates the meta-bundle hierarchy this transport's holonomy would live in. Interface change: introduce `register_transport`/`get_transport` mirroring `register_group`, route `e_step` transport through it, add a `regime_ii` builder `Omega_ij = exp(phi_i) exp(delta_ij.G) exp(-phi_j)` with `delta_ij` a per-edge (or data-dependent `delta_ij^a = mu_i^T W^a mu_j`) Lie-algebra connection initialized at 0 (bitwise-flat == Regime I), plus the optional Wilson `holonomy_penalty` regularizer the diagnostic already supports. Keep the flat cocycle as the default registered entry so the pure path is preserved. Effort L. Modularity impact: restores the one major geometry axis missing its registry, and makes Yang-Mills/holonomy research (Regime II), the cocycle-relaxation homotopy, and RoPE-on-mu all writeable-and-registerable rather than requiring edits to the E-step call sites. *Manuscript/spec ref: spec sec 4.2 lines 54,60; `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:837-844` (`eq:edge_relaxed_omega`), :806 (two-regime statement), :876-880 (cocycle_relaxation homotopy).*

**Autoregressive generation and sampling path (major missing LM capability).** The model produces single-step decode logits (`prior_bank.decode`, `prior_bank.py:154`) and a training/evaluation forward, but there is no autoregressive generation loop, no sampler (greedy / top-k / top-p / temperature), and no KV-style incremental-state reuse: a grep over `vfe3/model` for `def generate`, `def sample`, `autoregressive`, or `@torch.no_grad` returns only `prior_bank.decode` and the diagnostics `@torch.no_grad` at `model.py:179` (a diagnostics method, not a generation loop). Verified by synthesizer grep. This is the one genuinely new high-impact capability rather than a modularity nicety: a language model that cannot generate is incomplete for any downstream LM use, qualitative inspection, or sampling-based evaluation. Interface change: add a `generate(prompt_ids, max_new_tokens, *, temperature, top_k, top_p, sampler)` method that runs the existing forward to converged beliefs, decodes the last position, samples, appends, and repeats; register the sampling strategy behind a small `_SAMPLERS` registry consistent with the project's register-and-get pattern; consider incremental belief reuse across steps as a later optimization (the E-step is iterative, so naive re-running the full stack per token is the correct-but-slow first version). Effort M for the correct-but-slow version; L with incremental-state reuse. Modularity impact: opens evaluation and inspection modes (free-running samples, holonomy-of-generated-text probes) that the diagnostics tier cannot reach today. *Manuscript ref: n/a for the sampling mechanics; the per-token predictive distribution is the decode `GL(K)_attention.tex` log-softmax pin already implemented.*

**RoPE-on-mu transport variant and the T5/windowed positional priors (absent).** `transport_mean` (`transport.py:199-209`) applies only the gauge action `mu -> Omega mu`, with no rotary action on `mu`; the attention-prior registry carries `uniform/causal/alibi` only (`attention_prior.py:35,49,67`), with no T5-style learnable relative bias and no windowed prior, and RoPE is a multiplicative transport effect, not an additive prior, so the prior seam cannot supply it. CONFIRMED_ABSENCE (`canon-purity` lens), and `transport.py:7` and the spec Phase-2 plan (line 125) mark RoPE as an explicitly deferred later-phase item, so this is a known-future absence rather than a regression. Interface change: once the transport registry exists, add a `rope_on_mu` transport entry (block-wise 2x2 rotation of `mu` by position-dependent angle, identity on sigma) as a composable transport variant; separately register the T5 relative-bias and windowed priors in `attention_prior.py` (both are <10-line registrations). Effort M. Modularity impact: completes the transport-variant axis the spec enumerates and lets positional inductive biases be selected by config across both the multiplicative (transport) and additive (prior) channels. *Spec ref: sec 4.2 line 60 ("RoPE-on-mu is a further transport variant"), Phase 2 line 125; `GL(K)_attention.tex:823-838` (relative-position / T5 priors as the additive complement).* Severity low.

**Learnable / fully-Bayesian alpha form (registry-ready, member absent).** The self-coupling alpha axis is a genuine registry — `register_alpha`/`get_alpha` with a `per_coord` declaration (`alpha_i.py:15-39`) — carrying `constant`, `state_dependent`, and `state_dependent_per_coord` (`alpha_i.py:60,72,87`); the latter two are the Gamma-MAP closed form `alpha* = c0/(b0+D)` the `canon-purity` lens verified correct. What is absent is a learnable alpha (a raw `nn.Parameter` per coordinate or per block, trained by backprop within the documented learned-parameter exception) and a fully-Bayesian alpha that propagates the Gamma posterior rather than collapsing to its MAP point. Verified by synthesizer grep. Effort S–M (a new `register_alpha` member plus config fields). Modularity impact: the seam is built and the routing (`alpha_is_per_coord`) already handles the per-coordinate case, so this is a clean add-by-register; it is the natural place a learned or hierarchical alpha lands without touching the M-step call site. *Manuscript ref: `GL(K)_attention.tex:939-964` (the Gamma-MAP reading whose Bayesian generalization this would be).* Forward-looking expandability.

**SPD retraction registry (`register_retraction`/`get_retraction`).** The retraction is the one geometry seam the spec names as registry-backed yet it is dispatched by a hardcoded `if belief.sigma.dim()==belief.mu.dim()+1` branch in `e_step.py:199`; `retract_spd_diagonal` (`retraction.py:22`) and `retract_spd_full` (`retraction.py:48`) are bare module functions, and a grep for a retraction registry finds only the `phi_retract_mode` compose seam inside `retract_phi`, never a seam over the SPD retraction itself. CONFIRMED_SOURCE (lens `diffgeo-retraction-numerics` F1). Interface change: add `register_retraction`/`get_retraction` mirroring `_DECODERS` and `_PRECOND`, register `spd_affine_diagonal` and `spd_affine_full`, add `cfg.spd_retract_mode`, and dispatch from `e_step` via the registry. Effort M. Modularity impact: this is where log-Euclidean (`Sigma_new = expm(logm(Sigma) + Delta_log)`) and Bures-Wasserstein register without touching the E-step call site, which the spec forbids; a future non-Gaussian family needing a different manifold retraction has nowhere to plug in today. *Spec ref: sec 4 line 44, sec 4.2.*

**SO(N) principal-ball / antipodal-flip wrapping and optional Newton-Schulz re-orthogonalization.** `retract_son` (`lie_ops.py:246`) calls `_retract_core` with `max_norm=math.pi`, and `_retract_core:201-203` does only a radial rescale, no modulo-2pi wrap and no antipodal flip near the exp-map degeneracy at `||phi||=pi`; a grep for `antipodal|principal_ball|Newton-Schulz|reorthogonal` over `vfe3/geometry` returns nothing. ## Tier C — Speedups beyond the perf doc

The dense `(B,N,N,K,K)` Omega K-factor cost is the already-open P0 #2 in the perf doc and is not re-listed here. The items below are additive to it.

**Causal-triangle waste (GPU-real; equivalence-exact).** The default `attention_prior=causal` mask fills `j>i` with `-inf` (`attention_prior.py:64`), driving those `beta_ij` to zero, yet `compute_transport_operators` (`transport.py:152`) materializes the full `(B,N,N,K,K)` Omega and `pairwise_energy` (`free_energy.py:68-88`) evaluates the full `(N,N)` energy before the softmax discards the upper triangle. CONFIRMED_SOURCE (lens `performance` F1); the N-factor this saves is independent of and additive to P0 #2's K-factor. The "roughly 2x" magnitude is a runtime estimate not readable from source; the verified fact is the structural redundancy. Fix: after P0 #2, compute `m_j` and the `exp_phi` einsum only over `j<=i`, packing into a `(B, N*(N+1)/2, K)` buffer, guarded on `attention_prior=='causal'` with all other priors falling back to the full path. Effort L. Equivalence: the softmax discards `j>i` exactly, so skipping their computation is exact; re-pin the VFE_2.0 golden. GPU-real, memory-bandwidth change only, no new formula.

**Chunked-vocab decode (GPU-real; equivalence-gated).** `_decode_diagonal` produces a full `(B,N,V)` logit tensor via `lhs @ rhs^T + per-V constant` (`prior_bank.py:277`, `V=50257`), materialized for both forward and backward; the perf doc flags this as the prime suspect to re-emerge as the top GPU cost but gives no concrete fix. CONFIRMED_SOURCE (lens `performance` F2); the new contribution is the remedy. The ~1.6 GB forward / ~3.2 GB with backward figures are arithmetic estimates, not source-readable. Fix: implement `decode_mode='diagonal_chunked'` via `register_decode`, iterating V in chunks, computing chunk logits from the same formula with the `c=mean_v(mu_v)` centering offset (`prior_bank.py:271`) preserved, and accumulating cross-entropy with `torch.logsumexp` plus a target-logit gather, so the backward sees only per-chunk intermediates. Effort L. Equivalence: re-pin the `atol-1e-3` diagonal golden (the existing kernel has catastrophic-cancellation sensitivity, perf doc lines 186-190) against the chunked version at the same offset; for the `use_prior_bank=False` linear path a fused-linear-CE suffices. Modularity impact: a new `_DECODERS` entry, write-and-register, no call-site edit.

**Mixed-precision (bf16/fp16 autocast) opt-in (GPU-real; equivalence-gated).** No code path enables autocast; a grep finds only four `torch.amp.autocast('cuda', enabled=False)` disable-islands protecting `matrix_exp` and SPD retraction (`transport.py:53`, `retraction.py:37,73,131`). CONFIRMED_ABSENCE (lens `training-systems` F5). The fp32 mandate is the pure path, and the hardest prerequisite (fp32 islands around the sensitive ops) is already in place. Fix: add `cfg.amp_dtype in {None,'bf16','fp16'}` wrapping the E-step/forward in autocast, keep the existing disable-islands, and add the decode matmul (`prior_bank.py:234-259`) and cross-entropy to the fp32-island set; bf16 needs no GradScaler, fp16 would. Effort M. Equivalence: distinct from and safer than the perf doc's rejected global TF32 (which threatened the decode's `atol-1e-3` pin); verify against the VFE_2.0 golden at relaxed tolerance and document as opt-in non-pure. *Manuscript ref: n/a — systems.*

**Gradient accumulation (long-N memory).** `train_step` (`train.py:114-121`) does one backward and one optimizer step per batch with no accumulation counter, and no DDP/DataParallel exists; dense transport memory is `O(B*N^2*K^2)`. CONFIRMED_ABSENCE (lens `training-systems` F6). Fix: add `cfg.grad_accum_steps`, divide loss by the count, step and zero-grad every `accum_steps`, scale grad-clip accordingly. Effort M. Multi-GPU (DDP) is a larger, separable item. *Manuscript ref: n/a — systems.*

**Per-head batched `pairwise_energy` (GPU-real; bit-identical for equal blocks).** `pairwise_energy` (`free_energy.py:70-88`) iterates `for d in irrep_dims`, calling the functional once per irrep block then `torch.stack`; for the default `block_glk` with `n_heads=8` equal blocks this is 8 sequential launches. CONFIRMED_SOURCE (lens `performance` F3); a distinct site from the perf doc's P1 #3, which targets `_blockwise_matrix_exp` in `transport.py`. The launch-count impact is not source-measurable; the structural redundancy is. Fix: when blocks are equal size and more than one, stack the H slices along a new leading head axis and call the functional once, falling back to the per-block loop for unequal sizes. Effort S. Equivalence: same arithmetic in a different order, bit-identical for equal blocks; guard-test against the loop output at `atol=0`.

**`diagnostics()` forward recomputation (GPU-real; low impact).** At a log step the training loop runs three forwards on the same tokens (training forward, a separate no-grad CE forward, and `diagnostics()` which re-runs `vfe_stack` and recomputes transport, energy, beta, and attention; `model.py:214,218-247`, `train.py:221-225`). CONFIRMED_SOURCE (lens `performance` F5). At `log_interval=50` (default) this is roughly 4 percent of steps, so the "significant fraction of wall time" framing is overstated and the docstring's off-hot-path claim is accurate in the amortized sense; severity low. Fix: add a `return_diagnostics=False` flag to `forward` returning the converged `phi`, `omega`, `beta`, `self_div`, `alpha` bundle, consumed at log steps only; the default path stays unchanged. Effort M.

Items the perf doc does not address and that no delivered lens covered: `torch.compile`/CUDA-graph capture and a dedicated long-N memory treatment beyond the chunked decode and gradient accumulation above. These remain open; the `canon-purity` recovery lens did not touch the compile/capture territory, so they stay flagged as uninvestigated rather than confirmed.

## Tier D — Numerical robustness and other

**Diagonal-family sandwich transport keeps only `diag(Omega Sigma Omega^T)` under a non-block-diagonal GL(K) Omega (acceptable default-toggle approximation; pure path exists).** The diagonal branch of `transport_covariance` (`transport.py:227-229`) computes `Sigma_t[i,j,k] = sum_l Omega_ijkl^2 sigma_jl`, the diagonal of the full sandwich, discarding the off-diagonals; the full branch (`transport.py:230`) keeps the true sandwich `Omega Sigma Omega^T` that the gauge-invariance theorem depends on (`GL(K)_attention.tex:1067,1070`). Numerically verified: for `Omega=[[0.8,-0.6],[0.6,0.9]]`, `sigma=(2,1)`, the true sandwich `O@diag(sigma)@O.T = [[1.64,0.42],[0.42,1.53]]` with off-diagonals 0.42, while the diagonal branch returns `(1.64,1.53)` and discards them. (The originating claim's numbers `[[0.92,1.5],[-1.5,0.09]]` were wrong and non-symmetric; the corrected sandwich above is propagated.) CONFIRMED_SOURCE (`canon-purity` lens), but deflated to Tier D at LOW severity: a pure path DOES exist (`family='gaussian_full'`, `transport.py:230`, keeps the full sandwich), and per CLAUDE.md audit policy a non-pure default toggle with an existing pure path is acceptable, not a missing pure path; the docstrings at `transport.py:6-7,219-225` already label it "the diagonal approximation," so the "nothing flags it" framing is undercut. Fix: document at the diagonal call boundary (and ideally warn at config) that diagonal covariance plus a non-block-diagonal group is a speed approximation pointing to `gaussian_full` for the pure path; optionally expose a "full-transport, diagonal-readout" middle path that forms `Omega Sigma Omega^T` then re-diagonalizes only at the divergence so the off-diagonal mass enters the trace. Effort S. Modularity impact: clarifies which `(family, group)` combinations realize the exact gauge theory versus an approximation, which directly informs the admissibility seam (Tier B) and any future elliptical/location-scale family sharing the diagonal shortcut. *Manuscript ref: `GL(K)_attention.tex:1067,1070`.*

**alpha>1 / full-covariance Cholesky hardening (deferred robustness, build the pure path).** `_gaussian_full_renyi` (`divergence.py:182-184`) forms `sigma_blend = (1-alpha)*sigma_q + alpha*sigma_t` and calls a bare `torch.linalg.cholesky` with no try/except and no escalation; for `alpha>1` the blend can be indefinite and the Cholesky raises or returns NaN, NaN-ing the whole batch. The diagonal kernel clamps the blend gracefully; the full kernel has no equivalent guard. `safe_spd_inverse` (`numerics.py:41-48`) already implements the exact 5-round escalation ladder this needs but is not called. CONFIRMED_SOURCE (lens `diffgeo-retraction-numerics` F3); `alpha>1` is opt-in and warned, severity medium. Fix: replace the bare Cholesky with the escalation ladder (a `safe_cholesky` helper), mask any still-failing pair to `kl_max` via `safe_kl_clamp` before the logdet, and add the eigenvalue-floor SPD projection as the pure `alpha>1` path. Effort M. *Manuscript/spec ref: spec sec 3 line 34, sec 6 line 111.*

**Centralized SPD numerics fallbacks are orphaned; live paths re-scatter ridges.** `safe_spd_inverse` and `floor_eigenvalues` (`numerics.py:25,51`) have no importer anywhere in `vfe3`, while live SPD paths each inline their own `eps*I` ridge or `eps` clamp (`divergence.py:165`, `retraction.py:80,100`, `norms.py:63`). CONFIRMED_SOURCE (lens `diffgeo-retraction-numerics` F2). This is the scattered anti-pattern the spec rebuilt V3 to remove. Fix: route the full-cov divergence Cholesky and the norms solve through `safe_spd_inverse` and `retract_spd_full`'s final projection through `floor_eigenvalues`, making `numerics.py` the genuine single home; or, if the inline forms are intentional, mark the functions as a public API and add a delegation test. Effort M. *Spec ref: sec 6 line 111.*

**kappa_max condition-number cap unenforced (deferred hardening, not a theory violation).** `condition_number` is computed (`numerics.py:64`) but used only as a passive `register_monitor` probe (`numerics.py:136-139`), never enforced as a clamp in the SPD retraction; the diagonal natural gradient floors at `eps=1e-6` (`retraction.py:136`). CONFIRMED_MANUSCRIPT (lens `diffgeo-retraction-numerics` F5). The `eps=1e-6`-versus-`epsilon_SPD=1e-4` sub-point is dropped because spec sec 6 authorizes V3 to reimplement numerics and the `sanitize_sigma` carrying those constants does not exist in V3, so the constant choice is authorized; the surviving point is the unenforced `kappa_max=1e4` cap. Placed in Tier D, not Tier A, because the verifier characterizes it as deferred hardening V3 chose not to port, not a theory contradiction. Severity low. Fix: surface `spd_floor` and `kappa_max` as cfg fields and enforce `kappa_max` inside `retract_spd_full`/`floor_eigenvalues`. Effort S. *Manuscript ref: `GL(K)_supplementary.tex:650-652`.*

**exp(M) Frobenius clamp can silently rescale the gauge operator (conditional, overflow guard).** `stable_matrix_exp_pair` uniformly rescales the whole Lie-algebra element when `||M||_F > 15` (`transport.py:43-45`), so `exp(M)` becomes the exponential of a shorter element. On the pure default path this never fires: `retract_glk` caps `||phi||` at `max_norm=5.0` (`lie_ops.py:216`), so the pair's exp argument tops out near `2*5=10 < 15`; it is an overflow guard, and distortion is conditional on a cross-coupled or larger basis. CONFIRMED_SOURCE (lens `diffgeo-retraction-numerics` F6), severity low. Fix: emit a `run_monitors` record when the clamp is active so the distortion is observable, document that `max_norm=15` must exceed twice the retract cap, and consider scaling-and-squaring as the pure large-norm path behind a transport-numerics toggle. Effort S. *Manuscript ref: `GL(K)_supplementary.tex:668`, `GL(K)_attention.tex:1643`.*

**Oracle detach on non-default families (known-open, cross-referenced).** The default hand kernel keeps the mu/sigma gradient live while every oracle-fallback family (smoothing, full-cov, `alpha!=1`, surrogate) detaches its delta (`oracle.py:77-78` versus `kernels.py:182`), so swapping a divergence family silently changes the M-step gradient semantics. This is the authoritative perf doc's correctness flag #1 and is therefore not a novel finding, but it is listed here because the brief names "oracle detach on non-default families" explicitly. It is resolved by the straight-through E-step toggle in Tier A, which decouples which divergence from how the M-step differentiates. *Ref: `docs/perf/2026-05-31-speedup-opportunities.md:195-201`.*

## Recovery-pass addendum — modularity-architecture and theory-fidelity deep lenses

### Modularity-architecture

A single thread connects the high-severity items: the codebase encodes "what kind of distribution is this" as a name substring or a hardcoded string literal rather than as data the family declares.
