# A roadmap for a real-time interaction model: persistent beliefs, an exogenous observation channel, action, and a gated expected-free-energy layer

## 1. The question, and what survives scrutiny

The user's conjecture is that the canonical free energy's observation term `-sum_i E_{q_i}[log p(o|k_i)]` is the natural seam through which this architecture becomes an interacting agent: every time step Delta-t the agent receives an observation vector, updates its beliefs, and acts, with action perhaps selected by an active-inference expected free energy (EFE) over policies. Nine expert memos and three adversarial critiques examined the conjecture. The verdict is that the conjecture is substantially right about perception and persistence, right-with-conditions about action, and premature-but-testable about the EFE. The architecture is closer to an interaction model than it appears: it is already a two-timescale optimizer (fast per-token belief settling in the E-step, slow PriorBank learning in the M-step), and what a real-time loop adds is exactly three things, in order of decreasing certainty — beliefs that persist across time steps, an observation channel that is genuinely exogenous, and an action mechanism whose epistemic component must be earned empirically rather than assumed.

The 2026-06-07 analysis established why the observation term is vacuous in the current next-token language model: the present token already sets the prior (encode returns q = p from the PriorBank, prior_bank.py:192-197), the next token already drives the cross-entropy booked as the data term in metrics.py, and the E-step is target-blind by signature. Every threadable observation was leakage, double-counting, or a reparametrization of alpha. The real-time interaction model is precisely the escape from that trilemma: a per-Delta-t stream supplies the exogenous channel the term has been waiting for, and the temporal carry of beliefs supplies the top-down prior that frees the present input to be bottom-up data — the shadow-prior split (PIFB:1233) with the agent's own past playing the role of the coarser scale. The roadmap below is ordered so that each phase can falsify the direction before the next is paid for.

A reading consolidated across all nine memos deserves stating up front because it disciplines everything that follows. Three failure modes from the 06-07 analysis recur at every new seam: leakage (anything that lets the settling E-step see targets or preferences), double-counting (anything that books the same information twice in F or its diagnostics), and vacuity-by-reparametrization (anything that re-presents information already carried by the prior or the loss under a new name). Each phase below names which of these it is most at risk of and how the risk is checked.

## 2. What an observation is in this architecture

The settled position is the manuscript's environmental-agent construction (PIFB:1454-1502): an observation is a clamped boundary agent e with a sharp Gaussian belief q_e = N(c, Sigma_o) = p_e, coupled to ordinary agents through an information-theoretic term, with the sensor's gauge frame identified with the receiver's (Omega = I, an explicit gauge fixing; env agents are the external source fields that break gauge symmetry). The manuscript proves mean-gradient equivalence with the likelihood term for any Sigma_o > 0, and full variational equivalence under the cross-entropy substitution -E_{q_i}[log q_e] = KL(q_i||q_e) + H(q_i) (PIFB:1495-1500).

How to realize this in the codebase was the largest design fork in the brainstorm, and the critics resolved it against the majority of the memos. Five memos proposed that env agents could ride the existing coupling block as extra graph rows with pinned beta = 1 and "zero kernel edits." All three critics killed this route, for three independent reasons verified against the code. First, the attention weights are a row-normalized softmax (free_energy.py:252-263): a pinned beta_{i,e} = 1 inside the simplex would annihilate all inter-agent attention mass, while a finite bias breaks the PIFB:1469-1477 equivalence proposition, whose construction stipulates beta_{i,e} = 1 as a boundary condition outside the normalization (PIFB:1463). Second, a free softmax weight on an env key is agreement-weighted: beta_{i,e} shrinks as the observation gets more surprising, inverting the surprise response a likelihood term must have. Third, the divergence seam saturates at kl_max (default 100), and both the self-term and pair-term gradients carry masks that zero them outside (0, kl_max) (kernels.py:126-132, 225-231) — a sharp sensor at K = 64 would sit in saturation exactly when the observation matters most, and its pull would silently vanish.

The committed design is therefore a separate fixed-weight observation channel added to both gradient paths, exactly as the gated-stub discipline at free_energy.py:341-351 has demanded all along: an additive term in the analytic kernel assembly (belief_gradients, kernels.py:164, which today has no observation argument) and in the autograd oracle (oracle.py), with free_energy()'s dormant log_likelihood argument finally becoming the live oracle-path mirror it was documented to be. The channel takes the cross-entropy form by default,

    F_obs = sum_i [ (1/2)(mu_i - c)^T Lambda_o (mu_i - c) + (1/2) tr(Lambda_o Sigma_i) ] + const,
    dF_obs/dmu_i = Lambda_o (mu_i - c),        dF_obs/dSigma_i = Lambda_o / 2,

because the cross-entropy form is the one with full variational equivalence to the likelihood term and the one whose covariance sector performs Bayesian precision addition. The theory critic's sharpest finding lives here: the pure-KL coupling's covariance gradient (Lambda_o - Sigma_i^{-1})/2 produces precision averaging, not addition — under repeated observations confidence saturates at the sensor precision and evidence never accumulates — so any filtering, evidence-accumulation, or information-gain claim made on the KL route is wrong by construction. The KL form is retained behind a toggle for mean-tracking experiments only, with covariance claims deleted. The observation channel runs with its own saturation policy (kl_max = inf, mirroring the decode's policy at prior_bank.py:273-287, which disables saturation for the same reason: ranking and pull must survive large divergences). An in-softmax env-row variant is kept as an explicitly different, toggled channel — attention-competitive and agreement-weighted, interesting in its own right — but it is not the observation term and the roadmap must never describe it as such.

Two boundary conditions complete the definition. Exogeneity: the channel must carry information not derivable from the PriorBank lookup of the current token, else the term is the 06-07 reparametrization vacuity under a new name; the first exogenous sources are synthetic streams (a tracking task with known ground truth), masked-token reconstruction, or external embeddings. Stability: with the always-on self-coupling alpha against a fixed prior of precision Lambda_p, the xent channel has a finite covariance fixed point

    Sigma*^{-1} = Lambda_p + Lambda_o / alpha,

so the much-feared runaway collapse does not occur in the fixed-prior regime at all. The numerical memo executed the no-prior worst case and found the natural-gradient Fisher factor turns collapse into a sub-geometric crawl (roughly 6.6e4 steps from sigma = 1 to 1e-3 at Lambda_o = 1; the 1e-6 floor unreached in 2e5 steps). The genuine numerical risk is the opposite of dramatic: a silent long-horizon drift to overconfidence, plus the kl_max self-mask cliff — the prior's restoring force switches off discontinuously exactly when beliefs are pushed far from the prior (kernels.py:126-132). The streaming loop therefore carries a live monitor on min-eigenvalues of Sigma and on saturation-mask occupancy from day one.

## 3. The temporal architecture: two kinds of persistence, one new entry point

The memos used "persistent beliefs" for two mathematically distinct mechanisms, and the critics required them separated; conflating them produced most of the cross-memo contradictions, including the entire covariance-collapse dispute.

Init-carry (warm starting) seeds the next step's E-step from the previous converged belief instead of encode's q = p. It changes no objective: at the contraction fixed point the initialization is forgotten, so init-carry is compute amortization — valuable (it is what makes a small per-tick iteration budget feasible) but carrying no memory and resolving no vacuity. Prior-carry (filtering) routes the previous posterior into the prior slot: p_{t+1} = N(Omega mu*_t, Omega Sigma*_t Omega^T + Q), a predict step in the Kalman sense, with Q a mandatory inflation term — under prior-carry with Q = 0 the precision ratchet is real and the agent monotonically sharpens until it ignores new observations. Prior-carry changes F, is the only memory-bearing mechanism, and is the move that resolves the 06-07 vacuity: the prior slot becomes temporal and top-down, the present input becomes bottom-up data, and the observation term acquires information distinct from both prior and target. The two mechanisms get separate toggles and separate falsifiers in the roadmap.

Both enter through the same verified seam, and it is not the one two memos claimed. The prior_source config routes between two (V, K) vocabulary tables only (prior_bank.py:218-231); it cannot host a runtime posterior, and "persistence is a config flip" is false in mechanism. The real seam is that e_step already takes the initial belief and the prior as separate call-site tensors — e_step(belief, mu_p, sigma_p, ...) at e_step.py:460-463 — so persistence is a new model-level entry point, not a kernel change: a model.step(token_ids, state) -> (logits, state) method that branches at the encode call site (model.py:456), seeding belief from carried state (init-carry) and/or substituting the carried-and-transported posterior for the prior tensors (prior-carry), with the returned state detached. The prior slot alpha*KL(q_i||p_i) is thereby booked exactly once per mode: token prior by default, carried posterior under the filtering toggle. The third candidate occupant proposed for that slot — a goal or preference prior — is killed below.

One genuinely new inference-side mechanism is needed: a per-agent clamp mask threaded through the E-step update sites so that env-agent rows hold their beliefs fixed while ordinary rows settle. This touches the update application points in e_step.py and the block/stack handoff, is verifiable by a two-line invariant (clamped rows immobile to tolerance; unclamped rows still descend F), and is the only change the inner loop requires.

The differential-geometry framing earns its keep here as the correct lens on warm-started streaming: the per-tick E-step is a discretized natural-gradient flow under a time-varying objective, the belief tracks the moving minimizer with a lag set by drift rate over local curvature, and F_t is a Lyapunov function between arrivals. This yields the iteration-budget experiment (tracking error versus per-tick iterations versus innovation size) and one guard the numerics memo adds: under truncated settling the attention beta is stale with respect to the final belief, so the kernel-versus-oracle gradient residual at small iteration counts is checked before trusting the analytic kernel in anytime mode. By contrast, the proposal to brand the temporal carry as the manuscript's dim(C)=1 gauge field theory was assessed by the critics as vocabulary rather than content — a one-dimensional base manifold has no curvature, and the proposed holonomy falsifiers were tautologies — and is recorded under deferred material, not in the build path.

## 4. Action, preferences, and the gated epistemic layer

Action enters in the cheapest correct form first: emission. The autoregressive generate loop (model.py:736) already conditions the next forward pass on the emitted token; closed against an environment that responds (rather than a frozen corpus), emission is action in the operational sense that p(o_{t+1}) depends on a_t. The philosophy memo's demarcation gate makes this precise and non-rhetorical: a real interaction model must demonstrate two dependencies — q_t on q_{t-1} (persistent agent) and p(o_{t+1}) on a_t (consequential action) — and a teacher-forced corpus run has neither. That gate is Phase 0's exit criterion.

Preferences were proposed in four different homes across the memos: a clamped preference agent inside the coupling block, a goal prior in the prior slot, an actuator-target agent inside the VFE, and a controller-tier action scorer. The code-collision critic killed every in-settle placement, and the kill is upheld here: a preference that participates in the settling E-step biases perception toward what the agent wants to be true — wishful perception — and, because the settled belief feeds decode and the training loss, it is also training-time leakage through the back door. Preferences live only at the controller tier: they score candidate actions, off the training graph, under no_grad, and never touch any q that feeds decode or CE. The concrete Phase-3 form is a one-step pragmatic scorer: for each candidate action, roll the model one step (predicted observation o-bar(a)), and score by the Gaussian preference cross-entropy

    risk(a) = (1/2)(o-bar(a) - mu_C)^T Lambda_C (o-bar(a) - mu_C) + (1/2) tr(Lambda_C Sigma_o(a)) + const,

selecting argmin (or sampling the softmax over -risk/gamma). This is active inference's pragmatic value, it is closed-form, and it requires no rollout machinery beyond the step function Phase 0 builds.

The expected free energy is handled with the honesty the user asked for. Four findings discipline it. First, a structural seduction was killed: the observation that the policy posterior q(a) = softmax(-G(a)/gamma) has the same form as the attention row beta = softmax(-E/tau) is the generic Gibbs variational identity, not a stationarity property of the canonical F — there is no policy term in F whose row-Lagrangian yields it, and wiring one in would feed policy gradients into the beliefs, violating the target-blind E-step every memo independently demands. If a policy layer is built, it is an external controller-tier scorer that happens to reuse the softmax form, and it must carry its own entropy-plus-prior term tau_pi * q(a) log(q(a)/pi_0(a)) or it reproduces, at the policy tier, exactly the entropy-suppressed-surrogate gradient mismatch the manuscript warns about for attention (PIFB:1251-1258). Second, the memos themselves contained the standard EFE conflations — one stated the canonical EFE with the epistemic sign flipped (producing the FEF, the precise error Millidge, Tschantz and Buckley 2021 diagnose), and another advertised the FEEF as "the time-extension of the VFE" when it is the VFE of a counterfactual preference-biased generative model. The form choice (canonical EFE versus FEEF versus one-step information-gain-plus-risk) is pre-registered at the Phase-4 gate and decided there, not before. Third, the epistemic term can be provably inert: under linear-Gaussian dynamics with additive control the information gain is policy-independent (Koudahl et al. 2021), so an EFE agent in such an environment reduces to a pragmatic agent plus a constant. No theorem governs this architecture's nonlinear settling map, but the design consequence is hard: the crucial experiment must give actions multiplicative control — action selects the observation precision Lambda_o(a), which is what "go look" means — or the ablation would falsely kill a layer that never had a chance to act. The T-maze does this by construction (visiting the cue arm is choosing a high-precision observation channel). Fourth, the numerics bound the form: imagined rollouts through the nonlinear settling fixed point are unmeasured beyond a few steps, so the policy layer is short-horizon by design — K at most 5 imagined steps, preferring one-step — and is evaluated on policy-ranking stability under float32 rather than absolute G accuracy.

The epistemic layer's existence is therefore an empirical question with a pre-registered answer procedure, and that procedure is the philosophy memo's crucial experiment. In a T-maze with a cue arm (information, no reward) and two reward arms (reward known only after the cue), compare full G = risk - information-gain against the pragmatic-only ablation and against plain free-energy descent with no policy layer. The readout is cue-first visitation. If full G is statistically indistinguishable from pragmatic-only, the epistemic layer has no behavioral content in this architecture and is deleted rather than retuned — and the architecture's action story is, with no embarrassment, pragmatic preference scoring over a recursive Gaussian filter. If it passes, a transfer test on a fresh maze geometry with no retuning checks that exploration is a corollary of the functional rather than a fitted bonus (the Lakatosian novel fact).

## 5. The phased roadmap

| Phase | Deliverable | New mechanism | Crucial experiment | Kill condition |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | model.step + InteractionLoop + toy environment | call-site state carry (init-carry only) | two dependence probes | step cannot close the loop without kernel edits; or carried state changes single-call golden behavior |
| 1 | exogenous observation channel + clamp mask | fixed-weight xent term in kernels.py + oracle.py; per-agent clamp | FD gradient check; tracking with Sigma* fixed point | FD mismatch; or channel adds nothing beyond prior+target (vacuity) |
| 2 | prior-carry filtering mode | p_{t+1} = transported q*_t with inflation Q | drift tracking vs re-encode; Q=0 ratchet ablation | filtering no better than re-encoding at equal compute |
| 3 | pragmatic action at controller tier | one-step preference scorer (no_grad, off-graph) | preferred-outcome steering vs greedy/random | cannot beat greedy decode; or any train-graph perturbation (EM leak) |
| 4 | epistemic layer (gated) | short-horizon G with multiplicative control | T-maze with pre-registered epistemic ablation | full G indistinguishable from pragmatic-only -> delete the layer |

Phase 0 builds the loop and nothing else: model.step(token_ids, state=None) branching at the encode call site (model.py:456), an InteractionLoop driver and Environment protocol as click-to-run config-dict entry points, and a trivial token environment. Persistence is init-carry only; the returned state is detached. Exit requires the two demarcation probes and a golden check that the single-call forward is unchanged. Stated honestly: Phase 0 has no VFE-discriminating content; it establishes that the loop exists and that nothing regressed.

Phase 1 is where the direction can first genuinely fail, and it is the largest phase. It lands the clamp mask; the observation channel in both gradient paths with the xent/KL toggle, its own kl_max policy, and free_energy()'s log_likelihood going live as the oracle mirror; finite-difference verification of the new term against the autograd-of-F oracle (the project's standard discipline); the Sigma-health and mask-saturation monitors; and the first exogenous stream — a synthetic linear-Gaussian tracking task with known ground truth, where the xent route's steady state must land on Sigma*^{-1} = Lambda_p + Lambda_o/alpha and the mean must track the analytic filter. The Kalman comparison is run on the xent route only; on the KL route it would fail by construction (precision averaging), and observing exactly that failure is a cheap confirmation of the theory critic's adjudication. The metrics double-count trap is handled here: the channel's contribution to F is booked as a separate observation term, never folded into the CE-derived data_term at metrics.py.

Phase 2 turns on prior-carry with mandatory Q, implemented through the e_step prior-tensor arguments at the step call site. Its experiment is tracking a drifting target: filtering must beat full re-encoding at equal compute, the lag must scale with drift rate as the flow analysis predicts, and the Q = 0 ablation must reproduce the precision ratchet (confirming Q is load-bearing, not decorative). This phase is also where the roadmap meets the shadow-prior programme: prior-carry is the degenerate self-shadow p_i(t+1) sourced from the agent's own past rather than from a scale-(s+1) meta-agent, and if the shadow-prior roadmap is ever built the two share this seam.

Phase 3 adds the pragmatic controller described above, with the baseline ladder (greedy decode, random, softmax sampling) and the EM-leak check: action scoring must be bitwise absent from the training graph.

Phase 4 is gated, not scheduled. Its preconditions are an environment with multiplicative control and an explicit generative emission model declared as new theory — the decode is discriminative, and treating its vocabulary-normalized logits as p(o|s) would re-import the log-partition gap the 06-07 analysis closed. Its content is the short-horizon G, the policy softmax with its own entropy term, the closed-form Gaussian information gain (1/2) log|I + Lambda_o(a) Sigma_p| validated against Monte Carlo before use (the closed form is exact only in the conjugate idealization; the actual update operator is the nonlinear E-step), and the T-maze with its pre-registered ablation as the sole arbiter of the layer's survival.

## 6. Consolidated risks

The streaming regime's numerical risks are slow and silent rather than explosive: long-horizon overconfidence drift under the xent channel (monitored, with the fixed-prior pull and Q-inflation as the structural counterforces); the kl_max self-mask cliff, where the prior's restoring gradient vanishes discontinuously at high surprise (monitored via mask occupancy; the observation channel itself runs unsaturated); float32 drift in the gauge frames over many exp/log round trips (re-projection cadence to be set empirically); and stale-beta envelope inconsistency under truncated settling (guarded by the kernel-versus-oracle residual at small iteration counts). The conceptual risks are the three vacuity modes at new seams: preference leakage into perception (killed structurally by the controller-tier placement), double-booking of the data term in diagnostics (separate booking, Phase 1), and an "exogenous" channel that re-presents the prior (the exogeneity condition, Phase 1). The programmatic risk is the one the philosophy memo named: an EFE layer that is autoregressive sampling in active-inference vocabulary. The Phase-4 gate exists so that if the epistemic term is behaviorally inert here, the roadmap says so and deletes it.

## 7. Deliberately not built

Each deferred item carries its trigger. Timestamp-keyed positional gauge phases and recency attention priors: deferred until an irregularly-timed stream demonstrably defeats index keying. Online M-step with forgetting protection (replay, parameter-space penalties on the PriorBank): deferred until a stream task shows frozen-M with beliefs-as-fast-weights saturating; until then the M-step stays frozen during interaction. Regime-II temporal connections (a learned time-edge holonomy): deferred until a provably path-dependent temporal task beats the flat carry. The dim(C)=1 gauge-field framing: recorded as interpretation only; it acquires content only if a base of dimension at least two (time times space) is ever instantiated. A policy term inside free_energy.py: killed, for the EM-separation reasons above. bf16 streaming: the SPD retraction keeps its float32 island unconditionally; mixed precision is out of scope for the loop.

## 8. Open questions for the user

First, which exogenous stream should Phase 1 target after the synthetic tracking task — masked-text reconstruction, an external embedding channel (the multimodal reading), or directly the conversational/tool-use environment in which emission is already consequential? The synthetic task is non-negotiable as the verification bed; the question is what carries the first real semantics. Second, is the T-maze ablation acceptable as the binding go/no-go for the entire epistemic investment, with deletion (not retuning) as the committed consequence of a null result? Third, should Phase 2's prior-carry be developed as the degenerate self-shadow it is, or held until the scale-(s+1) meta-agent of the shadow-prior programme exists so both land together? Fourth, are actions token-valued for the foreseeable future (emission into a responding environment), or is a continuous action channel (an actuator vector with its own clamped boundary agent) wanted early enough to shape Phase 3? Fifth, the per-tick compute envelope: is the target regime interactive-conversational (hundreds of milliseconds per tick, generous iteration budgets) or control-loop real time (milliseconds, where the anytime-truncation guards become load-bearing)?
