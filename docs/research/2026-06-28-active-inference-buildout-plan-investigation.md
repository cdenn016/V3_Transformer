## 1. What the plan is

The plan proposes adding a default-off, no-grad, inference-time Expected-Free-Energy (EFE) policy scorer that ranks a finite menu of candidate continuations (and, separately, candidate agent/applicant sets) by a per-policy score `G(pi) = risk + ambiguity = KL[q(o|pi)||p(o|C)] + E_q H[p(o|s)]`, selected via `softmax(-gamma_policy * G)` (`investigation:16-39, 79`). Mechanically it factors the currently-inline `forward()` pipeline (encode -> pos_phi -> attention log_prior/rope -> `vfe_stack` -> final_norm -> decode, `model.py:660-792`) into a public belief-rollout helper, registers a `policy_mode` registry, and rolls beliefs forward over a short horizon on top of the existing `@torch.no_grad()` `generate()` loop (`model.py:1164-1222`). The ablation is staged Phase 0-4: Phase 0 fixes/quarantines blockers, Phase 1 is a one-step reranker on a frozen checkpoint, Phase 2 ablates the score decomposition against temperature-tuned logprob, Phase 3 tests horizons H=2/4 with closed-loop sample metrics and a >=3-seed requirement, Phase 4 repeats on the pure KL prior-bank path, and only afterward does Phase 5 contemplate a train-time EFE regularizer (`investigation:122-144`). Falsifiers are lesion-style placebos: shuffled sigma, sign-flipped epistemic term, random score, and compute-matched beam/best-of-N (`investigation:146-152`). The debate constrains all of this to a hard pre-build A/B/C/D/E contract (outcome likelihood A, rollout/transition rule B, preference prior p(o|C) as C, initial belief D, policy/candidate prior E) plus explicit horizon, policy precision, separately-logged risk/ambiguity diagnostics, fixed falsifiers, and matched-compute baselines, to be written as a spec before any code (`05_action.md:8-10`; `03_blue_rebuttal.md:19`).

## 3. Theory soundness (held under adversarial check)

C4 held at high confidence after an attack on both halves (equations/signs and references). The EFE functional `G_tau(pi)` (`investigation:16-19`), the risk+ambiguity split (`24-26`), the pragmatic+epistemic/mutual-information split (`34-39`), and the `softmax(-gamma G)` policy posterior are stated with correct signs and match the discrete active-inference canon (Friston et al. 2017; Smith, Friston and Whyte 2022) and the wiki's `[[Expected Free Energy]]` page term-for-term: bare definition matches `EFE.md:56`, risk+ambiguity matches `EFE.md:93-94`, pragmatic+epistemic with the MI-equivalence of the epistemic term matches `EFE.md:72-73, 81-82`. All nine cited references are real, with source notes and downloaded PDFs in the Research vault carrying DOIs/arXiv IDs, including the ones most likely to be fabricated (Waade et al. 2025 *As One and Many*, Entropy doi 10.3390/e27020143; Heins et al. 2024 PNAS doi 10.1073/pnas.2320239121; Friston et al. 2024 *Federated Inference and Belief Sharing*, doi 10.1016/j.neubiorev.2023.105500). No sign error, no fabricated citation.

Two corrections to the claim text itself (neither affects the verdict):

- "Da Costa 2020" is genuine canon but is NOT among the plan's nine references; the plan's nine are a different, fully verified set. The assessment may cite Da Costa as a canon anchor but should not attribute it to the plan.
- Parr and Friston 2019 is cited by the plan for the EFE form, but its object is generalized free energy, a strict superset; the plan uses plain EFE. Not wrong, a slight over-attribution.

Canonical subtleties the plan softens (completeness, not error): the "up to standard assumptions" hedge on the pragmatic+epistemic identity (`investigation:38`) never enumerates the load-bearing assumptions (predictive outcomes factor through the true likelihood `Q(o,s|pi)=P(o|s)Q(s|pi)`, and the future recognition density is the exact Bayes inverse), which under V3's approximate iterative E-step hold only approximately; policy precision `gamma` is a fixed knob rather than inferred via a Gamma prior (fine while it stays a hyperparameter; flag if Phase 5 makes it learnable, since the wiki warns learnable softmax temperature is unbounded-below in tau, `Multi-agent VFE.md:92`); and the community score's temporal discount `rho_tau` (`investigation:57`) is a defensible RL-style add-on, not canon.

## 4. Blockers (ranked)

All cited blockers are real. Ranked by how much they gate the recommended first scorer (no-grad, flat, one-step reranker). C5 held: blockers 4-6 do not bite the recommended scorer; the binding prerequisites are conceptual.

1. Preferences p(o|C) do not exist and are not defined (highest). The binding blocker per both canon and debate: without an explicit preference distribution and a genuine epistemic term, the scorer is a relabeled reranker (`02_red_opening.md:9,29`; `investigation:104`). The risk term is undefined until C is specified. This blocks legitimacy, not just code.
2. Sigma-as-ambiguity is unearned (high, and a divergence, see Section 6). The canonical ambiguity term is likelihood entropy `H[p(o|s)]`, not generic covariance magnitude (`03_red_rebuttal.md:13`). The plan's Phase-1 default ("ambiguity proxy can be a function of future sigma trace or entropy", `investigation:136`) is exactly what Red flagged. Must re-derive as outcome-likelihood entropy or first validate sigma against realized error/calibration before naming it ambiguity.
3. Generation cost (high, structural). `generate()` recomputes the full encode->E-step->decode per token with no KV/belief cache and truncates context (`model.py:1198-1201,1200`), so a top-k horizon-H rollout multiplies cost by ~Kp*H (`investigation:110`). This is what makes the compute-matched baselines decisive. The plan already guards this with the wall-clock honesty test "If the result only improves because it spends far more compute, it is not an architectural win" (`investigation:110`).
4. Observation-likelihood stub (medium, sidestepped). `log_likelihood` is inert (`free_energy.py:341,358-368,401-402`); a live observation pull would also need wiring into `kernels.py` and `oracle.py` (docstring `360-363`). The no-grad flat reranker never touches the E-step objective, so this bites only the Phase-5 train-time extension.
5. Covariant-oracle sigma-leaf gap (medium, sidestepped). `_omega_builder` closes over `belief.sigma` (`e_step.py:426-435`, guarded by `transport_mode in _TRANSPORT_NEEDS_MU`); affects only regime_ii/regime_ii_covariant. The recommended Phase-1 scorer is flat, so it is untouched. Bites only a covariant policy-gradient or train-time extension.
6. Flat model-channel transport (medium, scoping). The s/gamma channel is hardcoded `transport_mode="flat"` (`model.py:1047`). This supports a flat agent-set/route-selection experiment but NOT a non-flat covariant community policy; the debate forbids non-flat agent-set claims until a covariant rollout is built and tested (`04_verdict_code.md:91`; `03_red_rebuttal.md:25`).

Net: blockers 4-6 are non-fatal for the recommended no-grad flat one-step scorer (C5 confirmed); blockers 1-2 are conceptual prerequisites the spec must resolve before any code; blocker 3 is the reason the experiment lives or dies on matched-compute controls, which the plan already schedules.

## 5. Experimental-design critique (CORRECTED)

The draft asserted (C6) that the design "cannot yet deliver an honest yes/no" because it never schedules a closed-loop/downstream phase, omits the cheapest near-competitor baselines, and lacks a seed-noise floor at the operating point. The adversarial pass refuted the load-bearing form of this claim against the primary source, and the primary-source re-read confirms the refutation. The corrected position follows.

What the design already contains (the refutation):

- Compute-matched controls are scheduled, not merely named as falsifiers. Phase 2 requires the full score to "beat the best component and a temperature-tuned logprob baseline" (`investigation:138`); the executive judgment commits to "compute-matched log-probability, beam, random-score, and shuffled-uncertainty controls" (`investigation:7`); and the falsifier set includes "compute-matched beam or best-of-N" (`investigation:150`). Combined with the wall-clock honesty test at `investigation:110`, the design does contain the machinery to distinguish a real win from a compute-only win. The draft's conclusion that it "could fail to distinguish a real win from a compute-only win" does not hold as written.
- A >=3-seed gate is required by the design. Phase 3 states "Require at least three seeds or a matched checkpoint set before claiming a small effect" (`investigation:140`), and the train-time phase requires "compute-matched controls, and at least three seeds" (`investigation:144`). The deficiency is not in the design; it is that the CURRENT empirical data is single-seed (seed=6, `digest:163`) and the only multi-seed floor (R2 scaling) is the disjoint 60k-step/head_mixer regime, not the 15k-step/linear-decode ablation operating point. So the corrected statement is: the seed floor is mandated by the plan but has not yet been MEASURED at the operating point; that measurement is a prerequisite, not a design fix.
- The closed-loop regime is engaged, narrowly. Line 71 lists "the model's own later belief state" as an active case; Phase 3 evaluates "repetition and distinct-n sample metrics" (`investigation:140`), which require autoregressive emission; "task success in closed-loop generation" is named as a primary metric (`investigation:148`); and "if the policy does not causally change future observations in a closed-loop setting" is a named falsifier (`investigation:150`). The draft's "never schedules a closed-loop/downstream phase" is therefore too strong.

What survives as real but marginal spec-tightening items (the residue):

- No dedicated downstream task-success phase. "Task success in closed-loop generation" (`investigation:148`) and the closed-loop-causality falsifier (`investigation:150`) are named as a primary metric and falsifier, but no Phase 0-4 step instantiates a concrete runnable downstream task or interactive loop to evaluate them. The accurate gap is "a named primary metric and falsifier without a runnable phase," not "no closed-loop phase at all."
- Cheap near-competitors are present only as near-equivalents, not as separately named arms. The ambiguity-only decomposition arm (`investigation:138`) approximates a predictive-entropy/confidence reranker, and length-normalization is intrinsic to the scheduled beam/best-of-N. The spec should still name an explicit predictive-entropy/confidence reranker and explicit length-normalization (and the debate's locally-typical sampling and sampling adapters, `03_red_rebuttal.md:23`) so the comparison is unambiguous, but the draft's framing that the design "omits the cheapest near-competitor baselines" overstated their absence.
- Single-seed current data. As above: the floor exists in the design (>=3 seeds) but the measured data does not yet (`digest:163`). The paired same-checkpoint inference-time comparisons (EFE vs logprob on identical weights) remove seed variance for Phases 1-3; only cross-seed/train-time claims need the floor measured.
- Soft, multiplicity-uncorrected keep criterion. "Improve 1 primary OR 2 of ~8 secondary metrics" (`investigation:148`) has no effect-size bar and no multiple-comparisons correction, while each lesion control is strict. Asymmetric. Tighten "improve" to a magnitude beyond the operating-point seed floor, require the SAME metric to clear across seeds, and add explicit go/no-go gates between phases (only Phase 2 currently states a bar).

Net correction: the design is conservative and largely self-controlled; it already contains the compute-matched baselines, the wall-clock honesty check, the >=3-seed gate, and engagement with the closed-loop regime that the draft claimed were missing. The genuine remaining work is to instantiate a runnable downstream task, name the cheap baselines explicitly, measure the seed floor at the operating point, and harden the keep criterion. These are spec-tightening items, not a load-bearing incapacity to deliver a yes/no.

## 6. Debate constraints (C7, held under adversarial check)

Binding outcome: BLUE_WINS, but strictly narrow, reached by panel=full Rule 3 (code-truth and scope both BLUE_WINS form a majority that overrode the canon-strict REMAND; `04_verdict.md:13,21,25`). The override means the canon-strict equivocation concern was recorded and outvoted, not resolved on the merits (`04_verdict_canon.md:5,48`). The win accepts ONLY a default-off, no-grad, inference-time EFE scorer over explicit candidate continuations or agent sets, conditioned on a written contract.

The A/B/C/D/E pre-build contract (mandatory before code, `05_action.md:8-10`, `03_blue_rebuttal.md:19`): A = outcome likelihood; B = transition/rollout rule through V3 beliefs; C = prior preference distribution p(o|C); D = initial belief from current context; E = policy/candidate prior. Plus: outcome space q(o|pi), fixed finite horizon, policy prior OR precision gamma, risk and ambiguity diagnostics logged separately from raw log-prob, a fixed (pre-registered) candidate generator, fixed falsifiers, matched-compute baselines, and a public no-grad belief-rollout helper returning final beliefs+logits.

Must-not-claim list (off-limits). The literal word "seven" does not appear in the debate docs; the seven prohibitions are an aggregation across the four judge documents (`04_verdict.md:29` gives three, `04_verdict_scope.md:55` one, `04_verdict_code.md:91` three). The aggregation is defensible:

1. That V3 already implements an EFE/active-inference scorer (`04_verdict.md:29`).
2. That EFE efficacy / PPL / generation-quality improvement has been shown (`05_action.md:10`).
3. That a train-time EFE replacement is justified (`04_verdict_scope.md:55`: not until a policy-conditioned generative layer exists AND the no-grad scorer survives all ablations).
4. Calling V3 sigma an ambiguity/epistemic value without validating it predicts future error/calibration/info-gain (`03_red_rebuttal.md:13`).
5. Non-flat covariant community-policy claims over the model channel until a non-flat covariant rollout is built and tested (`04_verdict_code.md:91`).
6. Sharing empirical conclusions between candidate-continuation and agent-set experiments; they need separate outcome spaces, metrics, and baselines (`04_verdict_scope.md:37,55`).
7. (anti-Lakatos) Post-hoc rescue by changing preferences, horizon, candidates, or proxies after a failed result; falsifiers must be fixed up front (`04_verdict_scope.md:12`).

Where the debate is more cautious than the plan (the plan must change to comply). Two of these three divergences are solid; the third is weak:

- The plan's Phase-1 sigma-trace ambiguity proxy (`investigation:136`) is exactly what Red rejected (must-not-claim #4). Solid divergence.
- The plan folds continuations and agent sets into one `policy_mode` registry (`investigation:87-98,158`); the debate requires two sub-experiments with non-shared conclusions (#6). Solid divergence.
- The plan "jumps to API shape" (`investigation:83-98`). Weak as a divergence: `05_action.md:10` itself lists "rollout API" as a required spec deliverable, so sketching the API is arguably part of the mandated spec, and the plan predates the verdict by ~3.5h (the plan is the debate's subject, not a downstream artifact that ignored the verdict). Treat this as "the plan's API sketch must be folded into the spec," not "the plan violated the spec-first rule."

All four canon-cop passes recorded zero strikes (the one raw flag was a validator false-positive over a nonexistent bibliography). Both sides anchored AI theory to external canon and used the wiki/report/code only as feasibility evidence; the spec must do the same.

## 7. Verdict: could / should / would

Could it be built? Yes. The insertion point is empirically verified (code-truth ran a live smoke check: forward returns logits, generate appends after a no-grad forward; `04_verdict_code.md:15-23`), the integration map is accurate (C1), no prior infrastructure exists to collide with (C2), and the recommended no-grad flat one-step scorer sidesteps every code blocker 4-6 (C3, C5). Building it is low-risk engineering.

Should it be built now? Not as code. Two conceptual prerequisites (an explicit preference distribution p(o|C); a legitimate non-sigma ambiguity term) are unresolved, and the debate explicitly demands they be fixed in a written contract first (C7). The narrow BLUE_WINS is permission to run one finite, falsifiable experiment under a hard contract, not a vindication of EFE efficacy.

Would it pay off? Honestly uncertain, and the plan's own most-plausible win (calibration/uncertainty, `investigation:116`) is also its most contestable, because the sigma-as-ambiguity reading is unearned until validated against realized error. The genuine risk, flagged by both Red and the plan, is that EFE reduces to a costly reranker. Correcting C6: the experiment as designed is better equipped to detect that failure than the draft credited. It already schedules compute-matched logprob/beam/best-of-N controls, a wall-clock honesty check, and a >=3-seed gate, so it CAN in principle separate a real win from a compute-only win. The residual reasons for uncertainty are narrower and concrete: the seed floor has not yet been measured at the 15k-step/linear-decode operating point (current data is single-seed, `digest:163`), there is no runnable downstream/closed-loop task instantiating the named primary metric and falsifier, and the cheap near-competitor baselines and keep criterion need to be named and hardened.

Recommended next step: write the spec, not code. I agree with the debate. Concretely the spec should (a) instantiate A/B/C/D/E with an explicit, falsifiable preference distribution p(o|C) and re-derive ambiguity as outcome-likelihood entropy, or pre-register sigma-validation against realized error/calibration as a gate; (b) split candidate-continuation and agent-set into two sub-experiments with separate metrics/baselines; (c) name the cheap baselines explicitly (predictive-entropy/confidence reranker, length-normalized logprob, top-p, locally-typical/sampling adapters) and elevate matched-compute beam/best-of-N from falsifiers to scheduled comparison arms with explicit go/no-go gates; (d) instantiate a runnable downstream/closed-loop task so the plan's already-named "task success in closed-loop generation" primary metric (`investigation:148`) and closed-loop-causality falsifier (`investigation:150`) are actually evaluable; (e) commit to measuring the >=3-seed plain-baseline floor (already mandated at `investigation:140,144`) at the 15k-step/linear-decode operating point, and set a magnitude+significance keep/demote bar with multiplicity correction; (f) cite external AI canon for legitimacy and the wiki/code only as feasibility, and ship a real external bibliography. The spec should be reviewed against the A/B/C/D/E contract before a single line of scorer code is written.
