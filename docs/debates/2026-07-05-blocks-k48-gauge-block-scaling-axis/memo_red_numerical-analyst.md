# Memo — debate-expert-numerical-analyst — red — opening — blocks-k48-gauge-block-scaling-axis

## Lens
Numerical analysis — FLOP accounting, fit conditioning / identifiability, dynamic range of a power-law regression, and the difference between a fit that *looks* good (R^2) and one whose exponent is *determined*.

## Steelman of the opposing position
The five blocks_K48 points are strictly monotone with tight seed noise (PPL std <= 1.08 << the 32-PPL gap), and the analytic FLOP proxy est_flops_analytic moves only 1.03x, so within the reported instrument the loss falls while the compute proxy holds — a clean "more accuracy at fixed compute" story.

## My position (in service of red)
Two load-bearing sub-claims fail on numerical grounds. (1) "Compute is essentially flat" is an artifact of a decode-dominated proxy that is, by construction, blind to the axis being scaled: the only compute term that varies is the transport sub-term `2*N*d_head^2` (run_artifacts.py:626, `d_head=g`), which grows 64x (2304 -> 147456 FLOP/token, GL3->GL24), but it rides on the fixed decode term `2*V*K = 4,824,672` FLOP/token (line 625), so its share is diluted from 0.30% to 3.31% and the total moves 1.030x. The real instrument, wall time, is U-shaped and non-monotone (6366 -> 4657 min -> 11049 s); the best-loss run GL24 is the slowest, GL24/GL8 = 2.37x, GL24/GL3 = 1.74x. Compute is not flat; it is masked. (2) The "scaling exponent" is not identified. Over this compressed x-range the 3-parameter offset law `CE = E + A*N^{-alpha}` is rank-deficient: I reproduce alpha = 0.929 vs n_params and 0.180 vs n_gen on *identical* CE data (a 5.15x swing), with parameter correlation corr(E, alpha) = 0.976 / 0.998 — near +1, meaning the floor E and the exponent alpha are not separately estimable. n_params spans 0.559 decades and n_gen 0.903 decades; both are under one order of magnitude, below the >=2-decade floor the power-law literature requires. This is an ill-conditioned, axis-dependent regression, not a measured exponent.

## Evidence
- **Executed conditioning test (numpy/scipy 1.17.1, 5 seed-averaged blocks_K48 points).** Offset-law fit `CE = E + A*N^{-alpha}`: vs **n_params** alpha = 0.9288, R^2 = 0.99845, corr(E,alpha) = **+0.976**; vs **n_gen** alpha = 0.1804, R^2 = 0.99657, corr(E,alpha) = **+0.998**. Ratio alpha(n_params)/alpha(n_gen) = **5.15x** on the same y. Dynamic range: n_params **0.559 decades** (3.62x), n_gen **0.903 decades** (8x); reference grow_K_GL10 spans 1.079 decades (12x) and is axis-robust (0.558/0.555/0.569). The R^2 ~ 0.998 measures curve fit, not identifiability; the ~+1 E/alpha correlation is the ill-conditioning. curve_fit's linearized CI ([0.66, 1.20]) understates the uncertainty for a 3-parameter, 5-point nonlinear fit, so the evidence pack's bootstrap CI **[0.07, 1.73]** (a factor-25 span crossing alpha=1) is the honest width — an undetermined exponent.
- **Executed FLOP recomputation (run_artifacts.py:611,624-627).** `fpt_decode = 2*V*K = 4,824,672` (fixed; the evidence/task gloss of "~12M/token" conflates this with active-*params*/token 12.06M — the FLOP proxy is 4.82M). `fpt_estep = L*T*(2*N*K + 2*N*g^2)`: GL3 = 14,592, GL24 = 159,744. estep/decode share = 0.30% -> 3.31%. Per-token total ratio GL24/GL3 = **1.0300x** while its own transport term grows **64x** — the proxy's flatness is signal masking, not constancy of work.
- **[Clauset, Shalizi & Newman 2009, "Power-Law Distributions in Empirical Data," SIAM Review 51(4):661-703]** (verbatim, extracted from PDF): "Typically this slope is extracted by performing a least-squares linear regression on the logarithm of the histogram ... Unfortunately, this method and other variations on the same theme generate significant systematic errors under relatively common conditions, as discussed in Appendix A." And: "the regression methods all give significantly biased values ... in each case where the estimate is biased, the corresponding error estimate gives no warning of the bias: there is nothing to alert unwary experimenters to the fact that their results are substantially incorrect." Directly indicts a log-log exponent read off 5 points with a linearized CI.
- **[Stumpf & Porter 2012, "Critical Truths About Power Laws," Science 335(6069):665-666]** (as reported by secondary summary, not personally OCR-verified): a credible empirical power law should span **at least two orders of magnitude**. blocks_K48 offers 0.56–0.90 decades — a third to a half of one.
- **[Belsley, Kuh & Welsch 1980, *Regression Diagnostics*, Wiley]** (condition-index thresholds as standardly attributed): a scaled condition index ~10 signals weak dependencies affecting estimates, and >100 signals estimates carrying substantial error; a regressor that is nearly a linear combination of others yields unstable estimates with inflated standard errors. My normalized Jacobian condition number reaches 274 for the n_gen fit; the scale-invariant E/alpha correlation of 0.98–0.998 is the collinearity itself.
- **[Hoffmann et al. 2022, Chinchilla]** (external source of truth): `L(N,D) = E + A/N^alpha + B/D^beta`; loss depends jointly on N and D, and a scaling exponent must be axis-robust. The 2x token-budget gap (245.76M vs 491.52M) and the 5x axis-dependent alpha both violate this.

## Falsification conditions
