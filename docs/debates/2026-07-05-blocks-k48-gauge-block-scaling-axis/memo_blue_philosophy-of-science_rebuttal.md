## Evidence
- **Bogen & Woodward 1988, "Saving the Phenomena," *Philosophical Review* 97:303-352, pp. 306, 314.** "Phenomena are detected through the use of data, but in most cases are not observable in any interesting sense of that term" (p. 306); "we need to distinguish what theories explain (phenomena or facts about phenomena) from what is uncontroversially observable (data)" (p. 314). The block-sweep regularity is a detected phenomenon; red's proxy/exponent/label critiques are critiques of the *theory that would explain* it, not of the phenomenon's existence.
- **Pearl 2009, "Causal Inference in Statistics: An Overview," *Statistics Surveys* 3:96-146, p. 99.** "An associational concept is any relationship that can be defined in terms of a joint distribution of observed variables, and a causal concept is any relationship that cannot be defined from the distribution alone... there is nothing in the joint distribution of symptoms and diseases to tell us that curing the former would or would not cure the latter." The measured `P(CE | block size)` is associational and robust; red's confound defeats only the interventional query `P(CE | do(gauge structure))`. Red conflates killing the causal concept with killing the associational one.
- **Duhem 1906, *The Aim and Structure of Physical Theory*, p. 187** (via SEP "Underdetermination"). "The physicist can never subject an isolated hypothesis to experimental test, but only a whole group of hypotheses... the experiment does not designate which one should be changed." Duhem underdetermines *which co-varying knob gets credit* — gauge algebra vs. head-width recovery vs. head-count reduction vs. table capacity vs. softmax retuning. He presupposes something changed. This is a demand for a control experiment, not a proof that the effect is absent.
- **Popper 1963, *Conjectures and Refutations*.** "Irrefutability is not a virtue of a theory but a vice." Turned against red: the narrow sub-claim is eminently refutable — red's own falsification condition 3 names the refuting observation. A claim with achievable, pre-registered falsifiers holds the demarcation credential Popper prizes, so Popper defeats the *compound* while endorsing the *sub-claim*.
- **Hacking 1983, *Representing and Intervening*, Part B ("Experimentation has a life of its own").** Experiment "has a life of its own, independent of theory." The seed-robust CE regularity is separable from the theory-laden efficiency proxy; red is right the proxy is flat by construction and does not reach the experimental regularity.

## Falsifiability assessment of the claim
The *compound* claim ("parameter-efficient publishable scaling axis") is under-falsifiable on its exponent leg — an exponent that takes any value in [0.07, 1.73] with the analyst's x-axis forbids almost nothing, and I concede this fully to red's section 2. The *residual* sub-claim is sharply falsifiable: (i) a `blocks_K48` run at the matched 491.52M-token budget that erases the GL3 -> GL24 CE decrease refutes the effect; (ii) a non-gauge multi-head control at `d_model=48` swept 16x`d_k=3` -> 2x`d_k=24` that reproduces the monotone gain without `phi_embed` growth refutes gauge-specificity; (iii) a matched-parameter plain `V x m` table that reproduces the GL24 gain refutes the generator-algebra attribution. These are red's own falsification conditions 1 and 3, which is the tell that a defined, corroborated phenomenon exists to be tested.
