# Memo — debate-expert-transformer-ml — red — opening — blocks-k48-gauge-block-scaling-axis

## Lens
Transformer architecture — scaled dot-product attention, multi-head decomposition (`d_k = d_model / h`), head-count vs head-width trade-off, the softmax `1/sqrt(d_k)` temperature, and the sparse-embedding total-vs-active parameter frame.

## Steelman of the opposing position
At fixed `embed_dim`, enlarging the GL(g) gauge block raises the per-head generator count `n_gen = 48*g`, so each head carries a richer intra-block transport algebra than plain multi-head width supplies, and if that added structure lowers loss at constant per-token active work it is a real, parameter-efficient axis distinct from growing `d_model`.

## My position (in service of red)
"Enlarging the GL gauge block at fixed `embed_dim=48`" is not an isolated gauge manipulation; by the code's own construction it IS the Vaswani multi-head trade-off `d_k = d_model / h` run backward. At fixed `K=48` the block width `g` equals `d_head` and sets `n_heads = 48/g` (run_artifacts.py:611 `d_head = K/n_blocks`; model.py:172 `HeadMixer(irrep_dims)`; one block per head). Going GL3->GL24 therefore drives `d_head` 3->24 and `n_heads` 16->2 in lockstep with the loss drop, and inflates total params 3.62x almost entirely inside a token-indexed `phi_embed (V, n_gen)` lookup (prior_bank.py:167,682) that MoE bookkeeping counts as total-not-active. The CE improvement is fully explained by (a) recovery from pathologically narrow `d_head=3` heads to reasonable-width heads at fixed `d_model`, (b) the known head-count/head-width redundancy trade-off, and (c) sparse-embedding total-param inflation — none of which is a scaling axis "complementary to width-scaling"; it is a move WITHIN the `d_model = h * d_k` parameterization.

The project's own softmax temperature seals the identification: `attention_tau` returns `tau = kappa * sqrt(d_energy)` with `d_energy` = block size = `d_head`, and its docstring states `kappa=1 -> Vaswani recovery` and "every head's softmax runs at the Vaswani temperature of the dimension ITS energy accumulates over" (free_energy.py:42-54). So `g` is literally `d_k` in the project's own `1/sqrt(d_k)` construction; enlarging the block moves `d_k` and re-scales the softmax temperature `~ sqrt(g)` — a fourth bundled confound.

## Evidence
- **Vaswani et al. 2017, "Attention Is All You Need," NeurIPS, Section 3.2.2 (Multi-Head Attention).** Verbatim: "h=8 parallel attention layers, or heads"; "d_k = d_v = d_model/h = 64"; "Due to the reduced dimension of each head, the total computational cost is similar to that of single-head attention with full dimensionality." This is the exact axis `blocks_K48` traverses: at fixed `d_model=48` the sweep sets `h = 48/g` and `d_k = g`, so GL3 = 16 heads of `d_k=3` and GL24 = 2 heads of `d_k=24`. Trading head count for head width at fixed `d_model` is a Vaswani-parameterized move, not a new dimension complementary to width.
- **Vaswani et al. 2017, Section 3.2.1 (Scaled Dot-Product Attention), and its footnote.** Verbatim: "We suspect that for large values of d_k, the dot products grow large in magnitude, pushing the softmax function into regions where it has extremely small gradients," motivating the `1/sqrt(d_k)` factor from the variance argument ("assume ... independent random variables with mean 0 and variance 1 ... dot product ... has mean 0 and variance d_k"). The project's `tau = kappa*sqrt(d_energy)` with `d_energy = d_head = g` reproduces exactly this `sqrt(d_k)` temperature, confirming `g` plays the role of `d_k` and that block-enlargement silently re-tunes the softmax sharpness.
- **Michel, Levy, Neubig 2019, "Are Sixteen Heads Really Better than One?" NeurIPS (arXiv:1905.10650).** Verbatim: "a large percentage of attention heads can be removed at test time without significantly impacting performance"; "some layers can even be reduced to a single head." Head count is a soft, redundancy-laden knob: dropping heads (GL3's 16 -> GL24's 2) is a documented regime, not evidence of a novel scaling axis. The monotone GL3->GL24 gain co-moves with fewer heads, which the literature says is often free or beneficial.
- **Voita, Talbot, Moiseev, Sennrich, Titov 2019, "Analyzing Multi-Head Self-Attention," ACL (arXiv:1905.09418).** Verbatim: "Our novel pruning method removes the vast majority of heads without seriously affecting performance ... on the English-Russian WMT dataset, pruning 38 out of 48 encoder heads results in a drop of only 0.15 BLEU." Head width and head count trade off; a fixed-`d_model` sweep from many narrow heads to few wide heads walks along that known trade-off curve.
- **Fedus, Zoph, Shazeer 2021, "Switch Transformers" (arXiv:2101.03961); Shazeer et al. 2017, "Outrageously Large Neural Networks" (arXiv:1701.06538).** Verbatim (Switch): "a sparsely-activated model — with outrageous numbers of parameters — but a constant computational cost"; "Mixture of Experts (MoE) ... selects different parameters for each incoming example." Verbatim (Shazeer): "Conditional computation, where parts of the network are active on a per-example basis ... dramatically increasing model capacity without a proportional increase in computation ... greater than 1000x improvements in model capacity with only minor losses in computational efficiency." The `phi_embed (V, n_gen)` table read one row per token (prior_bank.py:682) is exactly a sparse embedding: 99.7% of the 3.62x total-param growth lives there, and "active/tok flat while total grows 3.6x" is the DEFINITION of a sparsely-read table, not an efficiency discovery. Under this canon the claim is legitimate only paired with a compute axis — and the compute axis is adverse (transport FLOPs `~ g^2`, 64x; wall-time U-shaped; GL24 is the slowest run at 11049 s).

## Falsification conditions
This position is wrong on transformer-architecture grounds if:
- **X (head-geometry control).** A standard multi-head baseline at `d_model=48` swept 16 heads x d_head=3 -> 2 heads x d_head=24, WITHOUT any gauge machinery or `phi_embed` growth, shows NO monotone CE improvement. Then narrow-head recovery + head-count reduction is not the driver and something gauge-specific is. (Untrained; unsettled-question 2 in the pack.)
- **Y (non-gauge matched-size control).** A plain `V x m` learned table of matched parameter size, or a matched-param dense head-mix, fails to reproduce the GL24 gain at fixed head geometry — isolating the generator algebra from raw table capacity. (Untrained.)
- **Z (compute-axis survival).** GL24 is Pareto-better on FLOPs or wall-clock at a MATCHED token budget (491.52M), not merely on `active_params_per_token`. Present evidence runs the other way: GL24 is the slowest cell and trained on half the tokens, and transport cost grows `g^2` = 64x.

Absent X, Y, or Z, "gauge-block size" is not separable from `d_k = d_model/h` plus a sparse embedding table, and "distinct scaling axis complementary to width-scaling" is architecturally false — both `blocks_K48` and `grow_K_GL10` live on the same `(h, d_k)` plane, one holding `d_model` fixed and the other growing `h*d_k`.

## Confidence
HIGH — the code sets `d_head = K/n_blocks` and `n_heads = 48/g`, and `attention_tau` self-labels `kappa=1 -> Vaswani recovery` with `d_energy = d_head`, so the block-size = `d_k` identification is code-truth, not inference. I would shift toward MEDIUM only if a fixed-head-geometry control (condition X or Y) isolated a gauge-specific gain, or if the efficiency claim survived on a matched-token compute axis (condition Z).
