# Memo — debate-expert-ml-engineer — red — rebuttal — blocks-k48-gauge-block-scaling-axis

## Lens
General ML / optimization — Adam/AdamW, initialization, scaling laws, LR schedules, regularization, mixed precision, K-FAC / natural-gradient practice, training stability. Here the operative sub-lens is scaling-law identification: what a "scaling axis complementary to width" must satisfy under Kaplan (2020) and Hoffmann (Chinchilla, 2022), and what a fixed-token, fixed-active-compute ablation can and cannot establish.

## Steelman of the opposing position
Within a single 245.76M-token budget the cross-entropy falls strictly and seed-stably as the gauge block enlarges while the per-token working set barely moves, so blocks is at minimum a real, previously-unpublished structural knob that lowers loss without touching the active parameter set — a legitimate access-pattern property in the Shazeer/Fedus total-vs-active tradition.

## My position (in service of red)
The only conjunct Blue still defends that the claim actually needs — "a distinct SCALING knob COMPLEMENTARY to width-scaling (grow_K_GL10)" — is a cross-sweep scaling-law assertion, and it is non-identified on ML-engineering grounds: (i) every "complementary to width" placement compares blocks (D = 245.76M tokens) against width (D = 491.52M, exactly 2x), and Chinchilla's loss is additive in a term B/D^beta, so a cross-sweep loss/floor comparison across a 2x D gap has no identified answer; (ii) "scaling axis" means a law/trend, but Blue has already conceded the exponent is non-reportable, and the only x-axis on which blocks moves 3.62x (n_params) is the vocab/embedding-inflated axis Kaplan explicitly removes to get a clean law — on the Kaplan-correct non-embedding/active axis blocks is flat (+0.008%), i.e. a fixed-compute ablation, not a scaling axis; (iii) the Switch/Fedus "separate axis" is legitimate only at constant compute, and blocks buys its best loss (GL24) at the highest wall-clock (11049s, the slowest run) with transport compute up 64x, the opposite of constant-compute. The within-sweep monotone CE descent is real; it does not establish an efficient or complementary scaling axis.

## Evidence

- **Hoffmann et al. 2022 (Chinchilla, arXiv:2203.15556), §3.3 Eq. 2 and App. D.2 Eq. 10.** The fitted loss is `L̂(N,D) ≜ E + A/N^α + B/D^β` with `E = 1.69, A = 406.4, B = 410.7, α = 0.34, β = 0.28`. The `B/D^β` term is additive and does not vanish: moving from D = 491.52M to D = 245.76M (halving D) shifts the loss surface by `B(D^{-β} − (2D)^{-β}) = 410.7·D^{-0.28}(1 − 2^{-0.28})`, a positive, N-independent offset. Any statement that blocks sits "complementary to" or "favorably against" the width frontier compares two surfaces offset by exactly this term. The comparison is not identified without holding D fixed.

- **Hoffmann et al. 2022, abstract / §3.4.** "for every doubling of model size the number of training tokens should also be doubled"; "model size and the number of training tokens should be scaled in equal proportions." N and D are co-equal loss determinants; a sweep that varies structure at fixed D and then is set beside a second sweep at 2x D has confounded the two axes the paper says must move together. The matched-D (491.52M) blocks run does not exist, so the "complementary to width" placement rests on no identified data point — including Blue's own "suggestive" GL24 (CE 4.523) vs grow K50 (CE 4.544), which Blue concedes is "on half the tokens."

- **Kaplan et al. 2020 (arXiv:2001.08361), §1.3 Notation and §3.2.** "N – the number of model parameters, excluding all vocabulary and positional embeddings." And: "When we exclude embedding parameters, the performance of models with different depths converge to a single trend." The clean parameter power law is defined on the non-embedding count precisely because vocab/embedding tables do not scale like compute. `phi_embed` is `(V, n_gen)` = `(50257, n_gen)`, read one row per token — structurally the vocabulary/embedding table Kaplan removes. So n_params (which carries 99.7% of blocks' 3.62x growth inside `phi_embed`) is the exact axis Kaplan excludes; the Kaplan-correct axis (non-embedding / active) moves +1,008 params (+0.008%) across the whole sweep. A curve that is flat on the correct axis and 3.62x-wide only on the excluded axis is not a scaling axis.

- **Kaplan et al. 2020, §1.1.** "Performance improves predictably as long as we scale up N and D in tandem, but enters a regime of diminishing returns if either N or D is held fixed while the other increases." The blocks sweep holds D fixed at 245.76M and varies structure — by Kaplan's own statement this is a fixed-D slice, not a scaling law, and its slope is a data-conditioned artifact of that slice (consistent with Blue's conceded 0.929 vs n_params, CI [0.07, 1.73]; 0.181 vs n_gen; R² = 0.17 vs FLOPs — a 5.15x swing over a <1-decade x-range).

- **Fedus, Zoph & Shazeer 2021 (Switch Transformer, arXiv:2101.03961), abstract.** "The result is a sparsely-activated model — with outrageous numbers of parameters — but a constant computational cost," and "up to 7x increases in pre-training speed with the same computational resources." The total-vs-active axis is a real efficiency axis only at CONSTANT compute. Blocks violates the antecedent: its best-loss point GL24 is the slowest run (11049s, 2.37x the GL8 minimum of 4657s), wall-time is U-shaped, and the real transport sub-term `2·N·g²` grows 64x (g² : 9 → 576). Blocks buys its headline loss reduction at rising, not constant, compute — so the Switch analogy that licenses the access-pattern reading does not license an efficiency or complementarity claim.

## Falsification conditions

None of the three has been run.
