## False dichotomies / equivocations detected

The consequential finding: the equivocations live in **the claim as written**, and both debaters correctly surfaced and de-conflated them rather than committing them. That is what makes this a healthy REMAND rather than a contest.

- **E1 — the three-way parameter conflation (core).** The claim (`00_claim.md:13`) fuses three separate propositions into one phrase, "parameter-efficient scaling axis ... rather than a metric artifact": (i) `active_params_per_token` is flat — a **definitional identity** (`active = 5·V·K + 2·K + n_gen`; only `+n_gen` varies, +1,008 total = +0.008%); (ii) the model is **parameter-efficient** — a **compute claim**, which requires a flat compute axis and is adverse here (wall-time U-shaped, transport FLOPs 64×, worse-than-width at matched params); (iii) **gauge structure caused** the gain — a **causal claim**, unidentified (99.7% of the 3.62× growth sits in an uncontrolled token-indexed `phi_embed` table). (i) is true-but-empty, (ii) is unsupported/adverse, (iii) is unidentified. Neither debater conflated them: blue flagged (i)→(ii) as circular and refused it; red separated all three in section 1.
- **E2 — "scaling axis" equivocation.** The phrase trades on two readings: a **scaling law with a reportable exponent** (ill-posed here: axis-dependent, CI crossing zero, under two decades of range) and a **structural/design knob** (true: a genuinely new fixed-`embed_dim` block-partition direction absent from both manuscripts). The compound rides both readings at once.
- **E3 — novelty-for-efficiency.** "Previously unpublished / structurally distinct" (a fact about the literature and the code) is silently upgraded to "parameter-efficient scaling axis" (a fact about the compute-accuracy trade). Red names this in rebuttal; a knob can be new and inefficient simultaneously.
- **E4 — within-sweep vs. cross-sweep scope leakage.** "Genuine effect" is a **within-sweep** proposition (all five cells on the same 245.76M tokens, same `data_sha256`, no D-confound). "Complementary to width-scaling" is a **cross-sweep** proposition (against `grow_K_GL10` at 491.52M, exactly 2× tokens), non-identified under Chinchilla `L(N,D)`. The claim bundles a confound-free proposition with a confounded one. Both sides agree the within-sweep effect is clean and the cross-sweep comparison is not identified.

## Scope leakage detected

- The empirical effect is settled **inside** its scope (within-sweep, fixed budget) and is corroborated. The "complementary to width" and "parameter-efficient over width" conjuncts reach **outside** that scope to a different machine (491.52M tokens, different batch size) that was never run at the block axis — the Cartwright nomological-machine over-export both philosophy memos flag.
- The neutral evidence pack itself pre-partitions the claim: its "What this evidence does NOT settle" section (`01_evidence.md:77-83`) enumerates exactly the conjuncts that fail (matched-budget survival, gauge-vs-raw-capacity attribution, the right x-axis, the framing question). The scope boundary is drawn in the record before either side argued.

## My verdict reasoning

The claim is a five-fold conjunction. As a conjunction it is false: "parameter-efficient" is unsupported and partially self-falsifies (worse than width at matched total n_params; wall-time U-shaped with the best-loss point the slowest run); the "publishable exponent" is ill-posed (axis-dependent, CI [0.07, 1.73] over a compressed 3.62× range under the two-decade credibility floor); the "flat working set" that anchors "parameter-efficient / not an artifact" is a definitional identity, not a measurement; and the causal attribution to gauge structure is a three-knob confound (`n_params` 3.62×, `n_heads` 16→2, block width `g`) with the single discriminating control — a non-gauge `V×m` matched-size table — never trained. Yet a real, sharply-falsifiable, seed-robust sub-claim survives every attack untouched, on a fixed budget with no internal D-confound, and it is genuinely new to both manuscripts. This is neither RED_WINS (the surviving phenomenon is real and the claim author explicitly asked for REMAND in exactly this evidentiary state) nor BLUE_WINS (the conjunction as written is false and blue conceded it). It is not OUT_OF_SCOPE: the claim is answerable — it decomposes cleanly into one supported sub-claim and three unsupported/ill-posed conjuncts; only one conjunct (the exponent) is individually unfalsifiable, and a claim with one ill-posed conjunct among four answerable ones is decomposed, not discarded. Both coordinators, both philosophy-of-science memos, and the claim document itself converge here. REMAND is the correctly-framed disposition, and it is non-evasive: the verdict is clear, and its clarity is a decomposition.

## Decisive evidence

`01_evidence.md:77-83` — the "What this evidence does NOT settle" section — is the frame-breaking observation. The neutral pack, before either side argued, records that (1) matched-budget survival is untested, (2) gauge-structure-per-se vs. raw `V×n_gen` capacity is uncontrolled, (3) "parameter-efficient scaling axis" vs. "gauge-structure ablation at fixed width" is an open framing question, and (4) the x-axis for any efficiency claim is undetermined. Paired with the code identity `active = 5·V·K + 2·K + n_gen` (only `+n_gen` varies, +0.008%; `run_artifacts.py:616-620`) and the adverse compute facts (transport 64×, wall-time U-shaped with GL24 slowest, +4.8 to +12.8 PPL worse than width at matched n_params), this shows the single sentence packs one settled proposition and three unsettled ones. One claim, four propositions, three unresolved — the definition of a REMAND.
