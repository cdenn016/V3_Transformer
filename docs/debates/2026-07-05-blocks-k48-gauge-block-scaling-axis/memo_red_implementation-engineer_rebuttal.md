## External citations (source-of-truth anchors for the framing)
- **Williams, Waterman & Patterson 2009, "Roofline: An Insightful Visual Performance Model," CACM 52(4):65-76.** Performance is capped by `min(peak FLOP/s, arithmetic_intensity * peak bandwidth)`; a kernel below the ridge point is memory-bandwidth bound and cannot be characterized by a FLOP count alone. Full-vocabulary decode (`2*V*K` weight reads, low reuse) sits in that bandwidth-bound regime, which is why `est_flops_analytic` saturates on `2VK` and why measured `wall_time_s`, not the analytic FLOP proxy, is the ground-truth compute signal — and that signal is U-shaped with GL24 slowest.
- **Fedus, Zoph & Shazeer 2021, "Switch Transformers," JMLR (arXiv:2101.03961), Sec. 1.** They "investigate a fourth axis: increase the parameter count while keeping the floating point operations (FLOPs) per example constant." The total-vs-active efficiency claim is licensed ONLY when a genuine compute axis (FLOPs/wall-clock per example) is held fixed. Here per-token transport FLOPs (`2N*g^2`) grow 64x and wall-time grows/U-shapes, so the flat-`active/token` framing fails Fedus's own precondition.
- (Supporting) **Kaplan et al. 2020, arXiv:2001.08361.** The clean parameter power law is fit on the NON-embedding count precisely because vocab/embedding tables do not scale like compute — so `phi_embed (V, n_gen)` belongs in the excluded bucket, and a 3.62x-range `n_params` fit over a vocab-table-dominated axis is not a physical exponent.
