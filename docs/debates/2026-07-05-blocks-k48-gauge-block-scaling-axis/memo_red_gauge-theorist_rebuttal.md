## My position (in service of red)
Blue's Pillar 3 identification — "the 124.6 -> 92.2 gain is *structured gauge* capacity" — is not established on gauge-theoretic grounds. On the resolved path (`transport_mode='flat'`, `n_layers=1`, `n_e_steps=1`) the transport `Omega_ij = exp(phi_i.G)exp(-phi_j.G)` is a coboundary of the 0-cochain `g_i = exp(phi_i.G)`: it satisfies `Omega_ij Omega_jk = Omega_ik` automatically, every loop holonomy is the identity, and the curvature is identically zero (Nakahara §10.2-10.3). A flat, zero-holonomy connection carries no gauge-field content beyond a *per-token learned frame* `exp(phi_i.G) in GL+(48)`, whose coordinate width is `n_gen`. Within `block_glk`, `n_gen` **is** the structure-group dimension **and** the width of the `(V, n_gen)` `phi_embed` table (`prior_bank.py:167`), so "enlarge the group" and "widen the learned per-token table" are the *same* manipulation — dissociable only by the un-run `tied_block_glk` (`n_gen = g^2`) or a non-gauge `V x m` control. The gain is therefore confounded with raw added table capacity, not isolated to gauge structure.

## Evidence
- **Cohen & Welling 2016 (ICML), abstract, verbatim:** "G-convolutions increase the expressive capacity of the network without increasing the number of parameters"; "G-CNNs use G-convolutions ... that enjoys a substantially higher degree of weight sharing than regular convolution layers." Blue's own canon makes the group a **fixed-parameter, weight-*sharing*** axis. `blocks_K48` does the opposite: total params grow **3.62x** as the group enlarges (99.7% of the growth in `phi_embed`). The precedent for "group is a capacity axis orthogonal to width" is precisely the fixed-parameter property this sweep lacks; the analogy fails at the parameter-matching that makes it a clean structural axis.
- **Kondor & Trivedi 2018 (ICML), abstract, verbatim:** "convolutional structure is not just a sufficient, but also a necessary condition for equivariance to the action of a compact group." Equivariance is a *constraint* that **reduces** the admissible-map space (weight sharing), not free added capacity. Invoking it to license a 3.62x parameter inflation inverts the theorem's direction.
- **Weiler & Cesa 2019 (NeurIPS), abstract, verbatim:** "constraints for arbitrary group representations can be reduced to constraints under irreducible representations." Again the group enters as a *kernel-space constraint*, i.e. a restriction, not a capacity donor.
- **Nakahara, *Geometry, Topology and Physics* (2nd ed., 2003), §10.2 (Holonomy), §10.3 (Curvature):** a connection is flat iff its curvature two-form vanishes iff the holonomy of every contractible loop is the identity; curvature is the local density whose integral is holonomy (Ambrose-Singer). Regime-I flat transport is a coboundary, so its holonomy group is trivial and no curvature distinguishes it from a per-token frame choice. The cocycle identity holding "automatically" is the signature of a **trivial (coboundary) cocycle**, not of rich structure.
- **Code truth (confirmed, GL24 `config.json`):** `gauge_group='block_glk'`, `transport_mode='flat'`, `use_prior_bank=False`, `use_head_mixer=True`, `n_layers=1`, `n_e_steps=1`, plus co-moving learned tables `pos_phi='learned'`, `decode_bias=True`, `learnable_r=True`/`r_update_mode='gradient'`. Untied `generate_glk_multihead` returns `(n_heads*g^2, K, K)` -> `n_gen = 48*g` (144 -> 1152); tied `generate_glk_multihead_tied` returns `(g^2, K, K)` -> `n_gen = g^2` (9 -> 576). Tied GL24 (`n_gen=576`) equals untied GL12 (`n_gen=576`): a matched-`n_gen` tied-vs-untied control is runnable with the *existing* generators and was not run. Per CLAUDE.md exceptions (2), the head mixer breaks strict gauge equivariance off identity-init under the untied gauge (it shrinks 16x16 -> 2x2), and `use_prior_bank=False` composes decode as `logits = mu @ (WM)^T`, a learned linear map — so the operation credited as "gauge structure" is a **non-equivariant learned-linear composite**, not the strictly-equivariant KL-decode path.
- **Gauge fixing:** the consecutive-`g`-block partition of R^48 is an implicitly fixed basis/partition gauge; the residual group is `GL(g)^{48/g}` semidirect block-permutations. The head mixer acts in the head-index coset that the gauge-fixing discarded — it is the direct symptom that the untied product-group structure is not being preserved by the layer, contradicting the "structured gauge capacity" reading.
