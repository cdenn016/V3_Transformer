# Memo — debate-expert-philosophy-of-science — red — rebuttal — blocks-k48-gauge-block-scaling-axis

## Lens
Philosophy of science — falsifiability, scope, theory-ladenness, confirmation bias, progressive vs. degenerative research programmes, novel-construction-vs-rediscovery, manuscript-as-authority circularity.

## Frame check
The load-bearing proposition is a conjunction the claim document itself fixes: "genuine AND publishable AND parameter-efficient AND a distinct scaling axis, NOT artifact+confound." A conjunction is false when any conjunct is false. Blue's opening concedes two conjuncts outright — the publishable exponent (axis-dependent, CI [0.07, 1.73] spanning zero) and parameter-efficiency-as-dominance (worse than width by +4.8 to +12.8 PPL at matched total `n_params`). The compound claim as stated is therefore already refuted; what Blue then defends is a different, narrower proposition. Holding Blue to its own word: it calls the outcome a REMAND. A REMAND on a conjunction is a refutation of the conjunction, not a BLUE_WINS. That is the red verdict, stated in Blue's own vocabulary.

Two equivocations keep the corpse warm. First, novelty-for-efficiency. Pillar 3 establishes only that the fixed-`embed_dim` block axis is *previously unpublished* and *structurally distinct*. "Previously unpublished" is a fact about the literature; "parameter-efficient scaling axis" is a fact about the model's compute-accuracy trade. These are different predicates. Blue's own conceded evidence shows the axis is *less* efficient than width (U-shaped wall-time, GL24 the slowest run; real per-token transport compute grows 64x, `g^2` 9 to 576). A knob can be brand new and simultaneously inefficient. The compound claim converts novelty into efficiency by juxtaposition; the inference does not hold.

Second, and load-bearing: the causal attribution in Pillar 3 — "the gain is *structured gauge* capacity, not raw capacity" — is a confounded manipulation. The block sweep moves three variables in lockstep: total `n_params` (3.62x), `n_heads` (16 to 2), and block width `g` (which sets `n_gen = 48g`, so `phi_embed` grows 8x, 144 to 1152). Mill's Method of Difference and Woodward's interventionism both require that the manipulated cause be varied *alone*. It is not. Blue's head-mixer rebuttal (mixer shrinks 16x16 to 2x2, opposite to the gain) validly eliminates *one* alternative, but leaves the dominant one — raw added table capacity in `phi_embed` — entirely uncontrolled. Blue's own Falsification Condition 2 (a plain `V x m` non-gauge table at `m = n_gen`) is the single-difference control that would identify the mechanism, and it has not been run. Until it is, "gauge structure caused the gain" is unidentified: a confound, not a demonstrated mechanism.

The equivariance canon Blue leans on to sell "structured, not raw" cuts against Blue here. Cohen and Welling 2016 raise accuracy by enlarging the group *at fixed parameter count* via weight sharing; that is exactly the disanalogy — here the group enlargement is accompanied by 3.62x more parameters, so the very confound those papers hold fixed is the confound this sweep lets float. The published warrant for "group richness is capacity orthogonal to width" is a fixed-param warrant; it does not transfer to a 3.62x-param sweep.

Circularity flag: the reading of the extra parameters *as* "structured gauge capacity" (Lie-algebra coordinates in `transport.py`) is drawn from the project's own construction. Using that framework-internal labeling to argue the axis is structurally distinct — hence the gain is structural rather than generic capacity — borrows the conclusion from the framework's naming. Whether the mechanism is gauge-structural or generic added capacity is precisely the open empirical question (Condition 2). The manuscript's interpretation of the parameters is not independent evidence about their causal role.

## Steelman of the opposing position
Stripped of exponent and efficiency, a seed-robust (std ≤ 1.08), strictly monotone CE descent (124.6 to 92.2) along a previously-unpublished fixed-`embed_dim` gauge-block axis, all cells on identical 245.76M tokens, is a genuine reportable empirical phenomenon that the published width-axis work neither contains nor predicts.

## My position (in service of red)
The compound claim "genuine, publishable, parameter-efficient, distinct scaling axis" is refuted as a conjunction, because two conjuncts are conceded false and the surviving "distinct axis" conjunct establishes only novelty while the causal attribution to gauge structure is a three-knob confound with no single-difference control; the only proposition the evidence supports is the demoted observation "a robust monotone CE curve exists along a new block partition axis" — which is a REMAND, not a BLUE_WINS.

## Evidence
- **Mill, *A System of Logic* (1843 [1882], bk. III, ch. 8, p. 483):** "If an instance in which the phenomenon under investigation occurs, and an instance in which it does not occur, have every circumstance in common save one, that one occurring only in the former, the circumstance in which alone the two instances differ, is the effect, or the cause, or an indispensable part of the cause, of the phenomenon." The block sweep differs in three circumstances (`n_params`, `n_heads`, `g`), so the single-difference precondition for the causal inference "gauge structure caused the gain" is not met.
- **Woodward, *Making Things Happen* (2003):** X is a direct cause of Y "iff there be a possible intervention on X that will change Y ... when all other variables are held fixed"; "an intervention is a manipulation of the cause — and only of the cause." The sweep is not an intervention on gauge structure alone; the other variables are not held fixed. The mechanism claim fails the interventionist test.
- **Lakatos, *The Methodology of Scientific Research Programmes* (1978, pp. 33-34):** a theory is theoretically progressive only if "it predicts some novel, hitherto unexpected fact," and empirically progressive only if "some of this excess empirical content is also corroborated." The block-axis result was discovered and then narrated, not predicted from the gauge hard core; and its "excess content" is not corroborated *as content of the gauge programme* until the non-gauge control shows a plain table cannot reproduce it. Blue's Pillar 1 invocation of "excess empirical content" misapplies the criterion — the corroborated fact so far is consistent with "any added table," not specifically with the gauge core.
- **Hacking, *Representing and Intervening* (1983):** experiment "has a life of its own, independent of theory." Correctly applied, this protects the *observation* (the CE descent is robust however plotted) and by the same cut denies the *interpretation*: robustness of a theory-free regularity gives no warrant for the theory-laden readings ("efficient," "scaling axis," "structured gauge"). Blue cites Hacking to shield the effect; the same principle strips the interpretive layer the compound claim asserts.
- **Duhem, *The Aim and Structure of Physical Theory* (1906, p. 187):** "the physicist can never subject an isolated hypothesis to experimental test, but only a whole group of hypotheses ... the experiment does not designate which one should be changed." The improvement is entangled with two opt-in ablation toggles (`use_prior_bank=False`, `use_head_mixer=True`) plus three co-varying knobs; the CE drop does not designate the gauge block as the locus.
- **Cartwright, *How the Laws of Physics Lie* (1983):** phenomenological laws can be true where the covering fundamental law over-reaches. The CE-vs-`g` curve is a true phenomenological regularity; "parameter-efficient scaling axis" is the over-general covering-law dress it cannot yet wear. In *The Dappled World* (1999, p. 50) terms, a five-point, single-layer, single-E-step, two-ablation-toggle arrangement is a shielded nomological machine, not a license for a law-like "scaling axis."

## Falsifiability assessment of the claim
The compound claim is falsifiable in principle — Blue lists five falsifiers, which is to its credit. The decisive point is that the two discriminating tests have not been run. (1) The single-difference control (Condition 2): a plain `V x m` learned table with `m = n_gen`, no gauge action; if it reproduces 124.6 to 92.2 at matched added params, the gain is generic capacity and the "structured gauge axis" conjunct is refuted. (2) The matched-token run (Condition 1): retrain at 491.52M tokens; if the descent collapses into seed noise once D matches `grow_K_GL10`, the "genuine new axis" reading is a data-budget artifact. Because neither discriminating test exists, the mechanism conjunct ("gauge structure caused it") and the efficiency conjunct are neither corroborated nor refuted — they are *unidentified*. The finding: the current evidence base cannot distinguish the compound claim from the confound hypothesis, so presenting the claim as established is premature; only the demoted observation survives.
