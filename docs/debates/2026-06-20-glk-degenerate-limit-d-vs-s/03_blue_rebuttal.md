## Core attack — Red's own authority refutes Red's reduction/correspondence dichotomy

Red's entire S-tier case rests on one premise: that a derived limit must be *exact, parameter-free, and direction-asymmetric with the structure forced*, so any free parameter (the isotropy class), any approximate step (key-norm constancy), or any unexecuted limit (the σ-absorption) demotes the relation from reduction (D) to correspondence (S). Red sources that premise to Crowther 2018 (arXiv:1712.00473), E2 and E3.

Crowther 2018 says the opposite. Verified against the full text (ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/1712.00473):

1. **Reduction is a special case of correspondence, not its alternative.** Crowther, p. 5: "reduction (henceforth taken to mean reduction₂ as an approach to reduction₁) is also a special case of correspondence." Red's framing — "compatibility + symbol bridge without forced deducibility is correspondence (S), *not* reduction (D)" (E2) — treats correspondence and reduction as disjoint bins. They are not disjoint in the cited source. Every reduction *is* a correspondence; the discriminating question is not "compatibility vs. deducibility" but whether the stronger limiting/deductive structure is present *on top of* the compatibility. Red's E2 establishes only the genus (correspondence) and then asserts the species (reduction) is absent — without the genus excluding the species, which it cannot, because the genus contains it.

2. **Reduction₂ explicitly tolerates approximation and idealization.** Crowther, p. 5: "the correspondence relations of reduction₂ may be interpreted as imperfect attempts toward demonstrating that O is deducible from N." Verified summary of the source: "The paper does not require reduction to be exact or parameter-free ... it treats limiting relations with idealizations as legitimate reduction, not demoted to mere correspondence." The manuscript's one approximate step — key-norm constancy, flagged with `≈` (attn:1358) — is precisely the "imperfect attempt" Crowther licenses. Red's E3 demands "the special structure is *forced* by the limit" with nothing approximate; Crowther's own text does not.

3. **Reduction₂ recovers theory *parts*, in the reduced theory's *own* domain, in the M→L direction.** Crowther, p. 4: "Reduction₂ holds that the more-fundamental theory M reduces to the less-fundamental theory L in the domains where L is known to be successful." Crowther, p. 5: "it is not the entirety of theory L that will be recovered ... M and L usually stand for theory parts." This dismantles Red's direction-of-fit objection (E2/E3, "backwards"). The licensed direction *is* general→special, recovered in the special theory's domain. The gauge framework (M, more general) recovers standard attention (L) in attention's domain. Recovering the *bilinear-form part and the temperature part* — not the rectangular subspace selection (the manuscript explicitly cedes that to S at attn:1284, 1705) — is exactly "M and L stand for theory parts." Red cited the canon that defines this as proper reduction₂ and then read it as the signature of mere correspondence.

The standard reduction literature agrees on the parameter point Red needs to deny. The SEP entry on scientific reduction (verified) records that "Putnam (1965), Nagel (1970), Schaffner (1993) and Endicott (1998) have argued that augmentation and correction can be achieved by approximation and introduction of boundary conditions for bridge-laws." A bridge law carrying a free constant or a boundary condition is the *normal* form of the relation, not a disqualifier.

This is the decisive blow against the pure-S position: **a derived limit routinely retains free parameters and bridge constants in the reduced theory.** `c → ∞` recovers Newtonian mechanics — which still has free masses and free forces. Setting `v² → 0` does not delete `m` from `½mv²`; it leaves `m` free as the parameter of the *reduced* theory. The isotropy-class freedom in D♯ ("the lift fixed only up to right multiplication by the isotropy group," attn:1744) is structurally identical: the limit forces the *form* `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` into the unique cross-term slot (attn:1224, the only μ_i–μ_j coupling surviving the isotropic+flat collapse), and leaves a free representative within an equivalence class — the bilinear's value, fixed up to the SVD lift. A reduction that forces the form and leaves a free orbit-representative is a reduction with a free parameter, which is the ordinary case, not a downgrade to analogy.

## Defense — the form and the coefficient are forced, not fitted; vacuity-over-GL is completeness, not contentlessness

**Direction of fit (Red's first and strongest objection).** Red says the framework "tunes a free general parameter to match an already-learned object" and quotes "change of parameterization" (attn:1358) and "parametrize this combined quantity directly" (attn:1286). Read the surrounding derivation, not the absorption sentence in isolation. The cross-term coefficient is not chosen to match `W_Q W_K^T`. It is what survives the two stated idealizations applied to a *fixed analytic object* — the Gaussian KL. Imposing `Σ = σ²I` (isotropy) and `Ω_ij = Ω` (flat connection) on `D_KL(q_i ‖ Ω q_j)` collapses it to `(1/2σ²)‖Ω^{-1}μ_i − μ_j‖²` (attn:1198), and expanding the square leaves exactly one query–key cross term: `−σ^{-2} μ_i^T Ω^{-T} μ_j` (attn:1204, 1224). No `W_Q W_K^T` is consulted to produce that coefficient. The coefficient is *output by the limit*. The identification at attn:1268-1269 then *names* the forced coefficient `W_Q W_K^T`. The order of operations is limit-then-name, not target-then-fit. The σ-absorption sentence Red weaponizes (attn:1286) is doing something weaker than Red claims: it observes that `σ^{-2}` and `Ω^{-T}` only ever appear multiplied, so the dimensionless dot-product form does not require literally sending `σ → 0` — the same way the Newtonian limit does not require the universe's actual `c` to become infinite, only that the relevant ratio be negligible. An idealization that need not be physically executed to license the reduced form is the textbook structure of limiting reduction (Crowther p. 5, "imperfect attempts ... deducible"), not a disguised curve-fit.

The contrast Red itself draws cuts for me. Red concedes the softmax-from-KL row (attn:1700) and the `√d_k` temperature row (attn:1717) are forced D, because "the form follows from the variational principle ... with nothing fitted" (E3). The temperature row's exact wording is "τ = √d_k (dot-product form; σ^{-2} absorbed)" — it carries the *same* σ-absorption Red calls fitting in the `W_Q W_K^T` row, and Red still grants it D. The cross-term coefficient `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` is produced by the same algebra (attn:1204, 1224) and the same absorption as the temperature Red concedes is forced. Red cannot grant D to the temperature that rides the σ-absorption and deny derived-status to the bilinear that rides the identical absorption. The two are the same step. The honest split the manuscript already drew — D for the temperature, D♯ for the bilinear — differs only because the bilinear, unlike the scalar temperature, carries the matrix-valued isotropy orbit. That orbit is what the sharp records. It is a free parameter of the reduced theory, not evidence of fitting.

**Vacuity over GL (Red's second objection).** Red reads "the factorization always exists for any invertible Ω" (attn:1273) and "M_ij can be realized as any element of GL(d_k)" (attn:1183) as "constrains nothing, therefore re-reading." This conflates *completeness of the reduction* with *contentlessness*. "Every standard-attention head's invertible head-space factor is reached by some gauge Ω" is the statement that the reduction is *surjective onto its target* — it covers all standard-attention instances. A reduction that recovered only *some* attention heads would be a *weaker* result, not a stronger one. Crowther p. 4: reduction₂ holds "in the domains where L is known to be successful" — covering all of L's successful instances is the goal, not a defect. The free parameters of a reduced theory are *supposed* to range over the reduced theory's full instance space: Newtonian mechanics derived as a limit covers *every* Newtonian mass and force, and no one calls the `c → ∞` reduction vacuous because it "works for any mass." "Holds for every instance of the target" is the success condition of a reduction, the failure condition of a *prediction of a unique instance*. The claim under debate is the former (standard attention is a degenerate case), not the latter (the framework predicts one specific learned bilinear). The manuscript never claims to predict the specific learned `W_Q W_K^T`; it claims standard attention is recovered as a case. Surjectivity establishes that claim; it does not refute it.

What *would* make the identification vacuous is if `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` could be matched to a bilinear that standard attention can produce but the gauge structure cannot, or vice versa — a mismatch of expressive ranges. There is none: both sides realize exactly `GL(d_head)` per pair (attn:1183), and the manuscript fences the residue that is *not* covered (rectangular subspace selection → S, attn:1284). The reduction maps the derivable part onto the derivable part and labels the rest S. That is a calibrated reduction, not a vacuous re-reading.

**Why this clears D♯ and not S.** S in this table means "explains the role but does not uniquely predict the specific form" (attn:1744) — the ALiBi/T5 demotions fit S because the framework supplies the additive-log-prior *slot* but not the decaying-distance *form* (ledger §2; the bias function is free, unconstrained, any function of offset). The `W_Q W_K^T` row is categorically different: the framework supplies the *specific functional form* — a bilinear in the means with coefficient `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}`, a √d_k temperature, a softmax — all forced by the KL and the two limits. What it leaves free is one orbit representative inside `GL(d_head)`, not the functional form. "Form forced, representative free" is D♯. "Form free, slot only" is S. The ALiBi row is genuinely the latter; the `W_Q W_K^T` row is genuinely the former. Red's E4 consistency argument ("same demotion criterion") fails on this distinction: ALiBi's free object is the *form*; the bilinear's free object is a *representative of a forced form*. The criterion that demoted ALiBi does not reach the bilinear.

## Revised verdict and falsification conditions

The defensible claim, corrected: standard attention is a genuine derived degenerate limit of gauge-theoretic variational inference **up to the isotropy-class equivalence — D♯, in the derived family, not S**. The form (bilinear-in-means, σ^{-2}Ω^{-T} coefficient, √d_k temperature, softmax) is forced by the isotropic-covariance and flat-connection limits applied to the fixed Gaussian KL; the residual freedom is a free orbit-representative inside GL(d_head), which is the ordinary signature of a limiting-case reduction with a free parameter, sanctioned as reduction (not demoted to correspondence) by Red's own cited authority (Crowther 2018, pp. 4–5) and by the standard reduction literature (SEP; Nagel/Schaffner/Putnam on approximation in bridge laws).

This defense fails, and the row collapses to S, if:

1. **Form not forced.** Someone shows the isotropic+flat collapse of `D_KL(q_i ‖ Ω q_j)` admits a query–key cross term *other than* `−σ^{-2} μ_i^T Ω^{-T} μ_j` — i.e. the coefficient is a modeling choice, not the unique algebraic survivor. I verified it is the unique survivor (attn:1198→1204); if that expansion is wrong, I lose.

2. **The orbit freedom is not the reduced theory's free parameter but an unforced form.** Someone shows the isotropy-class freedom in D♯ ranges over *functional forms* (as ALiBi's bias does), not over representatives of a single forced bilinear form. The caption (attn:1744) restricts it to "right multiplication by the isotropy group" — an orbit within GL(d_head), not a family of forms — so I hold this is settled for me; but if the isotropy quotient is shown to alter the logit's functional dependence on the means, S is correct.

3. **Crowther actually requires exactness/parameter-freedom.** If the cited text, on fuller reading, conditions reduction₂ on an exact, parameter-free, fully-executed limit (contradicting p. 5's "imperfect attempts" and "special case of correspondence"), then my core attack collapses and Red's dichotomy stands. I quoted p. 5 directly against this; the burden is now Red's to produce the disqualifying passage.

4. **Bare-D is the only acceptable reading.** If the judge holds the claim must be read as *bare* D and D♯ is inadmissible as a defense, I have already conceded that — the claim as literally worded overstates. The substantive question is whether the row is *derived-family* (D♯) or *structural* (S). On that question, the evidence and Red's own canon place it in the derived family.

I do not defend bare D. I defend D♯, and on the live question — derived-family or structural — the pure-S verdict is refuted by the authority Red brought to argue it.
