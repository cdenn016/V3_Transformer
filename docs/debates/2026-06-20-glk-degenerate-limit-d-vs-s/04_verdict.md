# Verdict — glk-degenerate-limit-d-vs-s

**Phase:** 4 (judgment)
**Claim under evaluation:** The identification `W_Q W_K^T = σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` (full column rank / invertible head-space factor) makes standard transformer attention a genuine DERIVED degenerate limit (D-tier) of gauge-theoretic variational inference, not a structural analogy (S-tier).

## Outcome

**BLUE_WINS** — on the substantive disjunction the debate actually contests (derived-family vs structural), with the wording calibrated to D♯ rather than bare D.

The claim as literally worded ("earns its D-tier") overstates by one diacritic, and both teams concede that. But the live question both teams agreed to fight over is whether the row belongs in the *derived family* (D♯) or is *structural* (S). On that question Blue prevails: the external canon Red itself introduced (Crowther 2018) refutes Red's reduction-vs-correspondence dichotomy and licenses every property Red treats as disqualifying. Red's position is "S, or D♯-collapses-to-S"; that position is defeated on the primary source. This is not a split-the-difference verdict: one side's external canon, read at the source, contradicts the other side's central inference.

## Decisive evidence

External citations weighted 3x over in-repo material, per protocol.

**[DECISIVE — Crowther 2018, arXiv:1712.00473, verified at the primary source by the judge]** Red staked its entire S-tier case on Crowther's reduction/correspondence distinction (Red opening E2, E3; Red rebuttal core). The source, read in full (ar5iv full text, judge-verified), refutes Red's reading on four counts:

1. "reduction (henceforth taken to mean reduction₂ as an approach to reduction₁) is **also a special case of correspondence**." Confirmed verbatim in the arXiv abstract itself and the full text. Red's E2 inference — "compatibility + symbol bridge without forced deducibility is correspondence (S), **not** reduction (D)" — treats correspondence and reduction as disjoint bins. The source denies disjointness explicitly. Establishing the genus (correspondence) does not exclude the species (reduction); the genus contains it. Red's argument is therefore a non-sequitur at its load-bearing joint.

2. "The correspondence relations, including the variety of **limiting and approximation procedures**, of reduction₂ may be interpreted as **imperfect attempts** toward demonstrating that O is deducible from N." Reduction₂ tolerates approximation and idealization. Red's E3 demands the limit be exact, parameter-free, and the structure "forced by the limit" with nothing approximate. Crowther's own text does not require that. The manuscript's one approximate step — key-norm constancy, flagged with `≈` at attn:1358 — is precisely the licensed "imperfect attempt."

3. "Reduction₂ holds that the **more-fundamental theory M reduces to the less-fundamental theory L** in the domains where L is known to be successful... it is not the entirety of theory L that will be recovered... M and L usually **stand for theory parts**." The licensed direction is general→special (M→L), and recovery of theory *parts* in L's own domain is proper reduction₂. This defeats Red's "direction of fit is backwards" objection (E2/E3) and licenses the manuscript's partial recovery — the bilinear-form part and the √d_k temperature part, with rectangular subspace selection honestly ceded to S (attn:1284, table row 1705).

4. "Unlike correspondence generally, it also aims at evidencing **deducibility, and thus relative fundamentality**." This is the genuine discriminating criterion, and it is the only one of the four on which Red could still stand. The gauge construction meets it: the dot-product functional form is logically *deduced* from the isotropic + flat-connection limits applied to the fixed Gaussian KL (judge-verified algebra, below), before any value of Ω is chosen. The general theory (congruence-invariant Fisher/KL geometry) is more fundamental and the dot-product rule is deduced as its special case — the relative-fundamentality direction.

**[DECISIVE — the forced-form algebra, judge-verified line by line in GL(K)_attention.tex]** Under `Σ = σ²I` and `Ω_ij = Ω` constant, the Gaussian KL collapses to `(1/2σ²)‖Ω^{-1}μ_i − μ_j‖²` (attn:1198) and expands to leave exactly one query-key cross term, `−σ^{-2}μ_i^T Ω^{-T}μ_j` (attn:1204), surviving the softmax i-term cancellation (attn:1215) to give the effective logit `σ^{-2}μ_i^T Ω^{-T}μ_j` (attn:1224); the temperature is then forced to `√d_k` (attn:1314). Red conceded this in its rebuttal ("I concede the *form* in this minimal sense and will not contest it"; "There is no second admissible cross-term hiding under the same limits"). The form is an output of the limit, not an input. This is the deducibility Crowther point 4 requires.

**[DEFEATS Red's surviving "two non-uniquenesses" attack]** Red's rebuttal core — that the D♯ caption prices the *small* thin-SVD isotropy ambiguity (attn:1744) while Blue's non-vacuity defense smuggles in the *large* GL(d_k)-surjectivity (attn:1183, attn:1273) — correctly identifies two distinct facts, and the judge confirms they are distinct (verified: attn:1744 is the lift ambiguity of one fixed ambient kernel; attn:1183/1273 is surjectivity of the construction onto all of GL). But Red's inference from the distinction fails on the same canon. Red claims GL-surjectivity is "satisfiable by everything, therefore correspondence not reduction." Crowther point 3 makes covering all of L's successful instances the *goal* of reduction₂, not a defect: the more-fundamental theory recovers the less-fundamental one "in the domains where L is known to be successful." A reduction that recovered only *some* attention heads would be the weaker result. The Newtonian limit covers *every* mass m; no one demotes `c → ∞` to analogy because "it works for any mass." Blue stated this correctly (blue rebuttal 39-41), and the primary source backs it. "Form forced, orbit-representative free" is a reduction with a free parameter — the ordinary case, not vacuity.

## Reasoning

The debate narrowed cleanly. Both teams agree, and the judge independently verified, that the manuscript labels this row **D♯**, not bare D (attn:1701), with the caption defining D♯ as "derived up to a non-uniqueness equivalence class" sitting in the *derived* family alongside the explicit D rows for the softmax-from-KL recovery (attn:1700), the value aggregation (attn:1703), and the √d_k temperature (attn:1717), and distinct from the S rows for ALiBi/T5/sliding-window and subspace selection (attn:1705, 1710-1713). The caption's own taxonomy places D♯ as a sub-tier of D, not of S (attn:1744, judge-verified verbatim).

So the contested claim, read as bare D, overstates — every party agrees. The protocol forbids me from declaring REMAND merely to dodge the wording problem when the substantive question is decidable, and it is. The substantive question is derived-family (D♯) vs structural (S), and that question turns entirely on the reduction-vs-correspondence canon. Red made that canon the spine of its case (E2, E3, and the rebuttal's Crowther appeal). When I read Crowther at the source, the canon contradicts Red on three of four points and is met by the manuscript on the fourth. A team whose own cited external standard, verified at the primary source, defeats its central inference does not win the disjunction.

Red's best surviving move is the "unperformed limit" argument: attn:1286 ("the full limit need not be taken") and attn:1358 ("a literal change of parameterization rather than an analytic σ → 0 limit"). I verified both lines. They are real and they are load-bearing. But two facts defang them. First, Crowther point 2 licenses limiting *and* approximation procedures as imperfect attempts; a reduction does not forfeit derived status because a parameter is absorbed rather than literally sent to its limit — the relevant ratio being negligible is the standard structure (the universe's actual c does not become infinite for `c → ∞` to license Newtonian mechanics). Second, and internal to the manuscript, the *same* σ-absorption rides the √d_k temperature row that the manuscript marks plain **D** (attn:1717, verbatim: "τ = √d_k (dot-product form; σ^{-2} absorbed)") — and Red conceded that temperature row is forced D. Red cannot grant D to the temperature that rides the σ-absorption and deny derived-family status to the bilinear that rides the identical absorption. Blue made this point precisely (blue rebuttal 37) and it is verified against the table. The absorption is one step wearing two labels; the manuscript treats it as derived for the scalar and the matrix alike, differing only in the `♯` that prices the matrix-valued orbit.

The vacuity charge (Red E1, opening second objection) is answered on the same canon and on Chentsov. Reading the bilinear as `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` is not free relabeling of a bare matrix: it asserts factorization through an invertible congruence on a Gaussian covariance, and congruence-invariance is the structure that singles out the Fisher/KL functional (Chentsov; Ay-Jost-Lê-Schwachhöfer, judge did not independently re-verify Chentsov but the theorem is standard and uncontested by Red, who "grant[s] Chentsov's theorem"). Red's counter — congruence-factorability is universal over GL, so it adds nothing — is the surjectivity-is-vacuity move already defeated above.

One point of genuine internal tension survives and should be flagged regardless of who wins (this is what keeps the verdict honest rather than a clean sweep). The D♯ caption (attn:1744) prices the non-uniqueness on the *thin-SVD isotropy-group lift ambiguity* — a small, structured ambiguity in lifting one fixed rank-deficient ambient kernel to its head-space factor. But the manuscript's non-vacuity argument in the body (attn:1183: "the expressive power of the gauge-derived bilinear is therefore identical to that of an independently learned `W_Q W_K^T ∈ GL(d_k)`"; attn:1273: "such a factorization always exists for any invertible Ω") rests on a *different, larger* fact — GL-surjectivity of the construction onto the target space. Red's rebuttal correctly isolated this gap. It does not change the verdict — both facts are properties of a genuine reduction, as the canon shows — but the caption's `♯` and the body's surjectivity claim are answering two different non-uniqueness questions, and a careful reader can be whipsawed between them. That is an actionable expository defect, not a defect in the derivation.

The ledger's own words ("recovered not derived," verified-ledger.md:191, quoting PIFB 1639/1642; "proven per-pair... only a minor closing-analogy imprecision remains," verified-ledger.md:209 — Red cited this as line 199; the line number is off but the substance is verbatim-confirmed) are in-repo and weighted below the external canon. They cut against bare D, which is conceded, and they are consistent with D♯: "recovered" and "per-pair" describe a reduction-with-a-free-parameter, which Crowther classes inside the derived/correspondence family with the deducibility aim. They do not establish S.

## Action

The derivation needs no change. Two surgical manuscript edits would close the only real finding the debate exposed — the caption/body mismatch on *which* non-uniqueness the `♯` prices.

1. **Reconcile the D♯ caption with the body's surjectivity claim (attn:1744 vs attn:1183/1273).** The caption currently prices `♯` on the thin-SVD isotropy-group lift ambiguity alone. The non-vacuity content the body leans on (and that defeats the vacuity charge) is the *GL(d_head)-surjectivity* — that the forced bilinear *form* leaves a free representative ranging over the whole target group. Make the caption name both: amend the parenthetical to read, in substance, "derived up to a non-uniqueness equivalence class: the *functional form* (a single invertible bilinear in the means, temperature √d_k, softmax) is forced by the isotropic + flat-connection limits, while the free representative ranges over GL(d_head) — fixed only up to the thin-SVD isotropy-group lift of the rank-deficient ambient kernel." This states explicitly that `♯` records *form-forced, representative-free*, which is the defensible content, and it stops the body and caption from answering two different questions.

2. **Soften the abstract's strong wording to match the D♯ qualifier (attn:47).** The abstract asserts "standard transformer attention is **recovered as a degenerate, isotropic, flat-bundle case**" with no hedge, while the table prices the bilinear row D♯ and the manuscript explicitly disclaims parameter-level identity (attn:1282). Insert a half-clause: "...is recovered as a degenerate, isotropic, flat-bundle case **up to a head-space-factor equivalence class** (the rectangular subspace embeddings are structural, not derived)." This aligns the abstract's modality with the body's honest scoping (attn:1284) and forecloses a reader taking the abstract as a bare-D claim — the exact overreach this debate identified in the claim's wording.

Neither edit touches the algebra, which is correct and judge-verified (attn:1198→1224, 1314), and neither demotes the row: the row stays D♯, in the derived family, which is where the external canon places it.
