## Concession

I grant Blue's strongest correct point, and I grant it without reservation: **D♯ is a label distinct from S, and the manuscript does place this row at D♯, not at bare D.** Blue is right that the table caption (attn:1744) reads "$\text{D}^\sharp$ = derived up to a non-uniqueness equivalence class," that the `$W_Q W_K^\top = \sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-\top}$` row carries that symbol (attn:1701), and that the manuscript explicitly disclaims parameter-level identity (attn:1282, attn:1284). My opening's third objection ("the status column does not say D") was directed at the *plain-D* reading the claim's words assert; Blue has correctly narrowed the defended object to D♯. So the live dispute is no longer "is it D or S in the manuscript's own typography" — the manuscript says D♯. The live dispute is whether **D♯, as the caption actually defines it, sits above S in content, or whether it collapses into S once you read what the caption's non-uniqueness clause is priced on.**

I also grant the second item Blue states correctly: the *quadratic shape* of the surviving coupling is forced. Once `Σ = σ²I` and `Ω` is constant, the Gaussian KL leaves exactly one `(i,j)`-coupling, the bilinear `μ_i^T Ω^{-T} μ_j` (attn:1204, attn:1224). I verified this expansion line by line. There is no second admissible cross-term hiding under the same limits. I concede the *form* in this minimal sense and will not contest it. My attack is that "the coupling is quadratic" is not "standard attention is derived," and that the gap between those two is exactly where D♯ falls back to S.

## Core attack

Blue's defense rests on one load-bearing equivocation, and the manuscript's own caption is the instrument that exposes it. **Blue defends D♯ using a property the D♯ label is not priced on.**

The caption (attn:1744, verified) defines the D♯ non-uniqueness equivalence class precisely and narrowly: the bilinear "can be thin-SVD-lifted to a full-rank invertible per-head map `M_h ∈ GL(d_head)`, **with the lift fixed only up to right multiplication by elements of the isotropy group**." That is one specific, *structured*, *small* ambiguity — the orthogonal stabilizer of the thin-SVD factorization of a fixed target matrix. It says: given a particular learned `W_Q W_K^T`, the gauge lift of *that one matrix* is non-unique up to the SVD isotropy group. The `♯` prices the ambiguity *in the lift of a fixed target*.

Now read what Blue uses to argue D♯ is "above S." Blue's pivotal sentence (02_blue_opening.md:92-96): "That the surviving free coefficient then ranges over all of `GL(d_k)` is not vacuity; it is the *content* of the reduction ... A reduction that forces the right *form* and leaves the right-sized *parameter freedom* is a successful reduction." Blue's non-vacuity argument is built on the **GL(d_k)-surjectivity** of the bilinear — attn:1183 ("for each pair the bilinear `M_ij` can be realized as any element of `GL(d_k)`") and attn:1273 ("such a factorization always exists for any invertible `Ω`"). I verified both lines. attn:1183 states in the manuscript's own words that "the expressive power of the gauge-derived bilinear is therefore identical to that of an independently learned `W_Q W_K^T ∈ GL(d_k)`."

These are two different non-uniquenesses, and Blue's defense silently swaps the small one for the large one.

- The `♯` in the table is priced on **(A)** the thin-SVD isotropy ambiguity *of the lift of a single fixed matrix* (attn:1744). This is the deformation-parameter-freedom analogue Blue wants — like `m` free in `F = ma`.
- Blue's "right-sized parameter freedom, that's content not vacuity" argument is carried by **(B)** the surjectivity of the construction onto *all of* `GL(d_k)` (attn:1183, attn:1273). This is not the freedom of one undetermined constant in a forced law. It is the statement that the construction matches *every* possible learned bilinear — which is exactly the property my opening named as vacuity, and which the manuscript itself glosses as "expressive power identical to learned `W_Q W_T^T`."

Blue cannot have it both ways. If D♯'s non-uniqueness is the **(A)** isotropy-class ambiguity (what the caption literally says), then D♯ says nothing about whether the *target* dot-product is forced — it only says the *lift* of an already-chosen target is non-unique, which is true of any matrix factorization and carries no derivational content about standard attention. If D♯'s non-uniqueness is the **(B)** GL-surjectivity Blue actually argues from, then by the manuscript's own attn:1183 the construction is satisfiable by *every* invertible bilinear, and "satisfiable by everything" is the textbook signature of a *correspondence* (compatibility under a symbol bridge), not a *reduction* (forced deducibility) — Crowther 2018 (arXiv:1712.00473, verified): reduction "aims at evidencing **deducibility, and thus relative fundamentality**," whereas a relation that merely "link[s] two theories in the domains where both apply" and "establish[es] ... compatibility" is correspondence. A map that fits all of `GL(d_k)` evidences compatibility, not deducibility. Either reading of D♯ lands in S-territory on content.

This is the falsifiable core: **the limit forces that the coupling is *a* bilinear; it does not force *which* bilinear, and standard attention's identity is *which* bilinear (the specific learned `W_Q W_K^T`).** Blue's SEP appeal ("the functional form ... is logically forced ... before any value of `Ω` is chosen," 02_blue_opening.md:91-92) proves only the weaker thing. The SEP limiting-reduction criterion Blue cites requires `lim Q¹ = Q²` — the reduced theory `Q²` (standard attention with *its* specific `W_Q W_K^T`) must be the limit of the reducing theory. What the manuscript delivers is `lim Q¹ = {any bilinear}`, a *family* containing `Q²` as one member among all of `GL(d_k)`. "`Q²` is in the limit family" is not "`lim = Q²`." The general theory reaches `Q²` only by *selecting* the member that equals the pre-existing learned target — and selecting a member to match a pre-existing object is the direction-of-fit my opening flagged, not the forced direction SEP requires.

## Defense (holding the S-or-D♯-collapses position)

Blue's two best counters are the SR→Newton correspondence-principle pattern and the SEP "physically meaningful limit" criterion. Both are answered by a single manuscript fact that Blue's opening does not engage: **the manuscript states the load-bearing step is not a limit at all.**

attn:1286 (verified): "rather than completely taking `σ → 0` we recognize that `σ^{-2}` and `Ω^{-T}` always appear together ... The learned matrices `W_Q, W_K` can then be considered to parametrize this combined quantity directly ... **Therefore, the full limit need not be taken.**" And attn:1358 (verified): "the `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` absorption is **a literal change of parameterization rather than an analytic `σ → 0` limit**."

This breaks the SR→Newton analogy at the joint Blue needs. In SR→Newton the limit *is executed*: `v/c → 0` is genuinely taken, and the Newtonian law drops out as the value of an actually-performed limit, with no pre-existing Newtonian object to fit. The correspondence-principle pattern Blue cites (02_blue_opening.md:53-62) — "a parameter is sent to a special value and the special-case functional form drops out" — requires the parameter to *be sent*. The manuscript says it is not sent: `σ` is absorbed, not taken to zero, and the move is "a literal change of parameterization." A change of parameterization of a learned object is the relabeling operation, not the limiting operation. Butterfield's limiting-case reduction (study-cases; via Crowther-Linnemann arXiv:1712.00473 §Reduction₂, verified) is direction-asymmetric *because the limit is performed*; an unperformed limit is asymmetric in neither direction, and the absorption here runs target→parameter (read the learned `W_Q W_K^T` as `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}`), the relabeling direction.

Blue's falsification condition 2 anchors on attn:1143's finiteness statement ("`σ̃²·D_KL → ½‖Ω^{-1}μ_i − μ_j‖²` as `σ → 0` remains finite") and treats it as the SEP "physically meaningful limit" check passing. I read attn:1143 (verified) and it does not rescue the W_Q W_K^T row — it routes *into* the absorption, against Blue. The full sentence: the finite small-`σ` regime "should be understood as the regime where `σ` is small ... such that **`σ^{-2}` will be absorbed into the learned projections (see below).**" The finiteness statement is about the *rescaled* KL `σ̃² D_KL`, a different object from the `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` coefficient that carries the `W_Q W_K^T` identification; and the manuscript's own "see below" pointer sends that regime to the attn:1286 absorption, which it then declares is not a limit (attn:1358). So Blue's condition-2 anchor and the actual W_Q W_K^T mechanism are the same absorption step wearing two descriptions — and the manuscript labels that step a change of parameterization. The SEP `lim Q¹ = Q²` half is therefore unmet not because the limit "makes no physical sense" but because, for the load-bearing identification, the manuscript does not take the limit.

On Blue's Chentsov defense (02_blue_opening.md:100-110): I grant Chentsov's theorem and that congruence-invariance singles out the Fisher/KL functional. That establishes the *reducing theory* is the right general theory — it does not establish that the *reduction step* to standard attention is forced. Chentsov constrains the metric; it places no constraint on which `Ω` you read a learned `W_Q W_K^T` as. Blue's own sentence concedes the operative point: "the gauge reading asserts the bilinear factors through an invertible *congruence* `Ω^{-T}`" — and attn:1273 (verified) proves every invertible `M` so factors. Congruence-factorability is universal over `GL(d_k)`, so asserting it of `W_Q W_K^T` adds no constraint distinguishable from "it's invertible." That is the vacuity charge restated, not answered.

Finally, the project's own precedent (Blue's condition 5; my [E4]). Blue's claimed disanalogy is that ALiBi/T5 leave the *form* free ("any `π_j` admissible") whereas the bilinear limit forces "a single invertible bilinear ... leaving only the coefficient free." But the verified ledger (line 191, verified) records the cross-manuscript verdict on this exact recovery as "**recovered not derived** (1639/1642)" and "conditional-representation ... **not unconditional uniqueness** (4511)"; and (line 199) demotes the "expressive power identical to learned `W_Q W_K^T`" framing to "proven per-pair ... only a minor closing-analogy imprecision remains." The project's settled record already classifies this as recovery, not derivation, and as per-pair existential, not universal. The demotion criterion the project applied to ALiBi — "the framework supplies the form, not the specific [content]" — applies here in the form attn:1183 makes explicit: the framework supplies the bilinear slot, the architecture supplies an arbitrary `GL(d_k)` element to fill it. "Only the coefficient is free" understates it; by attn:1183 the coefficient ranges over the entire group, i.e. over every admissible standard-attention bilinear. The freedom is not a lone constant like Newtonian `m`; it is the whole target space.

## Net position after rebuttal

I concede D♯ ≠ S *as a label* and I concede the coupling's *quadratic shape* is forced. I hold that D♯, read as its caption defines it (attn:1744 isotropy-class lift ambiguity), carries no derivational content about standard attention beyond "any matrix factors," and that Blue's argument for D♯-above-S smuggles in the *separate* GL(d_k)-surjectivity (attn:1183, attn:1273), which is the vacuity property — so on content D♯ collapses toward S. The forcing Blue establishes is of the form (a bilinear), not of standard attention's specific learned `W_Q W_K^T`; the match to that target is by *selecting* `Ω`, and the manuscript itself calls the selection step "a literal change of parameterization rather than an analytic `σ → 0` limit" (attn:1358) and says "the full limit need not be taken" (attn:1286). An unperformed limit cannot instantiate the SR→Newton correspondence-principle pattern, which requires the limit to be performed. By Crowther's canon (arXiv:1712.00473, verified), compatibility-over-the-whole-group plus a symbol bridge, without forced deducibility of the specific reduced object, is correspondence (S), not reduction (D). The claim's plain-D assertion fails; the strongest surviving reading is D♯, and D♯'s defensible content is the structural-correspondence content the manuscript and ledger already record as "recovered not derived."
