# Red Opening — glk-degenerate-limit-d-vs-s

**Phase:** 2 (opening)
**Role:** Red team (falsify the claim)
**Claim under attack:** The identification `W_Q W_K^T = σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` (full column rank / invertible head-space factor) makes standard transformer attention a genuine DERIVED degenerate limit (D-tier) of gauge-theoretic variational inference, not a structural analogy (S-tier).

## Steelman

The strongest form of the claim is this. The general gauge-theoretic free energy yields, with no parameter fitting, the rule `β_ij = softmax(−D_KL[q_i ‖ Ω_ij q_j]/τ)`, and the KL between two Gaussians is a fixed analytic object. Imposing two *stated* idealizations on that object — isotropic covariance `Σ = σ²I` and a constant (flat) connection `Ω_ij = Ω` — algebraically collapses the KL to a quadratic form whose only query-key cross term is `−σ^{-2} μ_i^T Ω^{-T} μ_j` (attn:1204, 1224). The cross-bilinear coefficient `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` then sits in exactly the slot that standard attention fills with `W_Q W_K^T` (attn:1268-1269). On this reading nothing is reverse-engineered: the dot-product form *falls out* of the KL once the two limits are taken, the limits are the same kind of idealization (low velocity → Newton) that licenses limiting-case reduction in physics, and the manuscript candidly fences what it does not claim (rectangular `W_Q, W_K` are not gauge maps; subspace selection is structural; the identity is value-level on the head subspace, attn:1275, 1284). Under that fencing, "standard attention is a degenerate, isotropic, flat-bundle case" is a derivation of the *form*, and D-tier is the honest label for the form-level recovery.

## Position

The claim is **overstated**: the `W_Q W_K^T = σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` identification earns at most S-tier (structural correspondence), and the manuscript's own taxonomy already concedes this by *not* labeling the row plain D. Three independent failures block D-tier.

First, **direction of fit is backwards.** A derivation makes the special case follow *necessarily* from the general theory; it does not tune a free general parameter to match an already-existing learned object. The framework does the latter: it takes the standard transformer's *independently learned, arbitrary* bilinear and reads it as `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` by *choosing* `Ω` to fit. The manuscript states this in its own words — the absorption is "a literal change of parameterization rather than an analytic `σ → 0` limit" (attn:1358), and "the learned matrices `W_Q, W_K` can then be considered to parametrize this combined quantity `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` directly" (attn:1286). A change of parameterization of a learned object is re-labelling, not deduction.

Second, **the identification is vacuous over GL.** The manuscript proves the factorization "always exists for any invertible `Ω`: any `M ∈ GL(d_k)` can be written as `M = AB^T`" (attn:1273), and at the untied-QK level that "for each pair the bilinear `M_ij` can be realized as any element of `GL(d_k)`" (attn:1183). A statement satisfiable by *every* invertible bilinear constrains nothing. "`W_Q W_K^T` is a gauge transport `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}`" is therefore true of any standard-attention head by construction — the hallmark of a re-reading, not a forced consequence.

Third, **the manuscript's own status column does not say D.** Table `tab:fep_nn_correspondence` labels the `W_Q W_K^T = σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` row **D♯**, not D (attn:1701), and the caption defines D♯ as "derived *up to a non-uniqueness equivalence class* ... with the lift fixed only up to right multiplication by elements of the isotropy group" (attn:1744). The companion `verified-ledger.md` records the cross-manuscript verdict as "recovered not derived" (ledger pass14, PIFB 1639/1642) and demotes the strong "expressive power identical to learned `W_Q W_K^T`" framing to "proven per-pair ... only a minor closing-analogy imprecision remains" (ledger §3, G1). The debate claim asserts plain D-tier; the project's settled record asserts a strictly weaker, hedged tier. The claim overstates the manuscript.

## Evidence (external canon, verified)

**[E1] Vaswani 2017 §3.2.1–3.2.2** (verified via ar5iv full text). Standard attention is `Attention(Q,K,V) = softmax(QK^T/√d_k)V`, with projections `W^Q_i, W^K_i ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_k}` of dimension 512 × 64 (h = 8, `d_k = d_model/h = 64`), and the paper places *no* constraint on these projections — not square, not invertible, not positive-definite, not orthogonal, no transport or congruence structure. The object standard attention actually learns is the ambient bilinear `W^Q (W^K)^T ∈ ℝ^{512×512}` of **rank ≤ 64**, which is *not* an element of `GL(512)`. So the very object the claim says is "a gauge transport in GL" does not live in GL at all; only a thin-SVD-extracted square *factor* does (the manuscript concedes this at attn:1275, 1372). A claim that holds only for a constructed factored sub-object, not for the matrix standard attention learns, is structural — it identifies a shared functional slot, which is precisely the Crowther sense of *correspondence*, not *reduction*.

**[E2] Crowther 2018, "Inter-theory Relations in Quantum Gravity," arXiv:1712.00473** (verified full text). The canon distinction: *correspondence* is "any relationships that link two theories in the domains where both apply ... that establish ... the compatibility of the two theories"; *reduction* is the special case where the overlap is the entire domain of one theory and which "aims at evidencing **deducibility, and thus relative fundamentality**." Reduction₁ (Nagel–Schaffner) requires "**derivability** of corrected theory L* from M with bridge laws." The `W_Q W_K^T = σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` map establishes *compatibility* (the two share the dot-product slot) and supplies a *bridge identification* of symbols, but it does not deduce the dot-product form as forced — by attn:1273 it is compatible with every invertible bilinear, so it cannot evidence that the dot-product specifically is what the general theory *requires*. Compatibility + symbol bridge without forced deducibility is correspondence (S), not reduction (D).

**[E3] Limiting-case reduction, Butterfield/Crowther-Linnemann (arXiv:1712.00473 §Reduction₂; Butterfield 2011 study-cases)** (verified). A genuine limiting-case reduction *varies a parameter in the reducing theory* (e.g. `c → ∞` recovers Newton from SR) and recovers the reduced theory's structure as the limit; the relation is direction-asymmetric and the special structure is *forced* by the limit. The framework's own text disavows exactly this mechanism for the load-bearing step: "the full limit need not be taken" and "rather than completely taking `σ → 0` we recognize that `σ^{-2}` and `Ω^{-T}` always appear together ... the learned matrices `W_Q, W_K` can then be considered to parametrize this combined quantity directly" (attn:1286, 1358). No `σ → 0` limit is taken; the coefficient is absorbed into learned weights. Without an executed limit, the step is a definitional absorption (S), not a limiting-case reduction (D). The contrast with the rows the project itself kept as D is instructive: the softmax-from-KL row (attn:1700) and the temperature `√d_k` row (attn:1717) *are* forced — there the form follows from the variational principle and a concentration limit with nothing fitted. The `W_Q W_K^T` row differs precisely in that an arbitrary learned object is matched, which is why the manuscript already split it off as D♯.

**[E4] Project precedent (internal, cited as consistency check — not canon).** The ledger records that ALiBi, T5, and sliding-window rows were reclassified **D → S** because "the framework supplies the additive-log-prior form, not the specific bias" (ledger §2, pass6), and the "L-layer transformer ↔ L natural-gradient steps" row was "reframed as a structural correspondence" (ledger §2). The demotion criterion the project applied there — *the framework supplies a slot/form but does not force the specific learned content* — applies verbatim to `W_Q W_K^T`: the framework supplies the `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` slot but does not force the specific learned bilinear (attn:1273, 1183). Consistency of the taxonomy demands the same demotion, or at minimum bars the plain-D reading the claim asserts.

## Falsification conditions (what would show my attack is wrong)

My position fails, and the claim earns plain D-tier, if any of the following is established:

1. **Forced form, not matched coefficient.** Show that the general theory *forces* the cross-term coefficient to be `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` for an *independently specified* `Ω` (e.g. `Ω` fixed by the connection/RoPE geometry before seeing `W_Q W_K^T`), so the dot-product bilinear is predicted rather than fitted. If `Ω` is determined upstream and the resulting bilinear matches the learned one without per-target tuning, the direction-of-fit objection [E2]/[E3] collapses. (The RoPE row at attn:1712 may be such a case — there `U_i ∈ O(d_k)` is fixed by position — but RoPE is the *separate* D row, not the constant-`Ω` `W_Q W_K^T` row under debate.)

2. **Non-vacuous constraint.** Exhibit a learned `W_Q W_K^T ∈ GL(d_head)` that the gauge structure *cannot* represent as `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}`. If such an object exists, the identification is a genuine restriction and my vacuity objection (attn:1273, 1183) is wrong. (I predict none exists: attn:1273 proves surjectivity onto GL.)

3. **D, not D♯.** Show that the manuscript's D♯ label and the ledger's "recovered not derived" / "proven per-pair" wording are equivalent to the plain-D claim under debate — i.e. that the non-uniqueness-equivalence-class and per-pair-existence hedges do not weaken D-tier. If D♯ ≡ D and "for each W there exists Ω" ≡ "standard attention IS a degenerate case" universally, my third objection fails.

4. **Executed limit.** Show that an actual `σ → 0` (or other parameter) limit is taken that forces the absorption, contradicting attn:1286/1358's "the full limit need not be taken." If the limit is genuinely executed and singular/analytic in the reduction sense [E3], the change-of-parameterization objection fails.

I hold that conditions 2 and 4 are already settled against the claim by the manuscript's own lines (attn:1273, 1286, 1358), and condition 3 by its own table (attn:1701, 1744). The live question for Blue is condition 1: whether `Ω` is ever fixed upstream for the constant-`Ω` row, or only fitted to a learned target.
