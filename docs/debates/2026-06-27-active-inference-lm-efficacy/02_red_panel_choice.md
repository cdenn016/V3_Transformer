# Red Panel Choice - Phase 2 Opening

Selected panel: variational, transformer-ml, ml-engineer, implementation-engineer, philosophy-of-science.

- variational: The claim turns on whether an inference-time scorer is a genuine expected-free-energy policy functional or a variationally loose scoring rule.
- transformer-ml: The main red risk is collapse into ordinary language-model decoding, reranking, beam search, top-k, or top-p candidate selection.
- ml-engineer: The empirical half of the claim needs compute-matched baselines, seed-aware thresholds, and a cost model for rollout scoring.
- implementation-engineer: The V3 code currently exposes logits and generation hooks, while belief rollouts, preference objects, and transition models are not public inference products.
- philosophy-of-science: Mandatory frame-checking expert; the claim needs direct falsifiers and must forbid outcomes beyond "EFE can be tried."

Discounted default theory experts: info-geometer, geometer, and gauge-theorist are less central for this opening because the disputed load-bearing objects are active-inference policies, LM decoding practice, empirical test design, and V3 reachability rather than Fisher geometry, manifold structure, or holonomy.
