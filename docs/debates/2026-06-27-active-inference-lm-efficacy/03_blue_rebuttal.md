# Blue Rebuttal - active-inference-lm-efficacy

## Concession

Red's strongest point is true: a scalar reranker with active-inference vocabulary is not active inference. Canonical EFE policy selection requires explicit predicted outcomes, hidden states, policy-conditioned transitions, likelihoods, prior preferences, and policy priors. Smith, Friston, and Whyte's A/B/C/D/E template and Friston et al.'s softmax over negative expected free energy establish those requirements; Sajid et al. show that removing preferences or epistemic terms changes the object into a limiting case such as Bayesian design or expected utility [Smith et al. 2022; Friston et al. 2017; Sajid et al. 2021; memo_blue_variational_rebuttal.md].

Blue also concedes the implementation boundary. V3 does not currently expose a public active-inference policy layer. `VFEModel.forward()` returns logits at inference time, not a reusable final-belief rollout object, and the model-channel gamma path uses flat transport [vfe3/model/model.py:792-794; vfe3/model/model.py:1045-1062; memo_blue_implementation-engineer_rebuttal.md]. A train-time EFE replacement would be premature because the policy-conditioned generative-model layer is not yet present, and active-inference canon separates learning from policy selection rather than forcing them into one immediate objective [Friston et al. 2016; memo_blue_philosophy-of-science_rebuttal.md].

## Core attack

Red overreaches when it moves from "under-specified EFE is a reranker" to "the narrow claim overstates theoretical legitimacy." The claim under defense is not that EFE efficacy is established. It is that a finite, explicit, no-grad policy scorer over candidate continuations or agent sets is a legitimate active-inference experiment and worth a falsifiable V3 test. Finite policy scoring is native to the discrete active-inference literature: policies are enumerated, scored by expected free energy, and selected with a softmax or posterior over policies [Friston et al. 2017; Smith et al. 2022; Heins et al. 2022; memo_blue_variational_rebuttal.md].

The V3 code gives a real but incomplete implementation path. `BeliefState` carries `mu`, `sigma`, and `phi` [vfe3/belief.py:22-28]. `PriorBank.encode()` and `PriorBank.decode()` provide token-to-belief and belief-to-logit interfaces [vfe3/model/prior_bank.py:223; vfe3/model/prior_bank.py:312]. `generate()` already has an inference-time selection boundary after `forward(context)` [vfe3/model/model.py:1201-1221]. That is enough to justify an opt-in no-grad candidate scorer as an experiment, while still admitting that a clean belief-rollout helper must be added before the scorer can claim active-inference semantics [memo_blue_implementation-engineer_rebuttal.md].

Red's reranker objection becomes a design constraint rather than a refutation. Language-model decoding baselines are strong: Holtzman et al. and Finlayson et al. show that fixed-model decoding choices can shift text quality. Therefore the EFE scorer must use matched candidate pools, matched compute, length-normalized log-probability controls, beam or truncation baselines, and shallow sigma or entropy rerankers [Holtzman et al. 2019; Finlayson et al. 2023; memo_blue_transformer-ml_rebuttal.md]. If those controls erase the gain, blue loses. If preference and ambiguity terms survive those controls, the scorer is not merely relabeled log-probability.

## Defense

The defended proposal should be stated as an implementation contract. For each candidate continuation or agent set, define: A, the outcome likelihood; B, the transition or rollout rule through V3 beliefs; C, the prior preference distribution over declared outcomes; D, the initial belief state from the current context; E, any policy prior or candidate prior; and a finite horizon. Compute a no-grad score with a risk term comparing predicted outcomes to C and an ambiguity or epistemic term tied to belief uncertainty. Select among the finite candidates with a temperature or policy precision. This derives from external active-inference canon, not from the V3 manuscript or project prose [Smith et al. 2022; Friston et al. 2017; memo_blue_variational_rebuttal.md].

The scorer is nontrivial only under four tests. First, preference sensitivity: changing C while holding candidates and logits fixed must move rankings in the predicted direction. Second, ambiguity or epistemic causal ablations: removing the ambiguity term, shuffling `sigma`, or replacing V3 sigma with a logit-entropy proxy must degrade the declared target if the epistemic story is real. Third, matched-compute baselines: the same candidate pool and forward-call budget must be given to log-probability reranking, beam or truncation decoders, and shallow sigma/entropy rerankers. Fourth, separation from train-time replacement: the first experiment must run no-grad at inference time; it must not claim a new training objective until the policy/generative-model layer exists and has passed these ablations [memo_blue_transformer-ml_rebuttal.md; memo_blue_numerical-analyst_rebuttal.md; memo_blue_implementation-engineer_rebuttal.md].

The falsification conditions are explicit. The claim is not defensible if the scorer cannot be written with declared A/B/C/D/E analogs; if changing C has no ranking effect; if sigma shuffling or ambiguity removal has no causal effect; if matched-compute log-probability or decoding baselines erase the gain within the V3 seed-noise floor; if the epistemic term fails to correlate with realized uncertainty reduction or calibration improvement; or if failures are repaired by changing C, horizon, candidates, or proxies after the fact [Popper 1959; Lakatos 1978; memo_blue_philosophy-of-science_rebuttal.md; memo_blue_numerical-analyst_rebuttal.md].

Thus blue concedes Red's strongest true point and narrows the claim. Efficacy is unproven. A train-time EFE replacement is premature. But a finite, explicit, no-grad EFE policy scorer is theoretically legitimate as an active-inference policy-evaluation layer and empirically worth one hard test in V3, because the code has belief-state material and an inference-time selection hook, while the canon supplies a policy-scoring functional whose failure modes can be isolated.
