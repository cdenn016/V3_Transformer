# GL(K) Attention Manuscripts - Non-Empirical Peer Review Pass 15

Date: 2026-06-23

Scope: This pass reviews `Manuscripts-Theory/GL(K)_attention.tex` and `Manuscripts-Theory/GL(K)_supplementary.tex` while intentionally excluding all live empirical quantities, including PPL, CE, loss, benchmark ratios, seed statistics, and current experiment-dependent claims. I treated prior experiments as provisionally supporting the empirical narrative and focused instead on theorem statements, proof hypotheses, gauge-theoretic consistency, variational framing, and claim grading.

Recommendation: moderate theoretical revision before submission. With the live numerical results set aside, the manuscript is much stronger than the earlier empirical audit made it look. The KL-softmax derivation is clear, the GL(K) invariance theorem is sound, and the canonical-versus-surrogate free-energy distinction is responsibly fenced. The remaining blockers are mostly about overclaiming, overloaded geometric language, and a few mathematical statements that need correction before reviewers see them.

## Major Non-Empirical Comments

1. The "single variational principle" framing remains stronger than the manuscript's own derivation supports. The abstract and introduction say that transformer attention, layer normalization, temperature scaling, training dynamics, and broader architectural structure emerge as consequences of one variational principle (`GL(K)_attention.tex:45`, `58`, `62`). Later sections are more careful: the attention softmax is obtained by an entropy-regularized soft-assignment functional engineered to make the softmax the exact KKT stationary point (`700`, `715`), and the full gauge-covariant free energy is explicitly derived from two principles, namely single-agent FEP plus mixture-of-sources communication (`1033`). The fix is not to weaken the mathematics, but to align the abstract with the actual proof surface. Say that the KL attention rule follows exactly from the specified entropy-regularized mixture-of-sources functional, while several transformer correspondences are exact only under stated limits and several architectural motifs are structural interpretations or optional variational augmentations.

2. The paper should keep the 0-dimensional bundle story and the non-flat extension story sharply separated. The main text correctly states that the 0-dimensional base has no gauge connection or field strength and that vertex-frame transports form a flat cocycle (`GL(K)_attention.tex:514`). The supplement then says that the edge-relaxed extension promotes the system to a "non-trivial principal G-bundle" (`GL(K)_supplementary.tex:56`). On a finite token graph, arbitrary edge holonomies are more naturally a non-flat discrete connection on a trivial bundle unless a topological cocycle obstruction is actually constructed. Replace "non-trivial principal bundle" with "non-flat discrete connection" or "edge-relaxed connection with non-trivial holonomy" unless the manuscript develops the topological bundle classification.

3. The RG coarse-graining definition averages group-valued transports in ambient matrix space, which can leave GL(K). The supplement defines the meta-agent transport as `Omega_AB = (|A||B|)^{-1} sum Omega_ij` and then treats it as a transport (`GL(K)_supplementary.tex:963`). A linear average of invertible matrices need not be invertible, and even when invertible it is not a group mean with guaranteed gauge-covariance properties. This is independent of any empirical RG table. The manuscript should either retract the average back to GL(K), use a log-Euclidean or Karcher-type mean with stated domain assumptions, or explicitly state that the RG calculation is a perturbative algebra-valued approximation near the identity. Without this correction, the coarse-grained holonomy and coupling definitions are not cleanly group-valued.

4. Appendix H's conditional uniqueness theorem needs one statement-level hypothesis promoted from the proof, and one displayed local form corrected. The theorem summary says the local f-divergence has the form `int q_i f(q_i/(Omega q_j)) dc` (`GL(K)_supplementary.tex:1111`), but the standard f-divergence form and the later proof use the transported neighbor density as the base measure (`1236` in the proof region), i.e. `int (Omega q_j) f(q_i/(Omega q_j)) dc`. The reverse implication also relies on a rich admissible neighbor-belief family and normalizability after inverse transport (`1289`). That assumption is load-bearing and should be in the theorem statement, not only in the proof. The result is still useful, but it is conditional in exactly this sense.

5. The statement that GL(K) contains compact subgroups such as SO(N) and SU(N) is wrong or underqualified for the real Gaussian fibers used here. The supplement states this directly (`GL(K)_supplementary.tex:1331`). SO(N) embeds in real GL(N), but SU(N) in its defining representation is a complex matrix group. It embeds into real GL(2N) only by realification, or into a real representation of the appropriate dimension. Replace the sentence with a precise version such as: "GL(K) contains real representations of compact groups, including SO(K), and realifications of unitary or SU(N) representations when K is the corresponding real representation dimension."

6. The correspondence table overgrades several claims as derivations. The row "Forward KL uniqueness -> Why dot-product attention" (`GL(K)_attention.tex:1718`) should not be marked D, because forward KL uniqueness explains the alignment divergence and geometric-mean closure, whereas dot-product attention also needs isotropic covariance, flat/shared transport, key-norm control, and bilinear reduction. The RoPE row (`1728`) is at most a subgroup interpretation or exact accommodation under a chosen positional frame, not a derivation from the variational principle. The natural-gradient row equating belief descent with "Gradient descent (backprop)" (`1743`) conflicts with the text's own statement that backpropagation itself is not derived (`70`). The tied-recurrence/DEQ row (`1752`) is a structural correspondence unless the manuscript proves exact algorithmic equivalence. Downgrade these rows to S or D# as appropriate, or rewrite their targets more narrowly.

7. The symmetry-breaking discussion needs a cleaner distinction between gauge fixing, explicit breaking, and physical observables. The text correctly notes that gauge symmetries are redundancies and cites the Elitzur issue (`GL(K)_attention.tex:1027`). It then says the likelihood term is manifestly not gauge-invariant and selects a preferred orientation in the fiber (`1029`). If the gauge group is a reparameterization redundancy of the statistical fiber, then a non-gauge-invariant likelihood is a modeling/readout choice that fixes a gauge or breaks the redundancy explicitly; it is not by itself a physical symmetry-breaking mechanism. The safest wording is that observations select a coordinate readout or gauge-fixed representative, while any physically meaningful specialization claim should be expressed through gauge-invariant quantities or through an explicitly declared gauge-fixed diagnostic.

8. The FEP framing should include the critique literature already present in the bibliography. The main text cites Friston-style FEP sources (`GL(K)_attention.tex:58`, `688`) but does not visibly engage the philosophical and technical critique literature that appears in `references.bib` under Biehl, Aguilera, Bruineberg, and related entries. This matters because the manuscript's contribution is not a broad defense of the FEP; it is a finite-dimensional variational construction for attention. Add a short paragraph saying that the paper uses an explicit variational objective rather than relying on controversial global FEP claims, and cite the critique literature to show awareness of the debate.

9. The RoPE section is mathematically useful, but the claim level should match the construction. The text shows that RoPE can be represented as a position-dependent SO(2) subgroup of the gauge transport and carefully discusses a sign/transposition convention (`GL(K)_attention.tex:1868`-`1932`). That is a strong structural accommodation. It is not, however, a prediction of RoPE's frequency schedule from the variational principle; the frequency ladder remains an architectural choice. Keep the derivation of the subgroup embedding, but avoid implying that the framework derives RoPE in the same sense that it derives the entropy-regularized KL attention weights.

10. The pairwise GL(K) expressivity statement should not be allowed to imply arbitrary graphwise edge freedom. The supplement correctly argues that a single pairwise transport `Omega_ij = exp(phi_i) exp(-phi_j)` can cover GL+(K) by using two exponentials (`GL(K)_supplementary.tex:565`), and the main text correctly notes that the induced family is constrained by shared per-token factors (`GL(K)_attention.tex:1199`). Preserve that distinction everywhere. Per-edge existential surjectivity does not mean an arbitrary complete edge assignment can be represented simultaneously by vertex-local frames; the latter is constrained by cocycle/flatness.

## Minor Comments

1. Replace "Fischer curvature" with "Fisher curvature" and remove the informal phrase "much, much more" (`GL(K)_attention.tex:1021`). This paragraph currently reads less like a manuscript and more like a note to self.

2. The phrase "allows the FEP to be satisfied" is vague (`GL(K)_attention.tex:1035`). State the actual variational condition: agents minimize the specified free-energy functional under the stated fast/slow update scheme.

3. The sentence "Standard attention transformers are a 0-dimensional gauge theory" should be fenced (`GL(K)_attention.tex:2407`). A safer version is "Standard attention transformers can be represented, under this reconstruction, as the 0-dimensional flat-gauge limit..."

4. The word "noumenal" in the bundle exposition carries philosophical load without doing mathematical work. If retained, define it operationally; otherwise replace it with "base" or "latent base."

5. The state-dependent precision section should keep the optional-regularizer status visible. The Gamma/log-barrier construction is a legitimate augmented variational model, but it should not be folded into the same level of derivation as the KL-softmax result unless the prior over alpha is made part of the generative specification in the abstract or theorem-level framing.

6. The table caption defining D, D#, S, and I is useful; use it more aggressively. Several rows currently marked D are persuasive but conditional correspondences, and the table would become more credible if the grading were conservative.

## Strengths Preserved After Removing Empirical Results

The central GL(K) KL-invariance theorem and local gauge corollary are mathematically sound as presented. The attention derivation is also in good shape once described as an entropy-regularized mixture-of-sources variational problem rather than as an unconstrained consequence of FEP alone. The manuscript now handles the canonical free energy versus entropy-suppressed surrogate distinction carefully, including the gradient-envelope gap, which removes a major earlier ambiguity. The head-space treatment is also much more defensible than a naive "W_Q and W_K are gauge transformations" claim, because it distinguishes exact correspondences, structural accommodations, and implementation-dependent choices.

## Bottom Line

Ignoring live data, the manuscript is not blocked by the empirical section. It is blocked by a smaller set of theory-facing issues that reviewers will notice: group-valued RG averaging, the SU(N) subgroup statement, the f-divergence density typo and hidden richness hypothesis, and overgraded derivation claims in the correspondence table. Fixing those would make the non-empirical core much harder to attack.
