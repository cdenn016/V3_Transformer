## Methodology

Eight independent expert lenses (gauge theory, SPD/differential geometry, information geometry, variational free energy / predictive coding, transformer/attention architecture, numerical analysis, renormalization-group / statistical physics, and philosophy of science) cross-referenced the manuscript against their slice of the research wiki, looking for gaps, shortcomings, overlooked literature already in the knowledge base, and extensions. Every finding was then adversarially verified against the actual `.tex` body and the wiki primary sources to kill false omission flags and superficial connections. Totals: **52 findings verified → 48 survived** (15 confirmed, 33 partially-confirmed, 4 rejected), across 8 lenses and 61 agents. Severities below are the verifier's adjusted values; partially-confirmed findings are hedged in the prose.

# Referee Report

## Manuscript: "Attention as Gauge-Theoretic Variational Inference"
### Files: `GL(K)_attention.tex`, `GL(K)_supplementary.tex`

## 1. Summary of the manuscript

The manuscript advances the thesis that transformer self-attention is the stationary-point structure of a variational free-energy functional on a manifold of Gaussian beliefs `(mu, Sigma, phi)` carried by a non-compact `GL(K)` gauge group, with parallel transport `Omega_ij = exp(phi_i) exp(-phi_j)` comparing beliefs held in different per-token frames. From a single "mixture-of-sources" alignment free energy it recovers softmax attention as an exact KKT stationary point, derives the value-aggregation mean and a `beta`-weighted precision fixed point for the covariance, and identifies residual connections, multi-head structure, RoPE, ALiBi/T5 positional biases, and GLU-style gates as components of the same construction. It proves a `GL(K)`-invariance theorem for the Gaussian KL, proves an Appendix-H conditional-uniqueness result selecting forward KL among `f`-divergences, generalizes the alignment cost to Rényi/`alpha`-divergences (with a headline Rényi-1/2 language-model run), and offers a conjectural renormalization-group reading of meta-agent coarse-graining. A frozen-BERT correlation study and single-layer `GL(K)` language-model experiments accompany the theory.

## 2. Overall assessment

The core mathematics is sound: the `GL(K)`-invariance theorem, the softmax-as-KKT derivation, the precision fixed point, the SPD retraction, and the Appendix-H uniqueness result survive adversarial verification, and no finding overturns a derivation. The single most consequential weakness is not internal to the proofs but external and bibliographic: the manuscript is systematically under-cited against the lab's own catalogued literature, in three directions at once — the FEP-as-gauge-theory precursor it most directly extends, the attention-as-clustering/RG mathematics that would supply the external warrant its conjectures lack, and the predictive-coding/SPD-attention prior art it implicitly re-derives. Closing these citation gaps and aligning the abstract's rhetoric with the body's own (commendably candid) hedges would do more to raise the paper's standing with an informed referee than any new theorem. A handful of genuine but minor expository and numerical refinements round out the list.

## 3. Overlooked literature in the lab's own knowledge base

This is the centerpiece of the report. Every entry below was confirmed against the wiki source and the manuscript body; severities reflect the verifier's adjustments, and the partially-confirmed cases are hedged accordingly.

**`dong-2021-rank-collapse` — the highest-impact omission (strengthens by warranting, threatens the depth extrapolation).** Dong, Cordonnier & Loukas prove that pure stacked self-attention without skip connections and MLPs loses rank doubly-exponentially in depth, collapsing token representations to rank one. This reframes two manuscript claims at once. The residual-connection derivation in §Residual Connections (line 1660) treats skips as the incidental Euler-discretization shadow of natural-gradient flow; Dong shows they are a load-bearing counterforce against collapse. And the advertised "no MLPs, no activations" minimality (Abstract line 49; Tables at lines 2040, 2196) is untested precisely because all experiments are single-layer, the regime where collapse cannot manifest. The source is absent from both body and `references.bib`. This is the one finding the verifier held at **major**: the extrapolation from single-layer success to a multi-layer "why transformers work" thesis is exposed, although the manuscript hedges per-layer claims at line 1836 and flags deeper stacks as future work at line 2343. Enter at §Residual Connections (line 1660) and Limitations: cite Dong, and argue whether the entropy term `tau*beta*log(beta/pi)` and the token-dependent `Omega_ij` arrest collapse where the per-token MLP otherwise would.

**`geshkovski-2023-mathematical-transformers` — held at major (strengthens; supplies the missing external warrant).** Geshkovski, Letrouit, Polyanskiy & Rigollet prove that attention dynamics cluster tokens-as-particles and collapse to consensus in the depth limit, with rate set by the inverse temperature. This is the rigorous mathematics of exactly the consensus belief-coupling term (line 911), the meta-agent coarse-graining (Eq. meta_agent_beliefs, line 2304), and the emergent-clustering results (lines 2238–2248) the manuscript reports, with their inverse-temperature mapping onto `tau = kappa*sqrt(K)`. It is entirely absent from body and bib. Enter at the Emergent Semantic Structure section and the consensus term first, §RG Universality (line 2287) second; frame the meta-agent grouping as the discrete coupling-space shadow of their mean-field flow rather than a literal identity (the two coarse-grain over different variables).

**`mehta-schwab-2014-variational-rg-deep-learning` — held at major (strengthens via honest positioning).** Mehta & Schwab establish the canonical "a learning architecture IS an RG flow" result, an exact term-for-term map between Kadanoff variational RG and stacked RBMs. The RG conjecture's load-bearing move (§RG, line 2287; Supp. App. F, line 926) is to read the layered reduction as an RG flow, but it cites only Kadanoff/Wilson and answers the rigorous-vs-metaphorical question with a CLT it concedes is gauge-trivial. Mehta-Schwab is precedent-to-cite, not a rival to refute. Enter in the Scope-and-status paragraph (line 2336): contrast prior exact (Mehta-Schwab) and metric (Bény-Osborne) accounts against the present weaker, conjectural CLT contraction.

**`sengupta-friston-2017-bayesian-gauge-theory` and `sengupta-2016-neuronal-gauge-theory` — downgraded to major (positioning/novelty; complicates the novelty claim).** These are the most direct prior art: variational Bayesian inference is itself a gauge theory, free-energy minimization its gauge-covariant dynamics. The GL(K) body neither cites nor engages them, and its Introduction claim that FEP and attention "remain stubbornly separated" (line 58) is overstated against this lineage. ## 4. Major theoretical gaps and shortcomings

After deduplication and honoring the verifier's downgrades, no finding remains at the manuscript-invalidating level; the items below are the most substantive of the surviving minor/major shortcomings, stated with their true (reduced) severity.

1. **Covariance-versus-invariance conflation (minor; §Notation line 402 and 14 further uses).** Theorem 1 proves two-argument KL *invariance*; the per-vertex *covariance* law `Omega_ij -> g_i Omega_ij g_j^{-1}`, the invariance of `beta_ij`, and the covariant transformation of the assembled output `mu_hat_i -> g_i mu_hat_i` are asserted (footnote line 427) but never proved, while the whole functional is labeled "gauge-covariant." For the flat cocycle these are short corollaries of Theorem 1 once the coherent action `exp(phi_i) -> g_i exp(phi_i)` is stated, and line 665 already defers the Regime-II covariance proof to the companion. The fix is to promote the footnote to a stated Lemma and reserve "invariant" for the KL/weights, "covariant" for transported beliefs.

2. **`GL(K)` is non-compact and non-semisimple; compact-group equivariance theorems are cited as if applicable (minor; Future Directions line 2372, multihead line 1751 ff., Clebsch-Gordan reading line 2362).** The GDL lineage (Cohen, Kondor, Weiler, TFN) is proved for compact groups acting on data coordinates; here the group is non-compact and latent. The manuscript's actual Schur-commutant analysis (line 2364) is correct, so no derivation fails, but a one-paragraph caveat is missing — that "irrep block" denotes a type-labeling discipline, not a canonical isotypic decomposition, and that the off-diagonal-as-Clebsch-Gordan reading is an analogy. Wiki: `concepts/Irreducible representation`, `themes/Gauge equivariance and geometric deep learning`.

3. **Appendix-H positioning against the Bregman-∩-f-divergence characterization (minor; Supp. App. H, line 1313).** The forward-KL uniqueness theorem re-derives, by a variational-duality route, a selection the canonical "KL is the unique Bregman *and* f-divergence" result also yields; the manuscript gestures at the Bregman side once (line 1313) but never positions against it. A paragraph citing Amari 2016 and `cencov-1982-statistical-decision-rules` (and adding Banerjee 2005 to the bib) would honestly bound novelty without conceding it, since the duality route is complementary, not subsumed.

4. **The detached query-side filtering update and Neal-Hinton monotonicity (minor; line 883).** The default per-position update drops the (generically nonzero) key-side gradient term `eq:keyside_partial` yet is labeled "standard coordinate-ascent mean-field VI." The manuscript is candid that this is a partial update on a single objective `F_red` and provides the omitted term plus a full-gradient toggle, so the finding's stronger "monotonicity asserted as established" claim was rejected. What remains is a one-sentence fix: either show the partial step is non-decreasing on `F_red` (Neal-Hinton) or concede it trades that guarantee for filtering consistency, citing `neal1998view` (`neal-1998-variational-em`).

## 5. Mathematical and numerical issues

1. **Holonomy diagnostic `g3 = ||H_ijk - I||` is frame-dependent (minor consistency issue; Eq. g3_def line 2298, aggregate line 1782).** For non-compact `GL(K)` the Frobenius deviation is not conjugation-invariant, whereas the Wilson action at line 665 correctly uses the invariant `W_ijk = Re Tr(H_ijk)`. The verifier tempered this: `g3` (and the equally frame-dependent `g2`) are RG *coordinates*, not advertised curvature certificates, the RG/CLT argument explicitly does not rest on gauge content (line 2325), and in Regime I `H = I` exactly. The worthwhile edit is a clarifying sentence that `g2,g3` are gauge-fixed magnitudes and the admissible invariant certificate is `W_ijk` or `sum_k |lambda_k(H) - 1|`. Wiki: `concepts/Non-flat connection and the photon analogy`.

2. **The covariance fixed point is the natural-parameter (`e`-flat) barycenter, not the Karcher mean (minor; Eq. sigma_fixed_point_main, lines 1564, 1637).** The arithmetic-of-precisions update is correctly the `e`-flat barycenter and the manuscript already qualifies it "in the natural parameterization," so the affine-invariance worry is partly pre-empted; it does not cite Moakher 2005 or Jeuris 2012 nor contrast the affine-invariant Karcher mean. Add the one-sentence contrast and the citations. Wiki: `moakher-2005-geometric-mean`, `jeuris-2012-matrix-geometric-mean`.

3. **Pennec citation mis-pointed for the affine-invariant exp-map (minor; Supp. line 641).** The verifier rejected the headline "retraction-not-exp-map" framing: Eq. 643 `Sigma^{1/2} exp(tau B) Sigma^{1/2}` *is* the exact AIRM exponential map, and clipping merely rescales the tangent. The live defect is a citation swap — `Pennec2006` (Intrinsic Statistics) is cited where `PennecFillardAyache2006` (the Riemannian tensor framework, in the bib but cited only in the companion) is the correct source for the affine-invariant metric. Wiki: `pennec-2006-affine-invariant-tensor`, `absil-2008-optimization-matrix-manifolds`.

4. **The "SO(N) specialization" Rodrigues `dexp` is SO(3)-only (minor; Supp. lines 457–472).** The single-angle closed form is valid only for SO(3); the verifier confirmed the body actually restricts it ("For SO(3)") and supplies the general `(e^{ad}-1)/ad` series at Eq. 451 for `N>3`, with autodiff in production, so no reader gets wrong gradients.
