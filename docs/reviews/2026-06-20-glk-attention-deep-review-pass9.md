## Major comments (the five mediums)

### M1. Petz (1996) attribution is inverted (supp:1321)

The interpretations paragraph reads: "`\citet{Cencov1982}` established the uniqueness of the Fisher-Rao metric ..., `\citet{Petz1996}` extended the monotone-metric uniqueness to the matrix (quantum) setting." This states the converse of what Petz proved. Petz, "Monotone metrics on matrix spaces" (Lin. Alg. Appl. 244, 1996), characterizes the monotone metrics via operator-monotone functions (the Morozova-Chentsov-Petz family) and finds that, unlike the classical case, uniqueness *fails* quantumly: there is an infinite family of monotone metrics. Two independent sources confirm this (the math-ph/0601056 survey: "the Fisher information metric is the unique monotone metric in the classical case, while in the matrix case there is a family of monotone metrics"; and the Petz-paper synopsis: "this requirement no longer uniquely determines the metric").

This is a flat factual error, and correcting it strengthens rather than weakens the surrounding rhetoric: the manuscript's own setting is classical, so Petz is precisely the marker of *where* classical uniqueness breaks down. Suggested replacement: "`\citet{Petz1996}` characterized the monotone metrics in the matrix (quantum) setting, where, in contrast to the classical case, uniqueness fails and the invariant metrics form a parameterized family." Status: this is interpretive prose, but it is load-bearing for the "classical uniqueness tradition" framing it anchors.

### M2. Results-section causal claim over-reaches and disagrees with its own twin (attn:2242)

Line 2242: "these results establish that gauge-covariant variational inference is a viable computational substrate ... supporting the explanatory thesis that transformers succeed *because* they approximate this underlying mathematical structure." The parallel Discussion sentence (2272) is correctly hedged: "supports the explanatory claim that transformers succeed *in part because* they approximate the underlying mathematical structure." The strong form has two problems. First, it disagrees with its own twin on both verb ("establish" vs "support") and hedge ("because" vs "in part because"). Second, the inference is affirming the consequent: that a distinct architecture (single-layer GL(K), one main seed, below the parameter-matched transformer at PPL 71.6 vs 48.5) also models language is logically independent of *why* transformers succeed. The in-source TODO at 2236 makes this explicit — the causal attribution "the VFE's advantage stems from its geometric structure" is flagged as not yet tested, because the gauge on/off ablation harness exists but is unrun. Fix: harmonize 2242 to the weaker 2272 form, for example "are consistent with the explanatory hypothesis that transformers succeed in part because they approximate this underlying mathematical structure."

### M3. Abstract asserts as fact what the body declines to derive (attn:49 vs 2290)

The abstract's closing clause: "a geometric regime we hypothesize is matched to the approximately path-independent semantic transport that compositional language *requires*." The hedge "we hypothesize is matched to" guards the *matching*, but the embedded relative clause "that compositional language requires" asserts the requirement categorically. The body is explicit that this is unsettled: at 2290, "Whether natural language's compositional-semantic content carries an independent functional pressure toward path-independent meaning transport ... is an open question that the present work does not settle," and "The mapping between the homomorphism reading and the gauge-transport reading is a novel construction that we do not derive." The ledger (entry under section 3) demoted the holonomy-compositionality claim to an open question for `sec:flat_bundle`'s own framing but did not check the abstract's stronger wording. Fix: soften to a hypothesis, for example "matched to a hypothesized pressure toward approximately path-independent semantic transport in compositional language (an open question; Section `sec:flat_bundle`)."

### M4. WikiText-103 KN-5 figure attributed to a paper that does not report it (attn:2240)

The text: "the modified Kneser-Ney 5-gram model `\citep{chen1998empirical}`. `\citet{merity2017pointer}` report KN-5 test perplexity ~153--156, but this uses word-level tokenization with a ~267K vocabulary." Primary-source check (ar5iv render of arXiv:1609.07843, re-confirmed this pass): Merity et al. 2017 has exactly two results tables, PTB (Table 2) and WikiText-2 (Table 3); it introduces WikiText-103 as a dataset but reports no results on it, and its only KN-5 figure is the PTB 141.2 quoted from Mikolov & Zweig. The ~267K vocabulary detail is correct for WikiText-103, but the ~153--156 KN-5 number does not come from Merity 2017; it traces to the gated-convolution / Transformer-XL-era WikiText-103 literature (most plausibly Dauphin et al. 2017, "Language Modeling with Gated Convolutional Networks," which reports a Kneser-Ney 5-gram WikiText-103 baseline near this value). Fix: re-attribute the ~153--156 figure to its actual source (author to confirm against Dauphin 2017 or the intended reference), or drop the specific number and keep only the framework's own matched-tokenization KN-5 = 134.8. The footnote's "~18 PPL reduction (153--156 -> 134.8)" argument depends on this number, so the re-attribution should carry through to the footnote.

### M5. Wrong-source bib resolution for the RG-flow citation (supp:131, references.bib:2462)

Line 131: "This is the gauge-theoretic analogue of renormalization group flow in statistical field theory `\citep{anderson1984basic,wilson1974renormalization,garciaperez2018multiscale}`." The `anderson1984basic` entry resolves to "Anderson, James A. and Rosenfeld, Edward, *Neurocomputing: Foundations of Research*, MIT Press, 1988" with a self-flagging `note = {Often cited as Anderson 1984 for earlier technical report editions}`. A neural-network reprint anthology cannot support an RG-flow-in-statistical-field-theory claim; the key name and the intended context point to P. W. Anderson, *Basic Notions of Condensed Matter Physics* (Benjamin/Cummings, 1984), a legitimate RG / statistical-physics authority. Fix: replace the entry with P. W. Anderson, *Basic Notions of Condensed Matter Physics* (1984), or drop `anderson1984basic` from this citation group, which leaves `wilson1974renormalization` and `garciaperez2018multiscale` that do support the claim.

L5. RoPE interpolation-hierarchy notation (attn:1908). The displayed three-term set-containment writes the top "Full GL(K)" term in pure-cocycle form (`sigma^{-2} exp(-phi_i)^T exp(phi_j)^T`, no learned projections) while the middle (RoPE) and bottom (standard) terms carry the constant learned `W_Q, W_K`. The cocycle forces `Omega_ii = I`, so its diagonal kernel is `sigma^{-2} I`, whereas the RoPE diagonal kernel is `W_Q W_K^T`, equal to `sigma^{-2} I` only if `W_Q W_K^T = sigma^{-2} I` (false for generic learned projections). As printed the leftmost containment does not hold term-by-term. The caption's structural reading is correct; only the displayed equation conflates two parameterizations. Fix: write all three terms with a common `Omega_ij^{(.)}` symbol annotated by its admissible group, or restore the constant learned gauge in the top term. (The ledger's RoPE relative-composition and `exp(phi_i - phi_j)` transpose identities are different statements and do not cover this three-term chain.)

L6. `su2024roformer` bib. The entry lists "Su, Jianlin and Ahmed, Murtadha and Lu, Yu and Pan, Shengfeng and Bo, Wen and Liu, Yunfeng." The published order (arXiv:2104.09864, Neurocomputing 568:127063) is Su Jianlin, Lu Yu, Pan Shengfeng, Ahmed Murtadha, Bo Wen, Yunfeng Liu, and BibTeX mis-parses "Ahmed, Murtadha" and "Bo, Wen" as family-name-first, so it will print "M. L7. Elitzur attribution (attn:1011). "Elitzur's theorem forbids spontaneous breaking of local gauge symmetries in lattice gauge theory `\citep{weinberg1995quantum}`" names a specific lattice result but cites Weinberg QFT Vol. 2 (continuum applications). The primary source is Elitzur (1975), Phys. Rev. D 12, 3978, which is not in the bibliography. Fix: add and cite Elitzur 1975 as the primary (optionally keeping Weinberg as a textbook secondary).

L8. "novel" over-read (attn:1572). "The covariance gradient reveals a novel uncertainty channel absent in standard transformers." The Sigma-gradient itself is derived (ledger-settled), so "reveals" on the math is fine; "novel" over-reads, since per-token covariance dynamics are standard in variational Bayes / natural-gradient inference. The channel is novel relative to vanilla transformers, not to the science. Fix: "introduces an uncertainty (covariance) channel absent in standard transformers."

L9. dexp growth heuristic (supp:577). The motivational prose "for the symmetric directions, `||dexp_phi(T_a)|| ~ exp(||phi||)`" is quantitatively imprecise: the right-trivialised differential grows at the rate set by the spectral radius of `ad_phi` (the maximum eigenvalue-difference of phi), and directions commuting with phi are O(1). For `phi = diag(d,-d)` the rate is `exp(2d) = exp(sqrt(2) ||phi||_F)`. The numerical-analysis lens judged the `~` form an acceptable order-of-magnitude heuristic; the geometry lens wanted the sharper statement. Treat as an optional precision tweak: "`~ exp(rho(ad_phi))`, with directions commuting with phi unaffected." The load-bearing exact factor `exp(phi) exp(phi)^T = exp(2phi)` in the pullback metric is stated correctly and is ledger-verified.
