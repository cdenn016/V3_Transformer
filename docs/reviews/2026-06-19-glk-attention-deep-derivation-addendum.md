### GL(K) gauge-invariance (`thm:glk_invariance`)

The core theorem is correct and the derivation is clean. SymPy confirmed the trace term, the Mahalanobis term, and the log-determinant congruence identity `det(O Sigma O^T) = (det O)^2 det(Sigma)`, and the end-to-end Gaussian KL was verified numerically to machine precision including `det(O) < 0`; the chi-square f-divergence was confirmed by Monte Carlo. No sign, transpose, or index error exists. Of the three flags, the adversarial verifier downgraded two to none — the "GL(K) is the symmetry group" phrasing (GL(K)_attention.tex line 520) is defensible because the text consciously restricts to linear actions, and the `(det Omega)^2` vs `|det Omega|` pairing (lines 554/566) refers to two genuinely distinct quantities that the manuscript never equates. The one surviving item is an author judgment-call: the f-divergence corollary (lines 560–566) justifies the cancellation only in the integrand ratio `p'/q'` and omits the outer change-of-variables substitution `x = Omega^{-1} y, dy = |det Omega| dx` that actually preserves the integral. The stated conclusion is a standard textbook fact and is true; the one-liner is an expository compression, not an error.

### Softmax as KKT stationary point (`GLK-attention-softmax-KKT`)

This proof is mathematically sound and emerged from the deeper pass clean. SymPy reproduced the Lagrangian stationarity (including the `+1` constant correctly absorbed into the normalizer), the temperature softmax `beta* = pi exp(-E/tau)/Z`, its uniqueness via the strictly positive-definite Hessian `diag(1/beta)`, the substituted value `-tau log Z_i`, and the `tau`-rescaling equivalence. The load-bearing claim that dropping the `tau*beta*log(beta/pi)` attention-entropy term collapses the minimizer to a delta on `argmax(-E)` rather than a softmax was confirmed. All three originally flagged items were rejected or downgraded to none on adversarial re-reading: the per-row mean-field separability is already established by the single-query construction (line 688, line 699), the KKT/barrier boundary argument is correct in substance with the Gibbs/KL rewrite merely a tidier alternative, and the "WLOG" framing for `tau` is loose phrasing the text immediately disambiguates by spelling out the `tau`-dependence at line 773. No confirmed issues; the section stands.

### Three-limit reduction to standard attention

The load-bearing algebra is correct: SymPy and NumPy confirmed the Mahalanobis identity (line 1078), the isotropic KL expansion and its cross-term transpose (lines 1196–1222), the general untied decomposition `eq:full_kl_general` (line 1155, residual provably zero), the surjectivity of `M_ij` onto GL(d), the SVD factorization existence, and the RoPE identification (line 1187, which the spec's "known gap" warning does not in fact threaten since orthogonal frames rotate Q and K symmetrically). Two issues originally rated major were confirmed but reduced to minor. First, the high-dimensional-concentration justification for key-bias cancellation (lines 1229–1236) controls only the relative fluctuation of `||mu_j||^2`; after division by `tau = sqrt(d_k)` the residual key-bias spread is `O(1)`, the same order as the retained signal, so concentration alone does not render the bias negligible — only layer normalization (mechanism 2) is an exact softmax-shift cancellation. The manuscript already hedges this as "approximate" (line 1356), so the defect is that mechanisms (1) and (2) are presented as co-equal when (2) is exact; the fix is one or two sentences. Second, the `sigma`-bookkeeping across the two reduction routes contains a localized double-count: Route A absorbs `sigma^-2` into `W_Q W_K^T` with `tau = sqrt(d_k)` (lines 1284/1312), while Route B's per-head temperature equation (`eq:per_head_temperature`, line 1792) divides the full KL — which itself carries `(1/2 sigma^2)` — by `kappa sqrt(d)` with `kappa ∝ sigma^2`, yielding net `sigma^-4` rather than `sigma^-2`. The fix is to strip the `(1/2 sigma^2)` prefactor from the numerator of `eq:per_head_temperature` so the bare distance is what gets tempered; `kappa ∝ +sigma^2` is then physically correct. The two remaining flags (limit-ordering at lines 1141/1284/1356, and the `b_ij = -r_j/(2 tau)` vs isotropic `-(1/2 sigma^2)||mu_j||^2` normalization at line 1184) are judgment-calls: the former is already reconciled in the text (the manuscript explicitly states `sigma -> 0` need not be taken), and the latter is two correct expressions for the same bias under different covariance assumptions, coinciding under the closure `C^-1 <-> sigma^-2 I`.

### Covariance gradient and the indefinite Schur correction (Supp. App. B)

The core mathematics is correct and, where it matters, honestly hedged. SymPy and finite differences confirmed the KL covariance gradient, the `-(1+alpha)` coefficient, the `alpha=1` and general-`alpha` convex-barycenter fixed points, the single-KL second variation, and the reduced-free-energy Hessian's `-tau^{-1} Cov_beta` Schur correction (softmax log-partition Hessian identity, numerically matched). The e-flat barycenter terminology is accurate: the arithmetic mean of precisions is the e-geodesic barycenter for the Gaussian exponential family, distinct from the Karcher mean, and the manuscript correctly qualifies it as a barycenter "in the natural parameterization." One issue is confirmed: at Supp. App. B line 388 (mirrored at GL(K)_attention.tex lines 1648–1650) the `Cov_beta(.)` term is written with a single argument and is well-defined only as the PSD self-covariance operator `E_beta[X⊗X] - (E_beta X)⊗(E_beta X)` on Sym(K); the text never states this, yet that PSD structure is exactly what makes the correction negative-semidefinite and able to erode definiteness. The fix is to define the operator once. The remaining two flags are judgment-calls: the `tau->0` vs small-finite-`tau` distinction (the indefiniteness lives at small-but-finite `tau` with near-degenerate neighbor divergences, not the strict one-hot limit, which is benign) is a legitimate clarification but the text's "(alpha, tau small)" lists joint enabling conditions and asserts only "can be indefinite"; and the symmetric-matrix derivative convention at lines 227–235 yields the correct result because `-Sigma^-1 + Sigma_2^-1` is already symmetric, so the cited Petersen–Pedersen identities, though stated for unconstrained `Sigma`, give the right gradient.

### Gradient descent recovers the standard update

Every symbolically or numerically checkable step is correct: SymPy confirmed all four mean-gradient terms, the covariance gradient coefficient, the softmax sensitivity, the `alpha` chain rule, the Gaussian KL-wrt-`Sigma` derivative, the exact Fisher `sigma^-2` cancellation, and both envelope-theorem identities, with an independent NumPy central-difference check matching to `1.4e-10`. The envelope-theorem treatment is internally consistent: the boxed general gradient is the gradient of the attention-weighted surrogate (retaining the `dbeta` terms), while `F_red` drops them by the envelope theorem, the two differing by exactly `-tau^{-1} Cov_beta(E, dE/dx)` and coinciding at joint stationarity. The originally major residual-as-Euler-step and barycenter-ordering flags were both rejected on re-reading — the manuscript labels the residual identification a structural ("S") Euler discretization and leads with the `alpha_i=1` fixed point exactly as the suggested fix asks. One genuine confirmed item remains, a manuscript-versus-code reproducibility gap acknowledged by the author's own inline TODO (GL(K)_supplementary.tex line 656): the quoted SPD-retraction constants `eps_SPD=1e-4`, `kappa_max=1e4` (line 651) are not the live constants — the released code floors eigenvalues at `1e-6` and applies no condition-number cap in the retraction path, and the auditor's claimed live `max_condition=10.0` is itself ungrounded. Since the natural-gradient mean step is `Sigma_i · grad F`, the preconditioner amplifies the gradient by up to `cond(Sigma_i)`, so the reported runs' actual cap should be stated. The diagonal-projection flag (lines 1540–1566) is a judgment-call: the clean message passing `beta_ij(mu_i - Omega mu_j)` is correctly framed as the covariance-aligned special case, and the implemented update is the full `eq:natural_gradient_general`, so no false equivalence is asserted; only the diagonal-approximation error is left unquantified.

### Conditional uniqueness of forward KL (Supp. App. H)

All checkable algebra verifies — the functional derivative, the coefficient-2 forward implication, the rearrangement to a constant, the residual-sum subtraction, the WLOG `f'(1)=0` footnote, the `+1` absorption, and the envelope theorem on a toy. The first-pass worry that the config-equalization argument needs more than `sum_j beta_ij = 1` is resolved by the equalization configuration `q_j = Omega^{-1} qbar`. One issue is confirmed, a scholarly-positioning/citation defect rather than a math error: App. H cites only Csiszar1967 (the f-divergence definition) and never Chentsov1982, Petz1996, Amari, or Jiao et al. 2014, although Chentsov1982 and Petz1996 already sit unused in references.bib. The theorem's endpoint — KL is the unique f-divergence with the geometric-mean/exponential-family closure — coincides with the canonical Bregman-cap-f = KL uniqueness result, and line 1313 even names the negative-entropy Bregman generator while filing it under "interpretations." The proof is a valid variational/envelope re-derivation that does not assume decomposability, so the remedy is to add the missing citations and frame App. H as a re-derivation of the canonical uniqueness rather than an independent theorem. The two remaining flags are judgment-calls: the open-subinterval analytic-continuation step (line 1286) is correct and robust — varying `c` at fixed `p_i` makes `r(c)` a non-constant real-analytic function whose image is an interval by the intermediate value theorem, with the normalizer a fixed scale — and the envelope-theorem regularity conditions, though unstated, demonstrably hold.

### Gauge-frame gradients via dexp (Supp. App. C)

Every load-bearing identity verifies: the dexp series/integral relation, the Higham block-matrix Fréchet representation (numeric residual `3e-126`), the SO(3) Rodrigues closed form with correct `c1, c2` and small-angle Taylor (machine precision, resting on the genuinely SO(3)-specific `ad_phi^3 = -theta^2 ad_phi`), the three KL-through-transport derivative terms with correct signs, and the right-trivialised transport derivatives. No retraction is mislabeled: the covariance update at line 643 is the canonical affine-invariant SPD exponential map. Both surviving flags are exposition judgment-calls. The omitted `(terms from dSigma_t^{-1}/dphi)` in the mean-term derivative (lines 515–518) does stand for a genuine, generally nonvanishing term `-Delta_mu^T Sigma_t^{-1} (dSigma_t/dphi^a) Sigma_t^{-1} Delta_mu`; since the sibling trace and log-det terms are written out in full, writing this one out is a worthwhile completion. The `xi := T_a` indexing flag was rejected — the substitution is in fact stated at the point of use (line 546) and at the convention paragraph (line 402) — and the SO(N>3) caveat is an optional clarity addition since the universally valid general series (line 451) already opens the subsection.

## 2. Ready-to-Insert Connection Drafts

### Connection 1: Dong, Cordonnier & Loukas (2021) — rank collapse and the load-bearing identity path

Two insertions in GL(K)_attention.tex. The first appends to the "Residual Connections as Variational Gradient Flow" section after line 1679 and STRENGTHENS the residual-stream reading by supplying a theorem-grade reason the identity path is load-bearing. The second appends to the Limitations section after line 2345 and COMPLICATES the "no MLPs" minimality claim, since Dong et al. identify the per-token MLP as a secondary anti-collapse brake and every reported run is single-layer. Requires adding `dong2021attention` to references.bib (not currently present).

```latex
% Insertion 1: append to sec:residual_connections, after line 1679
The load-bearing status of this identity path is not merely a matter of optimization convenience. \citet{dong2021attention} prove that a transformer assembled from self-attention alone, stripped of skip connections and feed-forward layers, has output that converges doubly exponentially in depth to a rank-one matrix, so that every token representation collapses to a single shared vector. Their analysis tracks a residual measuring distance to the rank-one (constant-across-tokens) subspace and shows that each stochastic, row-normalized attention layer contracts it multiplicatively, with the skip connection identified as the dominant counterforce. Read in belief coordinates, this is exactly the heat-death consensus that iterated alignment dynamics drive toward when left unchecked: the alignment term $\sum_j \beta_{ij} D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij} q_j)$ is a consensus functional, and its unopposed fixed point is the degenerate state in which all beliefs coincide. The variational reading therefore reframes the residual connection from an incidental shadow of Euler discretization into the structural counterforce that this collapse requires, and it locates that counterforce concretely: the prior-anchoring identity path, weighted by the state-dependent precision $\alpha_i$ (Section~\ref{sec:state_dependent_precision}), supplies the per-token restoring term $\alpha_i D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_i \| p_i)$ that keeps each belief from being absorbed into the consensus, while the token-dependent transport $\Omega_{ij}$ replaces the single global mean of pure attention with a token-specific alignment target, so the stationary configuration is a structured, multi-cluster state rather than a rank-one one. That the same anti-collapse role is played in standard transformers by an architecturally bolted-on skip connection, and here by a term derived from first principles, is the substantive content of the correspondence rather than a coincidence of form.

% Insertion 2: append to sec:limitations, after the single-layer scope sentence (line 2345)
A second qualification attaches to the architectural-minimality claim. \citet{dong2021attention} show that the per-token feed-forward layer, which our construction deliberately omits, is one of the brakes (secondary to the skip connection) that arrest the doubly exponential rank collapse of deep pure attention. Because every language-model run reported here is single-layer, the depth regime in which that collapse becomes severe is not probed, and the practical adequacy of the no-MLP path at depth is therefore untested. The framework's claim is that the feed-forward brake is not needed because two of its functions are supplied elsewhere: the prior-anchoring identity path $\alpha_i D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_i \| p_i)$ resists absorption into the consensus, and the token-dependent transport $\Omega_{ij}$ makes the alignment target token-specific rather than a single shared mean (the precise feature that distinguishes a multi-cluster fixed point from the rank-one one of \citet{dong2021attention}). We note that the attention-entropy regularizer $\tau \beta_{ij}\log(\beta_{ij}/\pi_{ij})$ is a weaker safeguard than these two: it prevents the coupling row $\beta_{ij}$ from sharpening into a one-hot assignment, but a smooth, near-uniform $\beta$ is itself a route to token averaging, so the entropy term constrains the form of the mixing rather than injecting rank across tokens. Establishing that the $\Omega_{ij}$ transport and the $\alpha_i$ identity path together substitute for the feed-forward brake at depth, by training deep autoregressive stacks and measuring the rank-one residual of \citet{dong2021attention} against an MLP-equipped control, is left to future work.
```

Caveats: the residual derivation places the skip connection on the within-layer mean update whereas Dong's collapse concerns across-depth token mixing; the draft bridges these via the manuscript's own "consensus functional" language but the identification is interpretive, not a manuscript theorem (hence "the framework's claim is"). The draft deliberately downgrades the entropy term's anti-collapse role relative to the source note, since a near-uniform `beta` still averages tokens. Add the `dong2021attention` bibtex before compiling.

### Connection 2: Geshkovski et al. (2023) — consensus/clustering dynamics for belief coupling

Three coordinated insertions in GL(K)_attention.tex. The primary paragraph follows the "Belief Alignment" item after line 913 and STRENGTHENS (with a stated structural caveat) the consensus thesis by supplying a proof-backed dynamical-systems analogue. A one-clause cross-reference follows `eq:meta_agent_beliefs` (line 2308), and a one-sentence cross-reference follows the Quantitative Clustering Evidence paragraph (line 2248). Requires adding `geshkovski2023mathematical` to references.bib.

```latex
% Insertion (i): after item (2) Belief Alignment, following line 913
This consensus reading has a rigorous dynamical-systems analogue. \citet{geshkovski2023mathematical} model a transformer as an interacting particle system in which each token is a particle on the sphere and the residual layer stack is the Euler discretization of a mean-field flow whose particles attract one another through a softmax kernel of inverse temperature fixed by the query-key scaling. They prove that this dynamics clusters the particles and, in the long-time limit, collapses them onto a small number of consensus points. The structural correspondence to the belief-coupling term is direct: minimizing $\sum_{ij} \beta_{ij} D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij} q_j)$ is a consensus dynamics on the statistical manifold of Gaussian beliefs, with $\beta_{ij}$ playing the role of the softmax interaction weight and the inverse temperature $\tau^{-1} = (\kappa \sqrt{K})^{-1}$ (Eq.~\eqref{eq:per_head_temperature}) playing the role of their inverse-temperature control over how sharply and how fast beliefs are pulled together. The correspondence is structural rather than literal, since the two flows coarse-grain over different variables: their mean-field flow transports token positions on the sphere $(\mathbb{S}^{d-1})^n$ under network depth, whereas the present coupling acts on the discrete coupling-space of beliefs on the statistical manifold and the depth axis is supplied here by iterative variational minimization rather than a stack of layers. Within that mapping, their single-cluster collapse is the degenerate consensus that the attention-entropy regularizer $\tau \beta_{ij} \log(\beta_{ij}/\pi_{ij})$ is introduced to forestall, and their long-lived metastable multi-cluster configurations correspond to the structured intermediate regime we report empirically in Section~\ref{sec:emergent_structure}.

% Insertion (ii): one clause after Eq. eq:meta_agent_beliefs (line 2308)
This grouping of tokens into meta-agents is the discrete counterpart of the emergent consensus clusters (the ``leaders'') that \citet{geshkovski2023mathematical} prove form under the mean-field attention flow; coarse-graining over the coupling-space groups the same population of mutually attracting agents that their continuum analysis collapses toward consensus.

% Insertion (iii): one sentence after the Quantitative Clustering Evidence paragraph (line 2248)
The progressive emergence of category-aligned structure, strengthening monotonically rather than collapsing to a single point, is consistent with the metastable multi-cluster regime that \citet{geshkovski2023mathematical} characterize for softmax attention at intermediate inverse temperature, in which several coherent groups persist as long-lived configurations before any global collapse.
```

Caveats: the variable mismatch (token positions on `(S^{d-1})^n` versus Gaussian beliefs on a statistical manifold) is real and load-bearing, so the draft frames a structural analogy, never an identity. Their clustering is a long-depth limit, but the headline runs are single-layer; the draft maps their depth axis onto iterative VFE minimization, an asserted correspondence, not a proven one. The inverse-temperature direction was verified by hand from `eq:per_head_temperature` (`tau` in the denominator, smaller `tau` = faster consensus), but the exact rate constant could not be quoted from the full Geshkovski PDF; confirm against their clustering theorem before submission. ### Connection 3: RG precedent bracket — Mehta–Schwab and Bény–Osborne

A single insertion at the start of the "Scope and status" paragraph in GL(K)_attention.tex, line 2338 (immediately after `\paragraph{Scope and status.}`, before the existing scaling-dimension text). It STRENGTHENS the framing of `thm:rg_conditional`/`conj:rg_universality` by bracketing the conjectural CLT-contraction account between an exact discrete construction and a rigorous continuous information-geometric one, and it COMPLICATES the technical setup by naming the Fisher–Bures metric as the operationally correct yardstick — which surfaces a genuine derivation gap in the `g2` exponent (see caveats). Requires confirming `mehta2014exact` and `beny2015information` exist in the .bib.

```latex
Two established constructions bracket this conjectural contraction account and should be read as precedent rather than as competitors to displace. At the exact, discrete end, \citet{mehta2014exact} establish a term-for-term mapping between Kadanoff variational renormalization and stacked restricted Boltzmann machines, in which each coarse-graining step is literally one layer of the architecture; this legitimizes reading a hierarchical belief-coarsening map as a genuine RG flow rather than as a metaphor, and it is the standard our $R_n$ aspires to when it claims to act on the variational free energy functional itself rather than on an ad hoc pooling. At the continuous, metric end, \citet{beny2015information} recast RG as a monotone flow on a statistical manifold equipped with the Fisher--Bures information metric: coarse-graining is a completely positive map, contraction of distinguishability under it is the data-processing inequality, and the relevant or irrelevant taxonomy is recovered as the metric magnitude of a tangent perturbation rather than as scaling-dimension bookkeeping. Our Proposition~\ref{thm:rg_conditional} sits between these two precedents, sharing the exact-coarse-graining ambition of the former and the information-geometric reading of irreversibility of the latter, while remaining weaker than both in that its contraction is conditional on the i.i.d.\ premise rather than guaranteed by an exact identity or by monotonicity. The bracketing also dictates the metric in which the couplings of Eqs.~\eqref{eq:g1_def}--\eqref{eq:g3_def} ought ultimately to be measured. The norms $\|\cdot\|$ written there are flat operator norms on the parameter coordinates; the operationally correct yardstick, following \citet{beny2015information}, is the Fisher--Bures distance on the belief manifold, under which $g_1,g_2,g_3$ become genuine tangent magnitudes and the relevant-versus-irrelevant verdict is a statement about distinguishability that survives coarse-graining rather than about a coordinate-dependent matrix norm. Measuring the couplings in the Fisher--Bures metric (the same metric that grounds the natural-gradient dynamics used elsewhere in this work) is the natural route to reconciling the scaling exponents of Eq.~\eqref{eq:rg_scaling}, since it distinguishes the first-order anisotropy deviation $g_1$, which averages as $n^{-1/2}$, from the genuinely second-order holonomy $g_3$, whose leading contribution under the cocycle is the commutator term and which therefore averages as $n^{-1}$; we leave the metric-level formulation, and with it a principled treatment of $g_2$, to the companion development.
```

Caveats and the embedded derivation gap: `eq:g2_def` defines `g2 = ||Omega_ij - Omega||/||Omega||` as a first-order deviation structurally identical to `g1`, so the same CLT averaging that gives `y1 = -1/2` should give `y2 = -1/2`, yet `eq:rg_scaling` (line 2312) and `thm:rg_conditional`(ii) (line 2322) assert `y2 = -1`; the `n^{-1}` rate only follows if `g2` is silently re-read as a second moment. The "textbook CLT" sentence (line 2327) is therefore not quite right, and the author should reconcile lines 2299/2312/2322. The draft deliberately does not endorse `y2 = -1` — it defers "a principled treatment of `g2`" — so it inserts no false claim. The `g3` side is correct and SymPy-verified: under the cocycle `A+B+C=0` the order-`eps` term of `H-I` vanishes and `H-I = [A,B]/2 + O(eps^3)`, so `g3` is genuinely quadratic and `y3 = -1` is consistent with `y1 = -1/2`. The Fisher–Bures transplant from a quantum (CPTP) construction onto a classical Gaussian-belief manifold is interpretive (defensible because Bures reduces to classical Fisher on commuting families), and the draft hedges accordingly. Confirm `mehta2014exact` and `beny2015information` are in the .bib before insertion.

## 3. Net Assessment

The deeper, derivation-level pass does not overturn the first pass's verdict: the core mathematics survives. Every symbolically checkable step across all seven proofs was re-derived and confirmed, and no sign, transpose, or index error was found anywhere. The two issues initially rated major — both in the three-limit reduction, concerning concentration-based key-bias cancellation and the cross-route `sigma`-bookkeeping — were confirmed as real but adjudicated down to minor on adversarial re-derivation: the first is an expository asymmetry between an exact mechanism (layer norm) and an approximate one (concentration) that the manuscript already flags as approximate, and the second is a localized `sigma`-power double-count inside a single per-head-temperature equation that does not break either reduction's logic. No confirmed derivation issue rises above minor. The substantive non-bibliographic items that remain are all surgical: define the `Cov_beta` self-covariance operator (Supp. App. B line 388), strip the redundant `(1/2 sigma^2)` from `eq:per_head_temperature` (line 1792), and write out the omitted mean-term covariance derivative (Supp. App. C lines 515–518). Two confirmed issues are not derivation errors at all but documentation gaps the author should still close: the SPD-retraction constants quoted in the supplement do not match the released code (line 656, already flagged by the author's own TODO), and Appendix H re-derives the canonical KL-uniqueness result without citing Chentsov/Petz/Amari/Jiao. The one finding that touches a stated theorem is the `g2` scaling exponent in the RG section (`y2 = -1` versus the `n^{-1/2}` its linear-norm definition implies); it sits in a section the manuscript already marks conditional and conjectural, and the connection draft routes around it rather than endorsing it. The first pass's summary holds and is sharpened: the core derivations are sound, the principal residual weaknesses are bibliographic and documentary, and the remaining mathematical fixes are clarifications rather than corrections.
