## Overall assessment

The paper contains a defensible and potentially substantial contribution: attention over transported Gaussian token beliefs derived from an entropy-regularized mixture-source free energy, with an exact ambient invariance result for full Gaussian beliefs and an explicit route toward dot-product attention. The Gaussian KL invariance calculation, the row-wise KKT derivation of Gibbs attention, the full-covariance Gaussian Fisher natural-gradient formulas for `(mu, Sigma)`, and the reproduced fixed-`GL(10)` width curve are sound in the scopes stated below. This does not certify the projected diagonal implementation or the gauge-frame preconditioner.

The manuscript is not ready for acceptance. Four central claims fail as written, with two additional major mathematical defects in the gauge-frame metric and conditional uniqueness argument. A nonidentity constant transport is not an exact specialization of the paper's Regime-I vertex-frame transport. The renormalization-group proposition applies independent-edge counting to a vertex-generated cocycle whose edges are correlated and whose holonomy is identically trivial. Canonical stationarity does not imply stationarity of the entropy-suppressed surrogate. The ambient full-Gaussian `GL(K)` invariance theorem is then identified too freely with a diagonal-covariance, single-exponential frame parameterization that is not closed under the full group action.

The empirical situation is mixed rather than uniformly weak. The 36-run fixed-`GL(10)` width sweep is present and its tabulated means, standard deviations, and fitted coefficients reproduce. Most headline, baseline, mechanism-ablation, BERT, and qualitative claims lack their raw supporting artifacts in the current evidence roots and fetched repository. Those claims are unsupported in the current package, not thereby numerically disproved. The blanket statement that all exact configurations and seeds are distributed is false for the current repository state.

The broad novelty framing also needs revision. Prior work already derives attention as latent structural inference and already constructs gauge-equivariant self-attention with parallel transport. The manuscript's narrower novelty remains viable, but it must be stated as the transported-Gaussian, token-belief, local-frame construction rather than as the first variational or gauge account of attention.

## Major mathematical comments

### 1. Nonidentity constant transport is not a Regime-I specialization

The central reduction at main-paper lines 1162–1164 and 2027 assumes `Omega_ij = Omega` for all pairs and calls this an exact flat specialization of the vertex-frame model. Earlier, however, Regime I defines

\[
\Omega_{ij}=U_iU_j^{-1}.
\]

This definition enforces `Omega_ii = I` and the cocycle identity `Omega_ij Omega_jk = Omega_ik`. If every pair has the same transport `Omega`, the self-edge gives `Omega = I`. Even if self-edges are removed, any transitive triangle gives `Omega^2 = Omega`, and invertibility again gives `Omega = I`. The causal mask does not remove this contradiction because the paper uses all `j <= i`, including self-edges and transitive causal triangles.

An arbitrary invertible head-space bilinear factor therefore cannot be obtained as a nonidentity constant member of the stated Regime-I family. The identification

\[
W_QW_K^\top=\sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-\top},
\]

is an algebraic kernel correspondence outside the vertex-cocycle family unless the paper adds another mathematical object. A clean repair would introduce distinct query and key bundles with a shared inter-bundle isomorphism, or a separately transforming compatibility morphism after vertex transport. The minimal repair is to state that constant Regime-I transport forces the identity and to downgrade the arbitrary invertible head-space factor from an exact gauge specialization to a structural correspondence. This invalidates the nonidentity constant-transport route. It does not by itself invalidate the separate per-token query/key carving at main-paper lines 1166–1205, which remains a bias-qualified structural correspondence rather than the claimed constant-cocycle specialization. The abstract, the summary reduction, Table 1, and the conclusion all require the same correction.

### 2. The RG exponents use the wrong independence unit for Regime I

Main-paper lines 2375–2392 and supplementary lines 953–969 claim `y_2 = y_3 = -1` by treating the `n^2` inter-block edges as independent. In the realized Regime-I model they are not independent. Write

\[
U_i=\bar U(I+\epsilon X_i)+O(\epsilon^2).
\]

The resulting transport is

\[
\Omega_{ij}=\bar U[I+\epsilon(X_i-X_j)]\bar U^{-1}+O(\epsilon^2),
\]

and hence

\[
\Omega_{AB}=I+\epsilon(\bar X_A-\bar X_B)+O(\epsilon^2).
\]

There are at most `2n` vertex perturbations, with a common mode canceling. For disjoint blocks under centered, independent, finite-variance vertex perturbations, the root-mean-square or typical norm of their block-mean difference fluctuates as `n^{-1/2}`. The corresponding Regime-I exponent is therefore `y_2 = -1/2`, not `-1`. More strongly, the exact cocycle gives

\[
\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{jk}\Omega_{ki}=I,
\]

so `g_3` is identically zero in Regime I. Holonomy is not an independent Regime-I coupling, and its scaling exponent is undefined or vacuous.

The reported `-1` exponents can be retained for an explicitly enlarged independent-edge ensemble, which may be viewed as an idealized Regime-II theory space. The manuscript's data-dependent Regime-II bilinear parameterization shares vertex variables and weights, so it does not itself establish independent edges. The proposition should either give separate Regime-I and independent-edge results or be scoped solely to that enlarged ensemble. The synthetic independent-edge experiment does not validate the trained Regime-I model, and the statement that edge independence holds by construction must be removed.

### 3. Canonical joint stationarity does not imply surrogate stationarity

The covariance-gap identity at main-paper lines 887–891 is correct when the same prior and observation terms are included on both sides. Define

\[
J_{\mathrm{surr}}=P+\langle E\rangle_{\beta^*},
\qquad
J_{\mathrm{can}}=P-\tau\log Z.
\]

Then

\[
\nabla_xJ_{\mathrm{surr}}-\nabla_xJ_{\mathrm{can}}
=-\tau^{-1}\operatorname{Cov}_{\beta^*}(E,\partial_xE).
\]

The next sentence, repeated at lines 1011–1017 and 2128, says the two vector fields coincide at joint stationarity. That implication is false. Take `tau = 1`, a uniform two-key prior, `E_1(x) = x`, `E_2(x) = 0`, and

\[
P(x)=\frac12(x-a)^2,
\qquad
a=\frac{2+e}{1+e}.
\]

At `x = 1`, `beta_1 = 1/(1+e)` and `P'(1) = -1/(1+e)`. The canonical derivative is zero and `beta = beta*` satisfies the row KKT equations, so this is a joint canonical stationary point. Direct symbolic differentiation gives the surrogate derivative

\[
-\frac{e}{(1+e)^2}\ne0.
\]

The manuscript should state the exact condition: the gradients coincide if and only if the displayed covariance gap vanishes. Equal active energies or equal active energy derivatives make the covariance vanish exactly. If the covariance remains bounded, the difference tends to zero as `tau -> infinity`. Canonical stationarity by itself is not sufficient.

### 4. The ambient invariance theorem is sound, but the realized family is not full-`GL(K)` equivariant

The full-Gaussian KL theorem at main-paper lines 520–574 is correct. It acts on unrestricted full covariances and unrestricted group-valued frames or edges. Two closures fail in the reported implementation.

First, `U = exp(phi)` does not parameterize all of `GL+(K)` and is not closed under arbitrary left `GL+(K)` action. For example, `diag(-1,-2)` has positive determinant but no real matrix logarithm. Thus the transformation `phi -> log(h exp(phi))` asserted at line 429 is not globally defined. This does not contradict the manuscript's isolated-edge result that every matrix in `GL+(K)` is a product of two real exponentials. The defect is that arbitrary left multiplication is not a globally defined action on each single-exponential frame coordinate, and no graphwise reparameterization theorem is supplied.

Second, diagonal Gaussian covariances are not closed under general congruence. With

\[
h=\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\0&1\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\Sigma=I,
\]

one obtains

\[
h\Sigma h^\top=\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&1\end{pmatrix},
\]

which is outside the diagonal family. The subgroup preserving the complete diagonal SPD family under congruence is the monomial group, consisting of permutation matrices times invertible diagonal matrices. This is a generic failure, not only the RoPE-specific covariance break already acknowledged in the limitations.

The paper should distinguish three levels throughout: the ambient full-Gaussian invariance theorem; exact group-valued vertex or edge transport; and the reported diagonal-covariance, single-exponential-frame approximation. The experiment uses `GL+`-valued transports but does not empirically validate an exactly full-`GL(K)`-equivariant model. Phrases such as “full gauge model” should become “non-isotropic token-frame Regime-I model under a diagonal-covariance approximation.”

### 5. The gauge-frame metric claims are internally inconsistent

Main-paper line 1697 uses

\[
-\tfrac12\operatorname{tr}(X\theta(Y))
=\tfrac12\operatorname{tr}(XY^\top),
\]

which is positive on the center. Supplementary lines 592–602 instead use the Cartan-modified Killing form

\[
2K\operatorname{tr}(X^\top Y)-2\operatorname{tr}(X)\operatorname{tr}(Y),
\]

which is degenerate on the center before its stated `epsilon I` regularization. These are not the same metric.

The supplementary claim that the position-dependent Frobenius pullback restores full `GL(K)` covariance is also false. The pullback formula is a valid extrinsic preconditioner through the exponential map, but the ambient Frobenius norm is not invariant under general left multiplication. For `h = diag(2,1)`, the squared Frobenius norm of `E_11` changes from `1` to `4` under `E_11 -> hE_11`. The pullback is positive definite only where the differential of the exponential has full rank; it is degenerate at the adjoint resonances already described in the supplement.

The authors must reconcile these formulas or explicitly distinguish a conceptual positive-definite completion from the implemented regularized Cartan-Killing preconditioner. A concrete left-invariant alternative is `g_U(xi, eta) = tr[(U^{-1}xi)^T(U^{-1}eta)]`; it is not bi-invariant, and using it globally also requires a group-element parameterization or atlas. If the current forms are retained for conditioning, call them Riemannian or Lie-algebraic preconditioners rather than full gauge-covariant natural gradients.

### 6. The forward-KL uniqueness proof needs a stronger range hypothesis; the broader closure claim needs a scale convention

The theorem at supplementary lines 1212–1306 may be repairable, but the current proof does not establish the stated generality. In lines 1284–1292, the constant `k` comes from a normalization multiplier and is configuration-dependent. The proof varies the prior and configuration to make the density ratio traverse an open interval, but it then treats `k` as fixed across those variations. Analyticity cannot repair that missing step.

There is a direct counterexample to that proof step. On a two-point space with reference density `(1/2, 1/2)`, normalization gives realized ratios `r_1 + r_2 = 2` as the prior varies. Let

\[
f(t)=t\log t-t+1+\frac{(t-1)^3}{3}.
\]

Then `f` is real-analytic and strictly convex on `(0, infinity)` because `f''(t) = (2t^2 - 2t + 1)/t > 0`, while `f'(t) - log t = (t-1)^2`. For every fixed prior, `(r_1-1)^2 = (r_2-1)^2 = k_p`, so the residual equation holds with a constant over the two sample points; as the prior varies, the union of realized ratios is the open interval `(0, 2)`, but `k_p` varies. An open interval obtained only by pooling configurations therefore does not imply that `f'(t) - log t` is globally constant.

Analyticity completes the argument if one fixed admissible configuration makes the ratio range over an open interval. It does not complete an argument in which the interval is obtained only by varying configurations whose normalization constant `k` may also vary. The richness assumption should therefore require one fixed admissible configuration or establish overlap conditions that force all configuration-specific constants to agree. The reported diagonal family does not automatically satisfy this richness condition because inverse transport of a diagonal Gaussian under a general `Omega` has full covariance. This is a gap in the written necessity proof, not a counterexample to the theorem after its hypothesis is strengthened.

The broader claim that Gibbs or exponential-family closure alone selects unit-scale forward KL also needs qualification. The family

\[
f_c(t)=c(t\log t-t+1),\qquad c>0,
\]

retains log-linear Gibbs form while changing the exponents to `1/(1+c)` on the prior and `c beta_j/(1+c)` on the neighbors. This is not a counterexample to the theorem's exact unit-scale half-exponent target, which already fixes `c = 1`. It shows that the surrounding closure-only intuition selects at most the KL ray `c D_KL`; if that broader claim is retained independently of the half-exponent target, a scale convention such as `f''(1) = 1` fixes `c = 1`.

### 9. The BERT validation is in-sample and its uncertainty model does not match the sampling structure

## Interpretation and literature comments

### 11. The central empirical inference establishes sufficiency, not the mechanism of standard transformers

Main-paper line 2329 argues that a functioning VFE language model supports the explanation that transformers succeed because they approximate distributed variational inference. The experiment demonstrates that the proposed variational architecture is sufficient to learn nontrivial language structure. It does not identify the internal mechanism of conventional transformers. That stronger claim requires discriminating predictions or interventions on standard transformers.

The same calibration applies elsewhere. The archived `GL(10)` versus `GL(15)` comparison changes the per-head gauge-block dimension, number of heads, parameter count, and seeds, so it does not isolate gauge-group size; the manuscript describes different configurations and implementation generations as well, but the current V3 package lacks the archived dictionaries needed to reconstruct those differences independently. The embedding-matched comparison uses 17.7 times as many parameters in the VFE model, so it cannot identify gauge transport as the causal source of the difference. A learned-versus-frozen comparison isolates the value of optimizing the frame table relative to freezing the otherwise identical table; it does not by itself distinguish gauge structure from a generic learned token-indexed transformation. One Kneser-Ney 5-gram does not establish the “strongest purely non-neural method,” and the attention-head and frame-cluster interpretations should remain exploratory without preregistered labels, null controls, held-out passages, multiple seeds, and multiplicity-aware inference.

A current fixed-`K = 48` block-size curve supplies emerging structural evidence outside the manuscript: mean PPL decreases monotonically from `124.5736` for `GL(3)` to `92.1504` for `GL(24)` across three seeds and 245,760,000 tokens per run. The sweep also changes parameter count from 19.37M to 70.16M, spans three dirty SHAs, and is absent from the manuscript. It supports a within-sweep block-size trend, not the archived `GL(10)`/`GL(15)` causal comparison or a scaling law. The ongoing `blocks_K48_2x` route should be allowed to finish before this evidence is promoted into a revised claim.

The defensible empirical conclusion is narrower: a non-isotropic token-frame VFE architecture under the reported approximations can realize nontrivial language modeling behavior, and the fixed-`GL(10)` development sweep improves monotonically with width under a fixed token budget.

### 12. The novelty and related-work sections omit direct precursors

The broad claim that attention is variational inference overlaps directly with Singh and Buckley's [Attention as Implicit Structural Inference](https://papers.nips.cc/paper_files/paper/2023/hash/4e8a74988bc611495c2d3a5edac8493f-Abstract-Conference.html) and its companion preprint, [Attention: Marginal Probability is All You Need?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.04556). This work represents soft attention as a posterior over graph structures, obtains softmax by normalizing log joint probabilities, uses Gaussian recognition densities, and derives iterative attention from variational-free-energy gradients. It does not contain transported Gaussian-belief KL energies, local `GL(K)` token frames, or the manuscript's invariance theorem, but it is a direct priority-relevant precursor and must be discussed.

The gauge-attention discussion cites CASK but omits the earlier [Gauge Equivariant Transformer](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper/2021/hash/e57c6b956a6521b28495f2886ca0977a-Abstract.html), which constructs local-gauge-equivariant self-attention using local coordinate rotations and geometric parallel transport on triangle meshes. GET does not use statistical `GL(K)` token beliefs or transported Gaussian KL. It does preclude priority for gauge-equivariant self-attention itself. The paper should distinguish global group equivariance, local geometric gauge equivariance, [parameter-space or head-wise gauge geometry](https://openreview.net/forum?id=sPCLRX1yOY), and its own token-belief `GL(K)` construction.

Main-paper line 63 says Sengupta and Friston stop short of an explicit transport operator. Their [Approximate Bayesian inference as a gauge theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/1705.06614) already provides explicit geometric parallel transport of sufficient statistics through the Levi-Civita connection and Schild's ladder. The valid distinction is that it does not select a closed-form, token-indexed `GL(K)` matrix representation such as `Omega_ij`, define transformer attention, or derive a dot-product reduction.

The residual-gradient-flow discussion should also engage with [Energy Transformer](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2023/hash/57a9b97477b67936298489e3c1417b0a-Abstract-Conference.html), which constructs attention computation as descent on an engineered global energy rather than a variational posterior or a gauge-transported Gaussian objective. The inference lineage should include [Structured Attention Networks](https://openreview.net/forum?id=HkE0Nvqlg), [Latent Alignment and Variational Attention](https://papers.nips.cc/paper_files/paper/2018/hash/b691334ccf10d4ab144d672f7783c8a3-Abstract.html), and the weaker stochastic-attention precedent [Variational Attention for Sequence-to-Sequence Models](https://aclanthology.org/C18-1142/). [Transformers as Unrolled Inference in Probabilistic Laplacian Eigenmaps](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v322/ravuri26a.html), published in PMLR in 2026 after a 2025 preprint, is a direct precedent for transformer blocks as unrolled variational inference. [Free Energy Mixer](https://openreview.net/forum?id=vjQnKToCnV), an ICLR 2026 poster, solves a Donsker–Varadhan variational free-energy problem in which query/key scores act as a prior and values as evidence; it is not Friston-style VFE and has no Gaussian gauge transport, but it precludes priority for the generic entropy-regularized free-energy/Gibbs construction. These sources narrow broad priority claims without containing the manuscript's token-belief `GL(K)` construction.

The revised novelty claim should be precise: the paper instantiates the standard entropy-regularized Gibbs variational principle with gauge-transported Gaussian KL energies between token-local beliefs, proves covariance of the full-Gaussian score under independent local frame changes, studies stated reductions toward dot-product attention, and implements an iterative diagonal-covariance language model without learned `W_Q/W_K/W_V`, MLPs, or pointwise activations while retaining a learned linear readout and, in the reproducible sweep, an optional head mixer. It should not claim priority for attention as latent or variational inference, energy-minimizing attention, gauge-equivariant self-attention, the generic free-energy/Gibbs construction, or bundle descriptions of standard-transformer parameter space. The current bibliography contains the Sengupta–Friston and CASK references but omits the direct precursors named above.

## Additional corrections

Supplementary lines 1128–1139 have the entropy sign wrong: with `S(q) = -E_q log q`, `KL(q || p) = <H>_q - S(q) + const`, not `+ S(q)`. Supplementary lines 761 and 765 should distinguish computational cost from random magnitude: a dot product costs `O(d_k)`, while an independent, centered, unit-variance dot product has standard deviation and typical magnitude `O(sqrt(d_k))`; `O(d_k)` magnitude applies to coherently aligned or noncentered components. At main-paper lines 46, 1272, 1339, and 2058 and supplementary lines 755 and 809, the LayerNorm discussion should say normalization makes constant-norm approximation plausible under stated conditions, not that it enforces the complete reduction. At main-paper lines 1157 and 2456, straight-through differentiation of a discrete sign is an estimator, not the gradient flow of the discrete model.

At supplementary line 982, any asymptotic language-model scaling law should include an irreducible cross-entropy floor, for example `L(D) = L_inf + A D^{-beta}` and `PPL(D) = exp(L(D))`; `PPL(D) ~ D^{-beta}` tends to zero and cannot be a literal asymptotic perplexity law. The `C* = O(K^2 V)` crossover at supplementary line 990 and main-paper line 2466 requires a derivation or should be labeled a heuristic. The update described as coordinate ascent at main-paper line 901 conflicts with the qualification at lines 2125–2128: a one-step row update is a natural-gradient step on a row-local modified objective, not exact coordinate ascent.

The paper should retain several existing qualifications. It correctly distinguishes the engineered entropy-regularized row functional from an inevitability theorem, identifies the BERT study mainly as a consistency check, discloses the single-seed headline result and parameter overhead, and admits the development-provenance status of the width sweep. Those qualifications should be propagated into the abstract, results, and conclusion rather than confined to later sections.

## Required revision standard

I would recommend reconsideration after a major revision that satisfies eight conditions. First, repair the standard-attention correspondence so the arbitrary head-space bilinear factor is not presented as a nonidentity constant Regime-I cocycle. Second, split the RG statement by Regime-I vertex and explicitly independent-edge ensembles; remove `g_3` from the Regime-I coupling space or state `g_3 = 0` and leave its exponent undefined. Third, correct every canonical-versus-surrogate stationarity claim and make the covariance-zero condition explicit. Fourth, separate the ambient invariance theorem from the closure and symmetry of the implemented diagonal, exponential-frame family, including the gauge-frame metric. Fifth, strengthen the forward-KL uniqueness hypothesis to provide an open ratio range within one fixed admissible configuration, prove compatibility across configuration-specific constants, and state the scale convention needed for unit-scale KL. Sixth, release a self-contained, immutable empirical and LaTeX artifact package and restrict all claims to evidence present in that package. Seventh, reanalyze the BERT study with held-out or nested temperature selection and dependence-aware uncertainty, while distributing revision-pinned inputs and analysis artifacts. Eighth, recast the novelty and mechanistic conclusions around the narrower transported-Gaussian contribution and the causal limits of the experiments.

The required changes are substantial but localizable. They do not require abandoning the full-Gaussian KL theorem, the Gibbs attention derivation, or the supported fixed-`GL(10)` width result. They do require rewriting the paper's claimed path from Regime-I transport to arbitrary dot-product attention, the RG section, the surrogate-stationarity discussion, the forward-KL necessity proof, the BERT validation protocol, the empirical evidence map, and the novelty statement.
