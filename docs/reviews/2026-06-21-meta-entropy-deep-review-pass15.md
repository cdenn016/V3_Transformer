## Verified sound (recomputed to machine precision; representative)

- **Row-reduced free energy and Kac extensivity** (§Extensivity): inserting the Boltzmann optimum gives `−τ log Z` exactly (residual ~2e-16); with the `1/N` kept inside the log each `−τ log Z_i ∈ [0,M]` so the total is O(N), while dropping it gives the O(N log N) superextensive total the manuscript explicitly warns against. The Kac/Curie-Weiss `1/N` normalization and the speed-N (not N²) Sanov LDP are correctly invoked.
- **Configurational meta-entropy and its large-deviation form** (§Meta-entropy): the meta-entropy is correctly the contraction of the Sanov rate (`s(f) = −inf{KL(ρ‖μ₁): F[ρ]=f}`), with the entropy = constant − rate-function sign convention matching Touchette; the negative-pressure `φ(β)` is concave and `s(f) = inf_β{βf − φ(β)}` (infimum form) recovers the rate exactly, with the manuscript correctly warning the supremum-form does not (the standard ensemble-equivalence subtlety).
- **Meta-temperature as natural-gradient noise** (§Meta-temperature): the Riemannian Langevin drift (with the metric-divergence correction term) makes `P ∝ exp(−F/T_meta)√det g` stationary — verified by an exact Fokker-Planck residual of zero; T_meta is a genuine fluctuation-dissipation object, correctly distinguished from the attention temperature τ = κ√d and the M-step learning rate.
- **Fisher-Rao counting measure** (§Microstate/counting): the Gaussian Fisher-Rao volume `√det g = c_K (det Σ)^{−(K+2)/2}` (mean block −1/2, SPD block −(K+1)/2) verified symbolically and numerically; the Chentsov scale ambiguity is correctly tracked as a configuration-independent `N log c` shift (only entropy differences are scale-invariant), and the noncompact GL⁺(K) gauge-volume divergence is correctly handled by a container regulator / Faddeev-Popov quotient.
- **Solvable Gaussian response** (§Gaussian response): the model is genuinely exactly solvable — the magnetization is an exact eigenvector of the response matrix with `χ = 1/α` at finite N (not a mean-field approximation), field-convention variants both verified; the α→0 ill-conditioning is benign (the divergent direction is the closed-form response, not an ill-conditioned solve).
- **Gauge sector** (§Gauge, §Holonomy): vertex-frame holonomy telescopes to the identity (residual ~3e-15), so the manuscript correctly claims no residual entropy in the flat regime and scopes the frustration conjecture to a distinct edge-local regime; gauge redundancy is handled by the correct continuous object (Faddeev-Popov orbit-volume quotient, not naive dof subtraction); and the subtle, correct point that frustration *gaps* the consensus soft mode (lowering, not raising, entropy) is verified spectrally, with residual entropy correctly conditioned on extensive ground-state degeneracy and carried as a conjecture.

## Findings applied this pass (five, all low-severity)

- **§three — model-channel entropy sibling named (variational + citation lenses).** The "three entropies" section (which exists precisely to prevent conflation) enumerated the belief entropy, the β-channel attention entropy, and the configurational meta-entropy, but did not mention that the model channel carries a structurally identical attention-distribution entropy. Added a clause noting the model-coupling (γ) terms of the functional carry that sibling entropy, scoped out here with the rest of the γ block. The "three" count is correct for the belief sector the note works in.
- **Wiener increment `dW^k → dB^k` (eq:rlangevin, notation lens).** The Brownian increment was written `dW^k`, overloading `W` = the microstate count (S = k log W). Renamed to the standard `dB^k`.
- **Incoming kernel `K(·,y) → \mathcal{K}(·,y)` (notation lens).** `K` is the belief-fiber dimension throughout; the incoming-attention kernel reused the glyph. Renamed to a calligraphic `\mathcal{K}`.
- **Display-equation punctuation (notation lens).** The attention-entropy bound `∈ [0, τ log N]` ended without terminating punctuation, unlike every other display in the manuscript; added the comma the continuing sentence takes.
- **`H(ρ*) → H_FR(ρ*)` (notation lens).** The differential (Fisher-Rao) entropy at the boundary-negligible rate was written with a bare `H`, which the manuscript otherwise uses for the discrete Shannon row entropy `H(β)`; subscripted to disambiguate.

## Items reported for the author (not applied)

- **Embedded bibliography vs the shared `references.bib` (MEDIUM, citation lens).** `meta_entropy.tex` uses a self-contained `\begin{thebibliography}` (31 entries), so it does not consult the program's shared `references.bib`. The one shared key string, `nielsen2020elementary`, points to *different* works in the two places (here: Nielsen, "An elementary introduction to information geometry," *Entropy* 22(10):1100, 2020; in `references.bib`: a Nielsen "Elementary differential geometry" book). The manuscript's embedded entry is correct, but if it is ever migrated to `\bibliography{references}` the key would silently resolve to the wrong work. Recommend renaming the key (e.g. `nielsen2020infogeom`) before any migration, and verifying the `references.bib` differential-geometry entry is the intended one. (Not applied: restructuring the bibliography is an author-workflow decision.)
- **Dembo-Zeitouni theorem decimals (LOW, stat-mech + citation lenses).** The Sanov (`Thm. 6.2.10`) and contraction-principle (`Thm. 4.2.1`) citations are correct at chapter granularity and the results are correctly invoked; the exact decimal numbers could not be confirmed from open sources. Author to confirm against the physical 2nd edition.
- **"Quenched" terminology (LOW, stat-mech lens) — adjudicated already-handled, not applied.** Canonical "quenched" denotes frozen *random* disorder with a disorder average; the manuscript borrows it for a fixed non-random slow sector. The text already disclaims this explicitly ("it is not a claim that the frozen slow sector is itself disordered ... no annealed or replica step is claimed"), so no edit was made.
