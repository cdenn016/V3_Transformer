## E-step fixed-point and phi-numerics implementation

Implemented the approved buildout without changing the default runtime regime. `e_step_update="frozen_surrogate_exact"` now aliases the historical `mm_exact` spelling while preserving the serialized user value. The implementation and warnings state the actual contract: one `mm_exact` iteration solves a frozen-attention, frozen-transport, frozen-coefficient surrogate, while the live fixed point solves the coupled equation `q = T(q)`. Consequently, `n_e_steps=5` performs five inner belief-refinement passes inside one forward graph, followed by one backward pass and one optimizer or M-step update. It does not perform five optimizer passes. Training with a larger or randomized inference depth means that those depths occur inside training forwards, so the decoder and outer parameters are optimized against the corresponding inferred-belief distribution instead of seeing the deeper state for the first time at evaluation.

Added a one-step-ahead fixed-point diagnostic that preserves the configured depth as the decoded operating point and evaluates one extra detached iteration only for measurement. Training and held-out records now include the configured final-step movement, `estep_fp_kl`, component RMS residuals, the undamped surrogate-target gap where defined, attention Jensen-Shannon drift, and active-alpha drift. Finalization writes current-weight depth sensitivity to JSON and PNG with the trained depth marked and an explicit warning that unmatched depths were not retrained.

Added one-based `epoch`, `batch_in_epoch`, exact `steps_per_epoch`, and continuous `corpus_pass` columns to every training row, plus corpus-pass boundary annotations on the loss trajectory. For the investigated WikiText-103 run, the 57,050-step loader length places repeated-corpus boundaries at 57,050, 114,100, and 171,150 optimizer steps. The sharp changes near 110,000 and 170,000 therefore align with the second and third reshuffled corpus exposures; they are not extra M-steps, E-step events, or learning-rate restarts.

Expanded phi observability with embedded chart-radius and clamp quantiles, active vertex conditioning, BCH approximation error and norm amplification, and separate absolute and relative fp32/fp64 inverse, cocycle, and holonomy closure references. Flat paths are now labeled as numerical closure or numerical flatness; curvature language remains for nonflat transports. The training hot path remains float32. Sampled report-time geometry is rebuilt and reduced in fp64, which diagnoses precision loss without doubling persistent activation and transport storage or pretending that fp64 repairs an overlarge BCH chart.

Added the opt-in `phi_mstep_max_matrix_norm` projected M-step. After a successful optimizer update, it chunk-projects every live token, positional, and independent model-frame phi table by embedded Frobenius norm while leaving optimizer moments intact. Disabled and skipped-step paths perform no projection. Projection-hit fraction and minimum scale are recorded.

Added exact positional composition through `pos_phi_compose="group_product"` on the validated phi, flat-transport, tied-model-frame route. The belief carries token coordinates `X` and positional coordinates `Y` separately, and transport builders assemble `U=exp(X)exp(Y)` with `U^{-1}=exp(-Y)exp(-X)`. The separate factor survives E-step inference, shared transport, model-channel coupling, snapshots, diagnostics, and reporting, with gradients reaching both token and positional tables. Reports include both factor radii and evaluate numerical closure on the actual two-factor vertex. Existing BCH and Euclidean paths are unchanged.

Registered three opt-in, three-seed ablation matrices without adding them to `SWEEP_ORDER`: `estep_depth_damping` has six fixed or randomized depth and damping arms; `phi_chart_control` has five mass, learning-rate, natural-gradient, and projection arms; `pos_phi_composition` compares BCH, exact group product, and positional-off. These definitions are runnable experiment plans only; no long-training result is claimed.

## Phi projection hot-path optimization design

Added `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-15-phi-projection-hot-path-optimization-design.md` after reproducing the projection slowdown from adjacent K20 runs. The bound-enabled run recorded 340.7574 milliseconds per step versus 83.5599 milliseconds with the bound disabled, while only 6 of 50,385 eligible rows were projected. The specification traces the regression to vocabulary-wide dense algebra-to-matrix embedding and repeated per-chunk host synchronization after every accepted optimizer step.

The approved design replaces dense embedding with an exact diagonal-Gram coordinate norm for explicitly certified Frobenius-orthogonal generator bases, retains a fail-closed exact fallback for uncertified bases, preserves full-table hard-bound semantics under AdamW momentum, and suppresses diagnostic host synchronization on silent steps. It defines dense-oracle equivalence tests and K20/K240 CUDA acceptance benchmarks. Implementation remains gated on review of the written specification.
