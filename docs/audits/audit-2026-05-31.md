# Deep Audit — 2026-05-31

## Scope

Executable Python under `vfe3/` and the test suite under `tests/`, audited against the GL(K)
attention manuscript (`Manuscripts-Theory/GL(K)_attention.tex`) and the CLAUDE.md hard
constraints. This audit verifies and fixes the six findings of the prior single-agent GL(K)
audit (`docs/audits/audit-2026-05-30.md`) and adds a fresh multi-agent deep audit (five parallel
domain investigators plus an independent source-citing verifier) to surface new issues. Findings
were judged from executable code, not comments or docstrings.

## Method

Five investigators (code quality + security; bugs + gradient-flow + theory correctness; dead
code + duplication; performance; type safety + contracts) ran in parallel, each given the
CLAUDE.md hard constraints and the six prior findings, and returned structured findings citing
`file.py:line`. A single independent verifier re-read every cited source location and ruled
CONFIRMED / REFUTED / INCONCLUSIVE, rejecting any claim whose only support was a comment. The
verifier returned 23 verdicts over 29 candidate findings.

All six findings of `audit-2026-05-30.md` were reproduced from source and fixed under TDD. The
guiding principle (CLAUDE.md audit instruction) is that a theoretically pure path must EXIST and
be reachable under appropriate toggles; defaults need not be pure.

### CONFIRMED but DEFERRED (documented, not changed)

The three always-on performance findings are real but were deliberately parked for a
GPU-verification branch in prior work (they need GPU benchmarking and a golden-equivalence gate
before a rewrite), and they do not affect correctness. They are recorded here, not implemented:

- The per-batch E-step loop in `model.forward` (`model.py:92`) serializes inference over the
  batch; batch-vectorizing the E-step kernels is the GPU-branch task.
- `compute_transport_operators` materializes a dense `(B,N,N,K,K)` Omega every iteration
  (`transport.py:88`); fusing the per-token factors into the `transport_mean`/`transport_covariance`
  contractions removes the `O(N^2 K^2)` allocation.
- `block_glk` runs a full `K x K` `matrix_exp` rather than per-block `d_head x d_head`
  (`transport.py:84`); the `omega_to_block_exp_pairs` utility already exists for this.

Cheaper, gated perf items also recorded for later: the Killing inverse metric is rebuilt
(`float64` eigh) every phi step under `phi_precond_mode='killing'` (cache as `inv_metric`); the
causal log-prior mask and the optional norm objects are reallocated per forward (cache as
buffers / build in `__init__`).

### REFUTED

- "The oracle's `.detach()` truncates the M-step gradient" (debugger, rated high) was downgraded
  to a non-bug: CLAUDE.md states backprop is allowed and the manuscript (Algorithm 1 discussion)
  specifies a first-order straight-through estimator in which belief gradients are computed from
  detached snapshots and the loss reaches the priors through the additive update chain. The
  unconditional detach is the intended design, not a defect.
- Three `python-pro` items (union return types on `e_step` / `VFEModel.forward`, missing
  annotations in `viz/figures.py`) were refuted as static-typing nits with correct runtime
  behavior, outside the "clear violations on public surfaces" bar.

## Theory note — attention temperature

The code keeps the global softmax temperature `tau = kappa * sqrt(K)`, as stated in the CLAUDE.md
mathematical reference. The GL(K) manuscript's standard-attention recovery and its empirical
temperature `tau ~ 2 sqrt(d_k)` are derived PER HEAD over the per-head dimension `d_head`, which
would suggest a per-head `tau_h = kappa * sqrt(d_head)`. The two conventions differ by a constant
`sqrt(n_heads)` factor in the softmax sharpness. CLAUDE.md is authoritative and overrides the
manuscript, so the global `sqrt(K)` convention is retained and applied per head; switching to a
per-head temperature is a one-line change at `VFE3Config.tau` if desired. This is flagged for the
user rather than changed silently.
